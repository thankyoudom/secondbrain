? Techniques to Improve "Wetness" and Fullness:

Before adding reverb, highpass at 250 Hz and lowpass around 6–8kHz.

? Correct Ways to Get Kaytra/Ivy Lab Width:
Source Selection First
Choose samples/synths that already have stereo width.
Wide pads, wide clap samples, stereo FX built into presets.
Manual Panning (Critical)
Hi-hats: Pan one 30% Left, another 30% Right.
Percs: Scatter around 15–50% L/R.
Pads: Slight detuning + panning left and right separately.
Synth Arps: Pan left; Textures: Pan right.
Layered Instruments Split L/R
Duplicate your pad/synth, pitch it +3 cents left and -3 cents right.
Pan left/right separately = instant lush width.
Mid/Side EQ (Subtle Secret Sauce)
Boost side highs (~8–12kHz) gently.
Cut a tiny bit of side lows (~200–400Hz) to avoid muddy stereo lows.
Send FX Busses
Instead of widening each track, send reverb/delay to a stereo bus.
That FX return channel becomes a "wide bed" under your dry sounds.
Slight Auto-Panning / Movement
Fruity Love Philter (or Pancake VST) = automate gentle slow left-right drift.
Subtle: no seasick beats lol.
