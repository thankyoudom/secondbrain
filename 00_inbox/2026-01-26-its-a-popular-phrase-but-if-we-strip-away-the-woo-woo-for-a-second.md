# It’s a popular phrase, but if we strip away the "woo-woo" for a second…

It’s a popular phrase, but if we strip away the "woo-woo" for a second, "changing your vibration" is essentially the practice of shifting your physiological and mental state from reactive/stressed to intentional/expansive.
When you’re in a "high frequency," you’re usually operating out of the parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest) rather than the sympathetic (fight or flight).
Here is a blueprint for daily habits that actually move the needle.
1. The Morning Calibration
Your first hour sets the "tuning fork" for the rest of the day.
- The "No-Phone" Buffer: Checking your phone immediately triggers a reactive state, forcing your brain into a stress response before you’ve even brushed your teeth. Give yourself 20–30 minutes of phone-free time.
- Hydration + Light: Drink 16oz of water and get natural sunlight in your eyes. This regulates your cortisol rhythm and signals to your brain that it’s time to be alert and "high-energy."
- The Gratitude Circuit: Don't just list things; feel them. The goal is to trigger the release of dopamine and oxytocin. Pick one thing and visualize why it matters until you feel a physical shift in your chest.
2. Managing Your "Information Diet"
If you’re consuming high-conflict news, doom-scrolling, or hanging out with "energy vampires," your frequency will naturally plummet to match that environment.
- Curate Your Feed: Unfollow accounts that make you feel inadequate or angry.
- The "Complaining Fast": Try to go a few hours without voicing a complaint. Complaining reinforces a victim mindset (low frequency), while problem-solving reinforces an agency mindset (high frequency).
3. Physical Realignment
Frequency isn't just in your head; it’s in your body.
- Breathwork: When you feel a dip in energy, use the 4-7-8 technique. Inhale for 4, hold for 7, exhale for 8. It’s a literal "reset button" for your nervous system.
- High-Vibe Nutrition: Focus on "alive" foods—plants, water, and whole foods. Heavy, processed sugars often lead to a "vibrational crash" (aka a sugar crash) that makes it impossible to stay mentally sharp.
- Movement: You don’t need a grueling workout. A 10-minute walk or a quick dance session moves stagnant energy and clears mental fog.
4. The Evening Wind-Down
How you end your day determines the frequency you wake up with.
- Brain Dump: Write down everything on your mind. This offloads the "RAM" of your brain so you aren't processing stress in your sleep.
- Micro-Wins: Acknowledge three things you did well today. This trains your Reticular Activating System (RAS) to look for wins tomorrow instead of looking for problems.
Comparison: Low Frequency vs. High Frequency Habits
| Category | Low Frequency (Reactive) | High Frequency (Proactive) |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | Hitting snooze, checking emails | Sunlight, hydration, intention |
| Speech | Gossip, complaining, "I have to" | Encouragement, "I get to" |
| Focus | Past regrets / Future anxieties | Present moment / Opportunities |
| Body | Shallow breathing, sedentary | Deep breathing, intentional movement |
True frequency work is just the compounding interest of small, healthy choices.
