# North Star

Great question — let’s ground this in real, observed outcomes from creators similar to you: visual artists and beatmakers who post on TikTok. Getting clarity on your “North Star” is all about setting milestones that feel meaningful and achievable based on what’s real.

### ? YOUR CURRENT PATH
You’re an artist drawing portraits and posting your own beats — this hybrid approach has a real advantage on TikTok because it taps both:
Visual stimulation (art time-lapses, transitions, reveals)
Audio appeal (beats for storytelling, mood, and vibe)
Let’s walk through what can realistically happen at each growth stage:

### ? 1,000 FOLLOWERS
This is your first core milestone, and it's important because it unlocks creator tools and real traction.
### ? What Actually Happens
You unlock TikTok Creator Tools: including detailed analytics, video performance breakdowns, and the ability to test formats.
Your reach improves: TikTok trusts you more and might boost your content further if you have consistent engagement (not just followers).
People start recognizing your niche: Some viewers will follow not just because they like one video, but because they understand your vibe (e.g., "oh, they draw dope portraits and make fire beats").
Small brand opportunities: Micro art pages or small beat pages might DM you for collabs or reposts. It’s early, but a signal.
? Benchmarks to aim for at 1K:
5–10 videos with 1,000+ views
2–3 pieces of content with solid retention (people watched 75%+)
Comments like “how much for a portrait?” or “can I use this beat?”

### ? 5,000 FOLLOWERS
This is where creators often start converting audience into income or impact — not full-time yet, but early proof of concept.
### ? What Actually Happens
DMs turn into actual sales: People will ask about commissions, beat purchases, or usage rights.
You can go “mini-viral” more often: Instead of one-off 10k views, you might have 3-5 videos over 50k.
Cross-platform interest: Some of your TikTok fans will follow you on IG or Spotify or subscribe to a newsletter if you offer it.
You start developing a recognizable brand: A color palette, sound aesthetic, editing style. People recognize you on the FYP.
First collabs: Other mid-size creators (5K–20K range) might reach out for beat swaps, duets, portrait trades, etc.
### ? Monetization examples at 5K
$25–100 per portrait (commission-based or digital delivery)
$30–200 per beat (lease or exclusive — even just audio for other creators)
Tip jar or Ko-Fi donations
Sound usage credit: “Use this beat in your video if you tag me”
Small merch drops: Stickers, mini-prints, beat packs
