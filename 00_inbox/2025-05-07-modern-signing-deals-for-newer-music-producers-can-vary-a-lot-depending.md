# Modern signing deals for newer music producers can vary a lot depending…

Modern signing deals for newer music producers can vary a lot depending on the label, the producer’s leverage, and whether it’s a publishing, production, or label deal. Below are some realistic examples of modern deals and typical terms:

1. Publishing Deal (e.g., with Sony/ATV, Kobalt, or BeatStars Publishing)
Example: A producer signs a co-publishing deal with Kobalt after producing a breakout song that charts on Spotify.
### Typical Terms
Advance: $10,000–$50,000 for new producers with traction.
Ownership split: Usually 50/50 between producer and publisher (co-pub).
Term: 1–3 years with options.
Delivery: Required to deliver a certain number of songs per year.
Sync Opportunities: Publisher may pitch songs for film/TV placements.
Recoupment: Advance is recouped from publishing income (like streaming, radio, sync).

2. Label Production Deal (e.g., with a boutique label or producer collective)
Example: A new producer signs to an independent label or collective like Soulection or 88rising as part of their in-house talent.
### Typical Terms
Advance: Can range from none to $5,000–$20,000.
Royalties: 3–5% producer royalty on master sales/streams.
Backend: Points on songs they produce (usually 1–3 points out of the artist’s 10–15%).
Exclusive/Non-Exclusive: Often non-exclusive with a right of first refusal.
Term: 1 year or project-based.

3. Management/Development Deal (e.g., with a manager or production company)
Example: A new producer signs to a manager who shops their beats and gets them placements.
### Typical Terms
Commission: Manager takes 15–20% of gross income.
Term: 1–3 years with performance clauses.
Deliverables: Sometimes required to deliver a certain number of beats/month.
Expenses: Manager may recoup expenses spent on development (studio time, travel).

4. Beat Licensing Platform Deal (e.g., BeatStars or Tracklib exclusives)
Example: A producer gets signed to a curated or premium section of a platform like BeatStars.
### Typical Terms
Revenue Split: 70–80% to the producer, 20–30% to the platform.
Exclusive Rights Sales: Handled via platform, with set pricing.
Non-exclusive Licenses: You keep control but agree to their terms.
Royalties: You’re responsible for registering with PROs or a publisher to collect.

What to Watch Out For
Long-term exclusive deals (can limit your freedom).
Unclear recoupment terms (always ask how advances are paid back).
No audit rights (you should be able to see how money is made).
Lack of ownership (try to retain some publishing/master share).
