# With a utilization rate now at 15% ($1,000 of $6,500) and a steady…

With a utilization rate now at 15% ($1,000 of $6,500) and a steady income of $80,000–$100,000, the focus shifts from "crisis management" to "wealth building."
Here is a concise roadmap for a solid financial foundation:
1. Zero Out the Credit Card
Eliminate the final $1,000 immediately. With that income level, paying this off in one or two cycles saves on interest and pushes utilization into the "Excellent" zone (under 10%). From there, aim to pay the statement balance in full every month to avoid interest entirely.
2. Build the "Starter" Emergency Fund
Before aggressive investing, secure $2,000–$5,000 in a high-yield savings account (HYSA). This acts as a buffer against minor emergencies (car repairs, medical bills) so they don't end up back on the credit card.
3. Capture the Employer Match
If the employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute the exact amount needed to get the full "free money." This is essentially a 100% return on investment that shouldn't be ignored while finishing other goals.
4. Fully Fund the 3–6 Month Reserve
Once the credit card is zeroed, expand the emergency fund to cover 3–6 months of essential living expenses. At a $100k salary, this provides a massive psychological and financial safety net in case of job loss or major life changes.
5. High-Interest Debt vs. Retirement
- High-Interest Debt: Attack any other debts with interest rates above 7–8% (like personal loans or high-interest auto loans).
- Retirement: Once high-interest debt is gone, aim to invest 15% of gross income into retirement accounts (Roth IRA, 401k).
