# The concept of "thinking in frequency" sounds like something you’d…

The concept of "thinking in frequency" sounds like something you’d hear at a meditation retreat or in a sci-fi movie, but it’s actually a blend of physics, psychology, and a bit of "vibe" management.
At its core, it’s the idea that everything in the universe—from the chair you're sitting on to the thoughts in your head—is made of energy vibrating at a specific rate.
What Does "Frequency" Actually Mean?
In a literal sense, frequency is how often a wave repeats itself in a given amount of time. In a metaphorical or "lifestyle" sense, it refers to your emotional and mental state.
- Low Frequency: This feels heavy. It’s the "static" on the radio. It manifests as fear, resentment, chronic complaining, or feeling stuck. When you’re "low," you tend to attract more of the same—meaning you notice more problems than solutions.
- High Frequency: This feels light and effortless. It’s clarity, joy, gratitude, and flow. When you’re "high," you’re more resilient; obstacles look like puzzles rather than walls.
The Radio Metaphor: Think of yourself as a radio. If you want to hear jazz (joy/success) but you’re tuned to 90.1 (anger/stress), you aren't going to hear the music. To hear the music, you have to change your internal dial to match the broadcast you want.
How It Looks in Real Life
Thinking in frequency is about shifting from being a reactor to being a creator.
| Scenerio | Low Frequency Response | High Frequency Response |
|---|---|---|
| Traffic Jam | Cursing, gripping the wheel, ruined morning. | Listening to a great podcast, accepting the delay. |
| Constructive Criticism | Feeling attacked, getting defensive, brooding. | Seeing it as data to improve, staying curious. |
| New Project | "This is going to be so hard, I’ll probably fail." | "This is a challenge, but I'll figure it out." |
Daily Habits to "Raise Your Frequency"
You don’t need to move to a cave to do this. It’s about small, consistent shifts in how you handle your energy.
1. The "First Five" Rule
Your brain is most "tunable" right when you wake up. Instead of reaching for your phone (which immediately puts you in a reactive, low-frequency state of comparison or stress), spend five minutes on intentionality. * Try this: Identify one way you want to feel today before you even get out of bed.
2. Radical Gratitude
Gratitude is the "fast-forward" button to a higher frequency. It is physiologically impossible to be in a state of deep fear and deep gratitude at the same time.
- Daily Action: Write down three highly specific things you’re grateful for. Not just "my dog," but "the way my dog’s ears flop when he’s excited to see me."
3. Movement & Hydration
Energy that doesn't move becomes stagnant (low frequency).
- Daily Action: 10 minutes of stretching or a brisk walk. Also, drink more water—water is a conductor of energy. Dehydration is a literal frequency killer for your brain.
4. Media Diet
You are what you consume. If your social media feed is full of outrage, your "frequency" will match it.
- Daily Action: Audit your "digital environment." Unfollow accounts that make you feel "less than" or angry.
5. Conscious Breathing
When you’re stressed, your breath becomes shallow. This signals your nervous system to stay in "survival mode" (low frequency).
- Daily Action: Use the 4-7-8 technique: Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, exhale for 8. This resets your frequency by calming the amygdala.
> A grounded reminder: "Raising your frequency" doesn't mean you have to be happy 24/7. That's just "toxic positivity." Real high-frequency living is about processing the low moments quickly so you don't get stuck in them.

Since you're ready to go deeper, let’s look at the habits that bridge the gap between "mindset" and "physics." To keep your frequency high, you have to manage your energy leaks—the small things that drain your battery without you noticing.
Here are more advanced daily habits to fine-tune your "internal dial."
1. The "I Get To" Language Shift
Your words are literal vibrations. When you say "I have to go to the gym" or "I have to do these dishes," you’re operating in a frequency of obligation and heaviness.
- The Habit: Swap "I have to" for "I get to." * The Shift: "I get to move my body" or "I get to have food to clean up after." It sounds small, but it shifts you from a victim of your schedule to a benefactor of your life.
2. Close the "Open Loops"
Nothing lowers your frequency faster than mental "clutter." These are the tiny unfinished tasks (emails, unreturned texts, a lightbulb that needs changing) that sit in the back of your brain.
- The Habit: Every morning, identify one "open loop" and close it.
- The Shift: By completing a nagging task, you reclaim the energy that was being used to "remember" it. It creates a surge of dopamine and a "lighter" mental state.
3. Light Exposure (Biological Frequency)
Your frequency is tied to your biology. If your circadian rhythm is off, your energy will be "muddy."
- The Habit: Get 10 minutes of direct sunlight in your eyes (not through a window) within 30 minutes of waking up.
- The Shift: This triggers a precise release of cortisol to wake you up and sets a timer for melatonin later. You are literally syncing your internal frequency with the planet’s natural cycle.
4. Curated Soundscapes
If everything is frequency, then sound is the most direct way to change yours.
- The Habit: Use Solfeggio frequencies or Binaural beats while you work or sleep.
- 528 Hz: Known as the "Transformation" frequency.
- 432 Hz: Known for being "in tune" with nature.
- The Shift: Instead of silence or distracting talk-radio, these tones help entrain your brainwaves into a state of focus (Alpha) or deep relaxation (Theta).
5. The "Energy Audit" of People
We are "social oscillators"—we naturally mimic the frequency of those around us.
- The Habit: Notice how you feel after interacting with people. Use the "Radiator vs. Drain" test.
- Radiators leave you feeling inspired or calm.
- Drains leave you feeling exhausted or cynical.
- The Shift: You don't have to cut everyone off, but start limiting the time spent with "Drains" and intentionally increase time with "Radiators."
6. Digital Sunset
The blue light and "infinite scroll" of phones keep your brain in a high-frequency state of stress (Beta waves) when you should be transitioning to rest (Delta waves).
- The Habit: Implement a Digital Sunset 60 minutes before bed. No screens.
- The Shift: This allows your frequency to "downshift" naturally, leading to deeper sleep. High-quality sleep is the foundation of a high-frequency day.
Comparison: The Frequency of Environments
| Space | Lower Frequency | Higher Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Workspace | Cluttered, dark, stale air. | Minimalist, natural light, a plant. |
| Social Media | Comparison, news-doom, ads. | Learning, inspiration, connection. |
| Commute | Road rage, aggressive talk radio. | Audiobooks, silence, or singing. |
> The Secret Sauce: High frequency is often found in integrity. Doing what you said you were going to do creates an internal "resonance" that feels like confidence. When you lie to yourself or procrastinate, you create "dissonance" (static).
