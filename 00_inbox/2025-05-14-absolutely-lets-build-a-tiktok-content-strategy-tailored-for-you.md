# Absolutely, let's build a TikTok content strategy tailored for you…

Absolutely, let's build a TikTok content strategy tailored for you as an independent music producer. We'll break it down into sections:

? Section 1: Trending Music Genres as of May 13, 2025
As of yesterday, the top three trending music genres on TikTok are:
Electronic Music: Genres like techno and house have surged in popularity, with posts tagged #ElectronicMusic garnering over 13 billion views globally. This genre is especially popular in content related to sports, fitness, fashion, travel, and holidays. (The Guardian)
Afrobeats: Afrobeats continues to dominate, with artists like Rema and Tyla leading the charge. Songs like Rema's "Charm" and Tyla's "Jump" are trending, reflecting the genre's global appeal. (Wikipedia)
Hyperpop: This genre is gaining traction with its experimental sounds and visuals. KATSEYE's "Gnarly" exemplifies this trend, featuring chaotic energy and meme-worthy visuals that resonate with Gen Z audiences. (Teen Vogue)
Hashtags: #ElectronicMusic #Afrobeats #Hyperpop

? Section 2: General Music Trends, Topics, and Artists
### Current music trends on TikTok include
Dance Challenges: Songs that inspire dance routines are going viral. For instance, Tyla's "Water" sparked a dance challenge involving hip movements and pouring water down one's back. (Wikipedia)
Emotional Storytelling: Tracks like "Girls Like Me Don’t Cry" by thuy are used for storytelling, allowing users to express emotions and personal narratives. (Predictive Marketing)
Transformation Videos: Songs like Erica Banks' "Buss It" are popular for transformation videos, where users showcase dramatic outfit changes. (Predictive Marketing)
Hashtags: #DanceChallenge #Storytelling #Transformation

? Section 3: Trends in Afrobeats and Afrofuturism as of May 13, 2025
### In the Afrobeats and Afrofuturism scene
Fusion Genres: Artists are blending Afrobeats with other genres. Tyla's "Jump" combines Afrobeats, dancehall, and hip hop, featuring amapiano percussion and Zulu chants. (Wikipedia)
Cultural Representation: Music videos are showcasing authentic representations of African culture. The "Jump" video, for example, was filmed in Johannesburg's Hillbrow and Soweto areas. (Wikipedia)
Emerging Artists: New talents like OliveTheBoy from Ghana are gaining attention by blending Afrobeats with personal styles, contributing to the genre's evolution. (El País)
Hashtags: #AfrobeatsFusion #Afrofuturism #EmergingArtists

? Section 4: Content Ideas for Aspiring Music Producers on TikTok Today
As an aspiring music producer, consider the following content ideas:
Behind-the-Scenes: Share clips of your music production process, showcasing how you create beats or mix tracks.
Tutorials: Create short tutorials on producing specific sounds or using certain software, providing value to your audience.
Collaborations: Partner with dancers or vocalists to create content that combines your beats with their performances.
Trend Participation: Engage with current TikTok trends by incorporating your music into popular challenges or memes.
Hashtags: #MusicProducer #BehindTheScenes #MusicTutorial
