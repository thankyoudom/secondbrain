# job stuff

You're not alone—many computer science grads are feeling the same pressure, especially in today’s U.S. job market, where competition is fierce and tech layoffs have cooled off entry-level hiring.
Here’s a breakdown of career paths and degrees that are currently in higher demand or offer more security than a general CS degree right now (as of mid-2025):

? High-Demand Career Paths (With or Without a CS Degree)
1. Cybersecurity
Why: Constantly rising cyber threats, huge talent gap.
Path: Certs like CompTIA Security+, CEH, or a master's in cybersecurity.
Jobs: SOC Analyst, Cybersecurity Engineer, Penetration Tester.
2. Cloud Engineering & DevOps
Why: Companies are modernizing infrastructure.
Path: Learn AWS, Azure, GCP; consider certifications (AWS Solutions Architect, etc.).
Jobs: DevOps Engineer, Cloud Engineer, Site Reliability Engineer.
3. AI / Data Science (Niche)
Why: AI is booming, but only top-tier candidates land jobs.
Path: Upskill in ML, deep learning, or NLP via bootcamps or online master’s programs.
Jobs: ML Engineer, Data Scientist, Prompt Engineer (rare, but hot right now).
4. Healthcare Tech & Informatics
Why: Aging population + digital health = job security.
Path: Transition into health informatics, bioinformatics, or get a master’s in health tech.
Jobs: Health IT Specialist, Clinical Data Analyst, Informatics Engineer.
5. Skilled Trades + Tech (High ROI)
Why: Skilled labor shortage and tech-integration.
Path: Learn a trade (electrical, HVAC, etc.), add automation/IoT skills.
Jobs: Industrial Automation Tech, Mechatronics Engineer.

? Degrees with Better ROI in Today’s Market
? 1. Nursing / Healthcare Professions
RN, BSN, Physician Assistant, OT/PT
High job security, essential services.
? 2. Accounting / Finance (with certifications)
CPA, CFA, or even just solid bookkeeping.
Still in high demand despite AI automation.
? 3. Engineering (Electrical, Civil, Mechanical)
Especially infrastructure-focused roles (bridges, energy, smart grids).
Long-term stable demand due to federal funding.
?? 4. Legal / Compliance / Cyber Law
A tech background + a JD or compliance degree is gold in fintech and privacy.
Data compliance (GDPR/CCPA) is hot.
? 5. Psychology + Tech (UX, Human Factors)
Combining user empathy with tech is a rising need.
UX Design, Human-Centered AI, etc.

? Alternative Routes (Faster + Cheaper Than a 4-Year Degree)
Tech Bootcamps (e.g., cybersecurity, data analytics, software testing)
Trade Schools (electrician, CNC machining, diesel mechanics with automation)
Certifications (AWS, Cisco, Google IT, Salesforce, etc.)
Apprenticeships (especially through unions or workforce development programs)

? Real Talk
If your CS degree hasn’t led to a job yet, ask yourself:
Do I have portfolio projects or internships?
Am I applying too narrowly (e.g., just software dev)?
Could a 3–6 month cert or bootcamp refocus my trajectory?
Sometimes it's not the degree but the focus and packaging of your skills that makes the difference.
