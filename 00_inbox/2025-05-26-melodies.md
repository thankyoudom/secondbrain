# Melodies

Got it. If your melodies sound dry and lifeless, it’s likely an issue of harmonic content, stereo width, and lack of movement. Let's fix that step by step, and I’ll give you a complete plan using free tools, including frequency ranges, panning, plugin chains, and melody creation tips.

STEP 1: Melody Composition Techniques (Fix the Source First)
### A. Add harmonic movement
Use chord tones and passing tones: Write melodies that move within the scale but land on chords from the progression.
Layer in 3rds or 6ths: Harmonize your melodies. Write a second melody line that moves in parallel 3rds or 6thsabove or below.
Counter-melodies: Add a second melodic voice that’s rhythmically or melodically different but still in key.

STEP 3: Free Plugin Effects Chain for Lush Melodies
Here’s a chain you can build in FL Studio, using free plugins only:
1. Saturation / Harmonics
Adds warmth, subtle distortion = more harmonics.
Free Plugin: Softube Saturation Knob or IVGI by Klanghelm
Alt: Try FL’s built-in Fruity Blood Overdrive with gain turned low.
### Set it up
Add subtle drive. Use it on mono instruments like a sine wave to thicken.
Use in parallel if it’s too harsh.

2. Chorus + Flanger (Better Alternatives)
You said FL Chorus and Flangus aren't enough. Try:
Free Plugin: Magic Switch (by Baby Audio, free chorus)
Free Plugin: Blue Cat’s Chorus or TAL-Chorus-LX (Juno-style chorus)
These add stereo width, subtle detune, and warmth. Stack two with different depths/rates.

3. Delay (Adds Movement and Harmonics)
Free Plugin: Valhalla Supermassive (best free delay/reverb combo)
Use presets like “Gemini” or “Hydra” for spacey movement.
Use 1/8 or dotted 1/4 notes delay on melodic stabs or leads. Add light feedback.

4. Reverb (Adds Space and Glue)
Free Plugin: Dragonfly Reverb or OrilRiver
Use a high-pass filter before reverb to remove low-end mud (<250 Hz)
Set pre-delay to around 20-40ms to separate dry/wet signals

5. EQ (Tone Sculpting)
Free Plugin: TDR Nova
Add a gentle boost at 6k–8k Hz to bring out brightness
Cut harsh mids at 800Hz–1.5kHz if needed
Low shelf boost at 200Hz if too thin

STEP 4: Panning and Stereo Tips
Melody main layer: Keep centered or slightly off-center
Harmonic layer (3rd/6th above): Pan 20–40% left/right
Counter melody: Hard-pan opposite of your main secondary harmony
Reverb/delay return channels: Use a stereo imager to widen (e.g. Ozone Imager 2 – free)
Pro Tip: Use automation on pan or stereo width to add movement.

STEP 5: Layering for Harmonic Richness
### Use 3 layers minimum for melodies
Fundamental: Mono lead, simple waveform (sine, square)
Mid Harmonic Layer: Detuned saws/pulse waves, panned
Air/Sparkle Layer: High-passed pad or noise layer, stereo widened
### Example Plugin
Vital (Free Synth) – Use this for rich harmonic sound design
Layer 1: Sine + subtle distortion
Layer 2: 2 detuned saws, panned L/R
Layer 3: White noise or high saw, EQ’d to 6k–10k only

Final Tips
Modulation = life. Automate cutoff filters, LFOs on pitch or pan.
Use scale helpers in FL: Tools > Scale highlighting, choose key
Don’t overload low mids: too much at 200–400Hz gets muddy
Render harmonized melodies to audio, reverse or stretch slices for texture
