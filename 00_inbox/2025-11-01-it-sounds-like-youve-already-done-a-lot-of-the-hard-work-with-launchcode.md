# It sounds like you’ve already done a lot of the hard work with LaunchCode…

It sounds like you’ve already done a lot of the hard work with LaunchCode (LC) — great job on completing your coursework! I looked into the current status of the org and here’s a breakdown of what I discovered plus how you might proceed — with caveats.

? What I found that is clear
LaunchCode is headquartered in St. Louis, Missouri, at 4811 Delmar Blvd. (launchcode.org)
They explicitly say their core programs enrol learners who live in three “hub” metro areas: St. Louis (MO), Kansas City (MO), and Philadelphia (PA). (codecamps.launchcode.org)
They don’t currently note an open hub in South Florida (i.e., Boca Raton / Miami area) as part of their standard service area. From the FAQ: “At this time, we are only enrolling learners from the St. Louis, Kansas City and Philadelphia metro areas into our core programs …” (launchcode.org)
Their mission and work remain active: free tech training, paid apprenticeships, job-placement efforts. (launchcode.org)

?? What isn’t totally clear, and what that means for your situation
I could not find any definitive public statement saying “LaunchCode has closed operations in South Florida” though you saw locally they may have scaled back or shifted.
Because your circumstance is somewhat unique (you completed the coursework, but did not finish the capstone + placement steps), you’re in a kind of in-between zone. It’s unclear from the public docs if they allow “returning students” to pick up dropped-out elements after a long hiatus, especially if you’re outside their hub area.
Their FAQ says that living outside the hub area is generally a barrier:
“We are only enrolling learners from the St. Louis, Kansas City and Philadelphia metro areas…” (launchcode.org)
So being in South Florida may limit your eligibility for full program elements under their current model.

? What you can do (and steps I’d suggest)
Given your background and your openness to relocating (which is a big positive), here’s how you might approach next steps:
Reach out directly to LaunchCode
Email from their “About” page: the best way to contact them is via the address listed on that page. (codecamps.launchcode.org)
Phone: 314-254-0107 (St. Louis HQ). (Give STL Day)
### Ask specifically
“I completed the coursework in your earlier South Florida offering (or via your program) but did not complete the capstone/project + job placement steps. Am I eligible to resume/complete those steps now?”
“Even though I’m currently located in South Florida, am I eligible for the program given your hub-area policy? If I relocated to one of your hub cities, what are my options?”
“Are there any alumni-oriented or returning-student pathways that allow me to pick up where I left off?”
Evaluate relocation possibility
Since you said you’re open to relocating, you could consider moving (or commuting/remote-if-allowed) to one of the hub cities: St. Louis, Kansas City, or Philadelphia. That likely improves your eligibility significantly under LaunchCode’s current criteria. The key question: Would the “capstone + placement” piece require physical presence or local employer connections in that region? Ask when you contact them.
Document your past completion
When you contact them, it will help to have proof of your coursework completion (grades, certificates, transcripts, etc.). Explain the circumstances of why the capstone/placement weren’t done. That helps them evaluate whether you’re “in good standing” and eligible to resume.
Explore alternatives
While you contact LaunchCode, it would be wise to also research alternative programs (bootcamps or apprenticeships) that:
Accept learners in South Florida (or remote)
Accept learners who already have completed coursework and are just finishing capstone/placement
That way you have a plan B if LC cannot accommodate.
Check alumni/returning-student policies
Since LaunchCode has an “[Alumni Committee]” (mentioned in their FAQ) you might ask if the alumni network can help you advance or plug into job placement directly even without finishing the formal capstone via LC. (codecamps.launchcode.org)

? Summary of your chances
Given what you shared + what I found:
Good: You already completed most of the coursework; you’re motivated; you’re open to relocating.
Challenged: The program’s current policy limits standard enrollment/placement to hub areas and students who complete the full pipeline. Your prior enrolment + location outside their region complicates the “resume/capstone/placement” question.
Realistic next step: Contact LaunchCode and ask. You may be eligible (especially if you relocate), or they might offer a custom “alumni finish” solution. But you should approach expecting they may say you’ll need to finish via one of their hub city cohorts.
