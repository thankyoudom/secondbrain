# This is a fire goal and you’re asking all the right questions—getting…

This is a fire goal and you’re asking all the right questions—getting to that Ilan Bluestone level of polish, space, and emotion takes time, but it sounds like you’re ready to go deep. Let's break this down into a *layered game plan* (pun fully intended) that covers melody writing, layering, sound design, and mixing—specifically tuned for FL Studio and your style.

---

## **Core Goals for the Next Month (Melody & Layering Focus)**

### **1. Melody Mastery:**
Focus on **color**, **movement**, and **emotion**.
- **Daily Practice:**
- Write one melody per day using just piano or a basic sine/saw patch.
- Use MIDI pack references or transpose melodies from artists you love.
- **Weekly Challenge:**
- Pick a *mode or scale* (Lydian for uplifting, Dorian for chill, etc.) and write a 16-bar melody that evolves.
- **Color Theory Tip:**
- Use **passing tones** and **inversions** to bring motion.
- Layer **intervals** (3rds, 6ths, 7ths) subtly to bring color.

---

### **2. Layering Without Mud (The Ilan Blueprint):**

#### **Golden Rule: Sound Selection First.**
If the source is trash, no EQ can save it.

### **Layering Structure:**
Think of **melodic elements like an orchestra**:

| Role | Example Layer | Plugin/Sample Tip |
|------|---------------|--------------------|
| **Top Lead (Main Melody)** | Saw/Square Lead | Vital (Free), FLEX, or Tyrell N6 |
| **Harmonic Layer (same MIDI, diff timbre)** | Glassy pad, pluck or resampled vocal | Use LABS (Spitfire), Soundfonts |
| **Body Layer** | Wide detuned saw, chorused patch | Super-8 clone in Vital or any chorus’d saw |
| **Air/High Layer** | Noise layer, sparkle FX | Fruity PanOMatic w/ stereo spread |
| **Sub-melody/Counter** | Pluck or arp (lower octave) | Fruity Arp + any sample or patch |
| **Pad/Atmosphere** | Reverb-drenched chord wash | Fruity Convolver + Reverb tail sample |

---

### **3. Sound Design & Control Goals (Avoiding Mud & Peaks):**

#### **Clean Mix Tips:**
- **EQ Sculpting:**
- **Subtractive EQ** on each layer.
- High-pass every non-bass element (try 150–300 Hz).
- Fruity Parametric EQ 2 is your best friend—*visualize overlap*.

- **Saturation/Glue:**
- Use **Fruity Soft Clipper** gently before you mix to avoid harsh transients.
- Add **Fruity Waveshaper** or **CamelCrusher (Free)** for warm saturation.

- **Stereo Placement:**
- Keep **main leads** center, **pads/washes** wide.
- Use **Fruity Stereo Enhancer** or **PanOMatic**.

- **Automation Power:**
- Automate volume, filters (Fruity Filter), and reverb send to evolve layers.

---

### **4. Tools You Already Have (or Should Grab Free):**

#### **Plugins:**
- **Vital** – insane free wavetable synth, rival to Serum.
- **LABS by Spitfire Audio** – beautiful textures and pads.
- **Tyrell N6** – free analog-style synth.
- **TAL-Chorus-LX** – lush stereo chorus.
- **Dexed** – FM synth great for plucks and bells.

#### **FL Stock FX:**
- **Fruity Parametric EQ 2**
- **Fruity Reeverb 2 + Fruity Delay 3**
- **Maximus for gentle multiband compression**
- **Fruity Stereo Shaper**

---

### **5. Reference-Level Engineers / Producers to Study:**

#### **YouTube / Courses:**
- **Dan Worrall** – king of mixing clarity. Study his EQ, compression, stereo tricks.
- **In The Mix (FL Studio focused)** – layering, sound design, and mix tutorials.
- **EDMProd** – songwriting & arrangement tips.
- **Julius Dreisig** – melodic layering and arrangement.

#### **Track Reference Study (Ilan Bluestone / Anjunabeats Vibes):**
- Load tracks into FL, low-pass or mid-solo to hear layer depth.
- Write down what you hear:
- What’s the pad doing?
- What layers are harmonizing?
- What fills the high end?

## **Practice Plan: 30-Day Melody Layering Workout**

**Day 1–10: Melody & Basic Layering**
- Write a melody daily, stack 2 layers (lead + pad)
- Practice subtractive EQ, volume leveling

**Day 11–20: Add Depth + Space**
- Add atmosphere layer + counter melody
- Use automation for movement

**Day 21–30: Full Texture**
- Add 5–6 layers max
- Focus on stereo width, saturation, and clarity
- Compare to reference track and adjust
