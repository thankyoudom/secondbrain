# Choosing between focusing on music production and illustration depends…

Choosing between focusing on music production and illustration depends on your personal interests, goals, and the opportunities available to you. Here’s a breakdown of why you might focus on music production more, and why you might consider sticking with illustration:

### ### Why Focus on Music Production

1. **Growing Industry**: The music industry is ever-expanding, with platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube providing significant exposure. Music production allows you to tap into global audiences, and streaming services make it easier to monetize your work without the need for traditional gatekeepers.

2. **Diverse Opportunities**: Music production opens doors to various opportunities like film scoring, game soundtracks, live performances, and collaborations. There's also a broad range of genres, and with the rise of digital platforms, you can experiment and find your niche.

3. **Technological Accessibility**: With advancements in digital audio workstations (DAWs) and software, music production has become more accessible than ever. You can produce high-quality music from home with a minimal setup, and there's a constant stream of new tools and plugins to expand your creative possibilities.

4. **Creative Freedom**: If you're drawn to creating your own sound and exploring the world of audio, music production allows you to bring your vision to life in ways that visual art might not. The emotional and psychological power of music also connects with people on a deep level.

5. **Cross-Disciplinary Opportunities**: Music and illustration can be intertwined—many illustrators work on album covers, music videos, or other visual projects for musicians. If you’re a multifaceted artist, excelling in music production can complement your illustration work.

### Why Illustration Might Be Worth Focusing On:

1. **Visual Demand**: There's still a massive demand for visual art, whether for graphic design, animation, video game art, or marketing. The rise of platforms like Instagram and Pinterest has made it easier for illustrators to build a personal brand and monetize their work, especially with the growth of digital and NFT art.

2. **Sustainability and Versatility**: Illustration can often be a more sustainable career if you prefer creating visual content for businesses or individuals. The commercial sector needs illustrations for everything from branding and advertising to book covers and merchandise.

3. **Fulfilling Artistic Expression**: If you feel more connected to visual storytelling and have a strong passion for art, illustration can provide a more focused channel for your creative vision.

4. **Freelance Opportunities**: Illustration offers a lot of freelance work, and it’s easier to break into than music production if you're looking for a quick way to start generating income. There are platforms like Behance and Dribbble where illustrators can get hired for projects.

---

Ultimately, you don’t have to choose just one—if both music production and illustration are passions of yours, it’s possible to balance both depending on your goals. But if you feel more excited about the possibilities in music production, it might make sense to prioritize it—especially if you see more potential for long-term growth or are more energized by it.
