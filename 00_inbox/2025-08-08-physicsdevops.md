# physics/devops

The statements in this post are based on real sentiments that have been shared publicly by Jensen Huang (Nvidia CEO) and Elon Musk, though the way they are phrased here may be slightly simplified or paraphrased for a social media audience.
### ? Real Basis
Jensen Huang has recently said that if he were starting his career again, he'd focus on physics and understanding the real world, especially as AI starts handling more of the "routine" coding and software work. This was highlighted during interviews and public talks in 2024–2025.
Elon Musk has long promoted the idea of “first principles thinking,” which is rooted in physics. He’s mentioned many times that he approaches problems from a physics-based foundation.

? How This Applies to Your Cloud Engineering / DevOps Aspirations
If you're aiming to build a future-proof career in cloud and DevOps, here's how to use their advice meaningfully:

1. Go Beyond Coding – Understand Systems
"AI will handle routine tasks – but real-world systems are harder to replicate."
As AI automates infrastructure-as-code, deployment pipelines, and monitoring, you’ll need to master how systems behave under load, how energy and compute resources interact, and how networks function in complex real-world environments.
### You Can
Study systems design, not just tools like Docker or Kubernetes.
Explore computational physics, distributed systems, and resource optimization.
Understand how data centers work physically – cooling, power, latency, etc.

2. Bridge AI + Infra: The Future Is Intelligent Infrastructure
AI is increasingly being integrated into DevOps tools (AIOps), CI/CD optimization, and anomaly detection in logs/metrics.
### You Can
Learn how to deploy and monitor AI models at scale (MLOps).
Integrate AI with tools like Terraform, Prometheus, Grafana, KubeFlow, etc.
Use your knowledge of physics/math to design resource-efficient cloud architectures.

3. First Principles in Problem Solving
Musk’s “Physics (with math)” highlights solving problems from ground truths, not assumptions.
### In DevOps/Cloud Engineering, this helps when
Debugging bottlenecks (e.g., network latency).
Designing secure, scalable systems.
Understanding cost-efficiency (e.g., cloud billing, energy use, autoscaling logic).

4. Future-Proof Your Skills Against AI
As AI becomes better at routine ops:
Routine scripting will be commoditized.
The winners will be those who can understand, interpret, and manipulate real-world complexity – something physics/math is great at teaching.
