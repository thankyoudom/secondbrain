# Got it — being 29 means you’re automatically considered independent…

Got it — being 29 means you’re automatically considered independent for FAFSA, which actually helps ?
Here’s exactly what that means for a master’s program in South Florida, especially looking ahead to 2026.

What you CAN get from FAFSA as a graduate student
? Yes, you should absolutely file FAFSA
Even with no income right now, you are eligible to file and often qualify for the maximum federal aid available to grad students.
1. Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loan
This is the main FAFSA aid for master’s students.
Up to $20,500 per academic year
Not income-based
Interest accrues while in school
No parent info required (you’re independent)
This is basically guaranteed as long as:
You’re admitted to an eligible program
You file FAFSA correctly
You’re not in default on prior federal loans

2. Graduate PLUS Loan (optional)
### If $20,500 isn’t enough
Covers remaining cost of attendance
Requires a basic credit check (not income-based)
Higher interest rate than unsubsidized loans
Many people only use this if needed.

3. Grants (important reality check)
? Pell Grant is NOT available for master’s students
Some schools offer need-based grants once they see your FAFSA
Low or zero income can help you qualify for institutional aid
So FAFSA still matters even though federal grants are limited.
