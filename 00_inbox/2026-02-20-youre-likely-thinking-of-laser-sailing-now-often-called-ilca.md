# You’re likely thinking of Laser sailing (now often called ILCA).

While most college sailing is done in two-person boats (like FJs or 420s), the Laser is the iconic "single-handed" (one-person) boat that is used in college club and varsity competitions. It's athletic, requires you to hike out (lean over the side) to keep the boat flat, and is the most common solo boat you'll see in that scene.
A few other possibilities that fit the "college club" and "one-person" vibe:
- Sunfish: Very popular in more recreational or beginner-friendly college clubs. It’s a bit simpler and more stable than a Laser.
- Tech Dinghies: If you’re thinking of schools in the Boston area (like MIT), they have a long history of using these small, one-person boats.
- Windsurfing: Some colleges have specific windsurfing clubs, which is obviously a one-person "sailing" sport.

The equivalent of "Gorpcore" for sailing is often called "Sailingcore" or "Deckcore." While Gorpcore (short for "Good Old Raisins and Peanuts") focuses on the utilitarian gear of hiking and camping—like Arc'teryx shells and Salomon boots—the sailing version focuses on Foul Weather Gear (or "Foulies"). It’s where extreme technical performance meets a high-end, coastal aesthetic.
The Breakdown: Sailingcore vs. Gorpcore
| Feature | Gorpcore (Hiking) | Sailingcore (Sailing) |
|---|---|---|
| The "Holy Grail" Item | Arc'teryx Alpha SV Jacket | Musto HPX or Helly Hansen Salt Jacket |
| Footwear | Salomon XT-6 / Hiking Boots | Sperry Boat Shoes or Technical Deck Boots |
| Mid-Layer | Patagonia Better Sweater | Fisherman/Guernsey Wool Sweater |
| Pants | Cargo pants / Zip-offs | Salopettes (High-waisted sailing bibs) |
| Key Aesthetic | "I just climbed a mountain" | "I just survived a North Atlantic gale" |
The "Big Three" Technical Brands
If you’re looking for the sailing versions of North Face or Patagonia, these are the heavy hitters:
- Musto: Widely considered the "Arc'teryx of the sea." They make the gear used in the Volvo Ocean Race. If you're wearing a Musto MPX or HPX smock in the city, you're at peak Sailingcore.
- Helly Hansen: They have the most "crossover" appeal. Their bright yellow or red offshore jackets are iconic and frequently seen in streetwear.
- Gill Marine: Pure performance. If Musto is luxury tech, Gill is the gritty, reliable workhorse gear.
How it relates to your "One-Man" Sailing (Laser Sailing)
Since you mentioned the Laser (the one-man boat), that specific sport has a sub-style called "Dinghy-core." It’s less about big heavy jackets and more about:
- Spray Tops: Lightweight, windproof pull-overs.
- Hikers: Neoprene pants with reinforced pads for leaning off the boat.
- Zhik Boots: Very technical, high-grip neoprene boots.
