# Spain is a great place to live, especially if you're in your 30s, tech…

Spain is a great place to live, especially if you're in your 30s, tech-savvy, and looking for a lifestyle that blends work-life balance with a solid community of like-minded people. I'll break down your questions, starting with the tech scene and then dive into the specifics of living in Spain, particularly Andalusia.
Tech Scene in Spain
Yes, Spain has a growing tech scene, especially in cities like Barcelona, Madrid, and Valencia, though it’s more nascent compared to places like Berlin or London. These cities have active startup ecosystems, great co-working spaces, and many opportunities for networking in the tech world.
Barcelona, in particular, is a hot spot for tech entrepreneurs and remote workers. The city offers a unique mix of innovation with the Mediterranean lifestyle. Madrid, being the capital, is also a central hub for both tech and finance. If you're more into AI, software development, or tech consulting, you’ll find ample opportunities.
However, if you're looking for a more laid-back, affordable area to live while still having access to the digital nomad and tech scene, Valencia and Malaga (in Andalusia) are becoming increasingly popular with remote workers and techies. Tech events, hackathons, and meetups are growing in these cities, and there’s a strong digital nomad community, especially in places like Málaga or Sevilla.
Owning an Acre of Land in Spain
In terms of real estate, buying land in Spain can vary quite a bit depending on location. In Andalusia, you could realistically find rural land for much cheaper than in metropolitan areas. An acre (around 4,000 square meters) of land can range widely depending on proximity to cities and the type of land (e.g., agricultural, residential, or undeveloped).
Rural Andalusia (outskirts): Expect to pay €5,000 to €15,000 ($5,300 to $16,000 USD) per acre, depending on how close it is to infrastructure and amenities. If you're looking for something closer to a town or city, prices will go up.
More developed areas (closer to cities): Prices can range between €20,000 to €50,000+ ($21,000 to $53,000 USD) per acre, depending on the proximity to a town or a city.
Cost of Living in Spain (Andalusia)
Spain is generally more affordable than countries like the U.S. or other Western European nations, especially when it comes to food, housing, and transportation. Here’s a rough breakdown of costs for a single person:
### Rent (1-bedroom apartment in city center)
Málaga, Seville, or Granada: Around €600–€900 ($650–$975 USD) per month
In more rural areas, you could find something for €300–€500 ($325–$540 USD) per month.
Utilities (electricity, water, internet): Around €100–€150 ($105–$160 USD) per month.
Groceries: A basic grocery bill for a single person will be €150–€250 ($160–$270 USD) per month.
Eating out: A simple meal at a local restaurant will cost €10–€15 ($10–$16 USD). Dining out at mid-range places could be closer to €20–€40 ($21–$42 USD) per person.
Transportation: Public transport costs about €40–€60 ($42–$64 USD) per month, but if you’re in a smaller town, you might not need it at all, as everything is walkable or bikeable.
Comfortable Living in Andalusia
If you were to aim for a comfortable lifestyle in Andalusia, with moderate leisure and travel, living on about $2,000 to $2,500 USD per month would cover most of your needs and give you room to enjoy yourself. If you have a remote tech job or a freelance income in USD, this would be a solid budget for a comfortable life, including rent, utilities, food, transportation, and some savings.
Can Techies Make It in Andalusia?
Yes! There’s an increasing number of tech-savvy people moving to places like Málaga, Seville, and Granada for the combination of lower cost of living, good internet infrastructure, and the more relaxed pace of life compared to bigger cities like Madrid or Barcelona.
Málaga, for example, has become a digital nomad hotspot, with plenty of coworking spaces, good internet, and a community of remote workers and entrepreneurs. The city has even been working on attracting startups and tech talent with incentives, and it’s close to some of Spain’s best beaches.
However, if you're thinking of living in very rural Andalusia or off the beaten path, make sure to check the internet and connectivity before committing, as some remote areas might have limited infrastructure for tech work.
Final Thoughts
Tech scene: Active and growing, especially in cities like Barcelona, Madrid, and Málaga.
Cost of land: Quite affordable, especially in rural Andalusia, but the price increases near cities or coastlines.
Cost of living: Much lower than in the U.S. or many Western European countries. Around $2,000–$2,500 USD per month can cover a comfortable lifestyle.
Techies: Absolutely, especially if you work remotely or in a niche like software development, digital marketing, or startups. You’d likely have a more affordable and high-quality life than in major cities in the U.S.
