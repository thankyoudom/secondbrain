# Alright, let’s do gentle feng shui (I like “gent shui” actually — very…

Alright, let’s do gentle feng shui (I like “gent shui” actually — very on-brand for you). This won’t be superstition-heavy; think environmental psychology + nervous system cues.
You’re already doing a lot right. I’ll go layer by layer: sleep, orientation, airflow, zones, and upgrades.

1. Sleeping on the ground (posture + psyche)
Pros
Ground sleeping increases proprioceptive feedback ? calmer nervous system
Encourages spinal neutrality (especially with knees supported)
Psychologically signals simplicity + control (very Jungian, actually)
Watch-outs
Too hard = body never fully surrenders
If sleep quality drops, posture gains aren’t worth it
Gent shui tweak
Put something between you and the floor:
thin tatami
yoga mat + wool blanket
firm shikibuton-style pad
This keeps the “grounded” effect without telling your nervous system it’s on alert all night.

2. Feet toward the door (this is the biggest flag ?)
In feng shui, this is called “coffin position.”
In nervous-system terms: your brain stays semi-vigilant.
### Why it matters
Door = entry of uncertainty
Feet-first = subconscious vulnerability
Even if you don’t believe it, your body does
Best setup
Head against a solid wall
Door visible from bed
But not directly in line with feet
If you can’t change it
Put a low bench, trunk, or folded blanket at your feet
? creates a symbolic “boundary”
Or angle your body slightly off-axis from the door
This alone can noticeably deepen sleep.

3. Sleeping on your back (good, but refine it)
### You’ve got
Eye patch ?? (excellent — darkness = melatonin + parasympathetic tone)
Pillow under knees ?? (reduces lumbar tension)
### Two refinements
Add neck support that keeps chin neutral (not tilted back)
Keep arms slightly elevated (small pillow under forearms)
? reduces shoulder tension + chest openness
Back sleeping is ideal if the body feels protected. Which brings us to…

4. Air purifier fan on you at night ??
Clean air = great.
Air blowing directly on you = mixed signal.
### Psychologically
Constant airflow = “outdoors / alert”
Can prevent full parasympathetic drop
Better approach
Angle it past you, not at you
Or bounce airflow off a wall
Keep sound low and consistent (white noise is fine)
You want the room breathing — not poking you.

5. Living in kitchen + living room (energy zoning)
This actually explains a lot.
Kitchen = doing, feeding, managing
Living room = social, alert, outward
If you spend most of your day there:
You’re reinforcing function over rest
Your nervous system never fully switches modes
Gent shui fix
Create a third zone, even if tiny:
One chair
One lamp
One surface (no screens)
### This is your
“Non-productive, non-consuming” spot
You draw, read, or just sit there.
No phone. No laptop. No music production.
This gives your psyche a home base that isn’t tied to output.

6. Missing element: containment
### Your setup is very yang
Minimal
Functional
Controlled
Optimized
What’s missing is containment / yin.
### Add one or two
Heavy curtain or tapestry
Warm, indirect light (2700K)
One grounded object you like (stone, wood, ceramic)
Not clutter. Weight.
### This tells your body
“You don’t have to stay sharp here.”

7. One subtle but powerful upgrade
### Before sleep
Place phone outside the room or in a closed drawer
Even if it’s off
### Jung + feng shui both agree
Objects carry symbolic charge.
A phone nearby = latent social field = ego half-awake.

TL;DR – biggest gains for you
### If you only do 3 things
Fix feet-to-door alignment (or block it)
Diffuse airflow, don’t blast yourself
Create one yin, non-productive zone
You’re already disciplined.
This is about letting your environment do some of the work so you don’t have to.
