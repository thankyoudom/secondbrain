# You're running into one of the biggest pain points for TikTok creators…

You're running into one of the biggest pain points for TikTok creators: audio compression. TikTok does degrade sound quality, especially if you're uploading music embedded in video from editing apps like CapCut. The “Import Sound” feature helps a bit, but it still compresses things and can flatten your mix.
To preserve your sound quality and make your beats easier to reuse by others (which boosts virality), your instinct is right: distribute your audio to TikTok as a sound via an official distributor.

### ? How It Works
Create an Account on Routenote
Sign up free.
Verify identity.
Upload Your Track
Choose Free Distribution option.

Wait for Approval

Use Your Sound in TikTok Videos
Go to TikTok, search your artist name or track name.
Select the sound and start building content around it.
Encourage others to reuse it: artists, dancers, sketch creators, etc.

? Pro Tips
Create short, hooky loopable beats for TikTok distribution (15–30s ideal).
Add artist tags in sound title, e.g. Sunset Skies (prod. @yourhandle)
Encourage art creators to use your sound — this will spread organically in your niche.
Drop visuals + track previews on your TikTok while you wait for distribution to go live (build anticipation).
You can later upload full beats to streaming platforms like Spotify via Routenote too.

? What NOT to Do
Don’t rely on uploading beats inside video unless you have no choice. Audio will always compress.
Don’t over-EQ/master — TikTok compression can harshen bright highs and sub-bass.
Don’t re-upload TikTok-exported vids back into CapCut; they degrade quality every time.
