# Awesome — you’ve got a great mindset, and you’re already doing more…

Awesome — you’ve got a great mindset, and you’re already doing more than most. Let's build a clear, structured plan around your goal of going from 120 lbs to 140 lbs, primarily through calisthenics and bodyweight training, and supported by nutrition. That’s a 20 lb muscle-focused gain, which is challenging but totally doable with consistency.

1. Goal Timeline
Target Gain: 20 lbs (mostly lean mass)
Rate of Gain: ~0.5–1 lb/week (any faster increases fat gain risk)
Estimated Timeframe: 5–7 months, assuming consistent training + nutrition

2. Calorie & Protein Goals
To gain muscle, you need to eat in a slight surplus.
Maintenance Calories: ~2,000–2,200/day (based on 5'5", 120 lbs, active)
Target Calories for Muscle Gain: ~2,500–2,700/day
### Macronutrients Breakdown (for lean gains)
Protein: 1.0g – 1.2g per lb = 120–145g/day
Fats: ~25–30% of calories = ~70–90g/day
Carbs: Fill the rest (~250–300g/day)
Sample Meal Ideas & Strategy? I can help with that too, if you'd like.

3. Training Plan (6 Days/Week, Sunday Off)
Focus: Progressive overload with your own bodyweight + minimal equipment. Pushup and pullup volume is great, but we’ll tweak it to stimulate hypertrophy and recovery.
Weekly Structure
Monday: Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
Tuesday: Pull (Back, Biceps)
Wednesday: Legs + Core
Thursday: Push
Friday: Pull
Saturday: Legs + Core
Sunday: Rest
Example Daily Breakdown
Push Day (Chest/Shoulders/Triceps)
Pushups: 5x20 (vary angles: incline/decline/diamond)
Pike Pushups: 4x10
Dips (if available): 3x8–12 (use chairs or band-assisted)
Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3x10 (slow reps with your 20 lb dumbbells)
Resistance Band Chest Flies: 3x12
Pull Day (Back/Biceps)
Pull-ups: 5xMax (use different grips: wide/neutral/chin-up)
Negative Pull-ups or Band-Assisted Pull-ups: 3x6–8
Dumbbell Rows: 3x12/side
Resistance Band Face Pulls: 3x15
Bicep Curls (dumbbell or band): 3x12–15
Legs + Core
Bulgarian Split Squats: 3x10 each leg
Dumbbell Goblet Squats: 4x12
Glute Bridges (single or both legs): 3x15
Resistance Band Lateral Walks: 2x20 steps
Hanging Leg Raises or Lying Leg Raises: 3x15
Planks (front and side): 3 sets x 30–60 sec

4. Progressive Overload (Key for Muscle Gain)
Track reps and sets weekly.
Slowly increase reps, add sets, or reduce rest time.
When exercises become easy, make them harder:
Pushups: Try pseudo planche pushups, explosive, archer pushups.
Pullups: Add slow negatives, holds at top, or band resistance.

5. Sleep & Recovery
Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep/night.
Hydration: At least 3 liters/day.
Sunday is your only rest day — be strict about it.
