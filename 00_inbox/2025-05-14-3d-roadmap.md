# 3D roadmap

Learning CAD (Computer-Aided Design) is a smart move—especially with your interest in jewelry design. CAD isn’t just for mechanical engineers anymore. It’s a core skill across many creative and technical industries, and it can open up solid career paths and freelance opportunities. Let’s break it down:

? Why Learn CAD in 2025?
? Career Flexibility & Future-Proofing
CAD is used in jewelry, fashion, architecture, interior design, product design, film/VFX, gaming, VR/AR, and manufacturing.
As 3D printing, digital fabrication, and virtual environments expand, CAD skills become even more valuable.
Remote-friendly: You can freelance from anywhere once you’re skilled.

? CAD in Jewelry Design
### CAD is heavily used in
Ring design
Custom grillz
Tooth gems
Chains, pendants, earrings
Luxury and high-end prototyping
Programs like Rhino, MatrixGold, and ZBrush (for organic sculpting) are standards in this space. You can design intricate pieces for:
3D printing for casting
Custom client work
Selling STL files (digital product income)

? How Long to Learn from Zero?
?
Consistent practice (4–8 hours/week) with tutorials and projects will get you client-ready in 6–12 months.

? Salaries & Income Potential
?
Selling STL files, taking custom design commissions, and even creating NFTs or digital assets are modern monetization routes.

? Industries & Serious Career Paths
Without a mechanical engineering background, here are real opportunities:
? Jewelry & Fashion Design
In-house or freelance CAD designer
Work with casting houses or luxury brands
Build your own brand or Etsy shop
? Product Design
Freelance prototyping for consumer goods
3D printing-focused design studios
? Entertainment & Media
Model props/jewelry for game or film assets
Work in 3D environments for AR/VR filters or assets
? Dental & Medical Modeling
High-end work: grills, crowns, custom tooth accessories
Often uses CAD + 3D scanning

? What You Can Start With (Free or Cheap Tools)
Blender – Free, powerful for modeling and rendering
Fusion 360 – Free for personal/hobby use; great for mechanical + organic
Rhino 3D – Paid, but industry standard for jewelry (look for student/demo)
Tinkercad – Super basic but a great place to learn fundamentals
ZBrushCoreMini – Free sculpting, good for grill/tooth gem modeling

? Pathway You Could Take
Learn Blender (basic modeling + rendering)
Move into Fusion 360 or Rhino for precision and real-world applications
Start modeling rings, grills, pendants
Learn how to export for 3D printing/casting
Build a small portfolio or online store
Network with dental techs, jewelers, or fashion artists
Scale into freelance or in-house design roles

? Final Thoughts
You don’t need a mechanical background—just a strong design sense, patience, and comfort with 3D space. CAD can turn your creative ideas into something real and profitable, with room to grow into other industries if you choose.
