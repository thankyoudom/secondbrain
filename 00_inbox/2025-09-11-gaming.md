# gaming

I get what you mean — RuneScape had that sweet spot: a big world, lots of skills, social play, but totally free-to-play (with optional upgrades). If you’re looking to scratch that same itch in 2025, here are some equivalents worth checking out:

? MMORPGs with Free Play
RuneScape 3 & Old School RuneScape (OSRS) – Still going strong, both are free to jump into. OSRS in particular has a huge community and regular updates. If you want that exact vibe, this is the safest bet.
Albion Online – Very RuneScape-esque in its sandbox style. You can gather, craft, trade, PvE, or PvP. Fully free-to-play, cross-platform, and the economy is player-driven.
Trove – More cartoony/voxel-based, but has that same "do anything" feel with building, crafting, and adventuring.
AdventureQuest 3D – A modernized version of the old browser game. More lightweight but scratches the casual MMO itch.

?? Free Sandbox / Social Games
Wurm Online – Survival crafting MMO (the game Notch worked on before Minecraft). Slow-paced but very RuneScape-y in skills and grinding.
Project: Gorgon – Quirky, old-school-style MMO where you can literally learn oddball skills like cheese-making. Free demo, small but dedicated community.
BitCraft (in testing) – Upcoming sandbox MMO that many people compare to RuneScape in ambition. Worth keeping on your radar.

?? If You Liked the “Level Everything” Grind
Villagers & Heroes – Free MMO with a focus on gathering, crafting, and casual play.
Lost Ark – More action-combat, but still F2P with lots of progression and exploration.

? Browser / Low-Spec Options (like OG RuneScape was)
Muck / Survive.io / Browser RPGs on Itch.io – If you want quick and light browser-based survival/RPG experiences.
Genfanad – A modern indie MMORPG that’s explicitly inspired by RuneScape.

? If you liked RuneScape mainly for the social + chill grind, Albion Online and OSRS are probably your best bets today. If you liked it for the exploration + quirky skills, check out Project: Gorgon or Villagers & Heroes.
