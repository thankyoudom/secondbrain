# Rebuilding your credit from a score of 400 with $6,000 owed can take…

Rebuilding your credit from a score of 400 with $6,000 owed can take 6 to 24 months or more, depending on your specific actions and how the debt is handled. Here's a breakdown of what affects your timeline and how to accelerate the process:

? 1. Understanding Your Starting Point
A credit score of 400 is considered very poor (FICO range: 300–850).
Owing $6,000 is not insurmountable, but what matters is:
Type of debt (credit cards, collections, charge-offs, etc.)
Payment history (any recent late payments?)
Credit utilization (how much you owe relative to your limits)

? 3. Key Steps to Rebuild Your Credit
? A. Address the $6,000 Debt
Pay down high-interest accounts first (especially credit cards).
If accounts are in collections, negotiate a settlement or pay-for-delete.
Consider a debt snowball or avalanche strategy.
Get current on any late payments immediately (or within 30 days).
? B. Start Positive Credit Behavior
Apply for a secured credit card or credit-builder loan (keep usage under 10% of limit).
Make all payments on time — payment history is 35% of your FICO score.
Don’t close old accounts (helps with credit age and utilization).
Ask for credit line increases after 6–12 months of good behavior.
? C. Avoid New Damage
No more late payments or defaulting.
Don’t apply for too many new accounts (each inquiry dings you slightly).
Monitor credit reports for errors — dispute anything inaccurate.

? 4. Example Recovery Scenario
Case: $6,000 on 3 credit cards; all maxed out; 2 recent 60-day late payments
Action: Start making minimums + $500/month extra, open secured card
