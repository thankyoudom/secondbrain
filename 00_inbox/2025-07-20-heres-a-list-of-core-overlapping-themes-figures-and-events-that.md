# Here's a list of core overlapping themes, figures, and events that…

Here's a list of core overlapping themes, figures, and events that are present in all three scriptures—the Torah (Jewish Bible), Christian Bible, and Qur’an (Islamic scripture). These are stories or principles explicitly present in all three texts, not just implied or traditional.

? 1. Monotheism (Belief in One God)
### All three scriptures affirm
There is only one God.
He is the Creator, all-powerful, and eternal.
Idol worship is condemned.

? 2. Adam and Eve
First humans created by God.
Lived in Paradise (Garden of Eden).
Disobeyed God by eating from the forbidden tree.
Expelled from paradise but not abandoned.

? 3. Noah and the Great Flood
God sent a flood to cleanse the earth of corruption.
Noah was a righteous man chosen to build the ark.
Saved his family and pairs of animals.
Covenant with God after the flood.

??? 4. Abraham (Ibrahim)
Father of monotheism in all three.
Made a covenant with God.
Willing to sacrifice his son in obedience (Isaac in Bible/Torah, likely Ishmael in Qur’an).
Respected as a prophet and patriarch.

? 5. Moses (Musa) and Pharaoh
Sent by God to free the Israelites from Egypt.
Faced Pharaoh, showed miracles/signs.
Parting of the sea.
Received the commandments on Mount Sinai.

? 6. The Ten Commandments / Divine Law
Given to Moses.
### Moral principles like
Worship one God.
Do not steal, kill, lie, or commit adultery.
Honor parents.
Note: Wording differs, but the core moral law is affirmed in all.

? 7. Judgment, Heaven, and Hell
God will judge humanity.
There is life after death.
The righteous will be rewarded (Heaven).
The wicked will be punished (Hell).

? 8. Angels
Exist and serve God.
Act as messengers, guardians, and agents of God's will.
E.g., Gabriel (Jibril) is the angel of revelation.

? 9. Satan (Iblis/Shaytan)
A rebellious being who defied God.
Tempted Adam and Eve.
Continues to mislead humans.

? 10. Prophethood
God sends prophets to guide humanity.
Prophets deliver revelation, warn of punishment, call to righteousness.
Common prophets: Noah, Abraham, Moses, Lot, Joseph, David, Solomon, Jonah.

??? 11. Ethical Foundations
### Emphasis on
Justice
Charity
Compassion
Honesty
Caring for orphans, widows, and the poor

? 12. Joseph (Yusuf) and His Brothers
Story of jealousy, being sold into slavery.
Rises to power in Egypt.
Eventually forgives and reunites with his family.

? 13. Destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah
Cities destroyed due to extreme wickedness.
Lot (Lut) was sent as a warner.
His wife looked back and was destroyed.

? 14. David (Dawud) and Solomon (Sulaiman)
Kings and prophets.
Known for wisdom, judgment, and devotion.
Solomon's rule is marked by justice and building the temple.
