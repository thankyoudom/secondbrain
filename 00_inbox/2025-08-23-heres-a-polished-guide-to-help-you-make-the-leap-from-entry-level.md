# Here’s a polished guide to help you make the leap from entry-level…

Here’s a polished guide to help you make the leap from entry-level Cloud Developer in South Florida to a successful DevOps-focused career in Chicago. I’ve broken it down into key categories: companies hiring, training & certification, communities & networking, and a practical roadmap to orient your move.

Companies & Opportunities in Chicago
Established and Emerging Employers
ServiceNow (Addison/Chicago) — entry-level roles like Associate Customer Success Guide involve onboarding SaaS/cloud products.(Built In Chicago)
GHX, Kraft Heinz, CNA, Samsara and others are hiring entry-level across Cloud, Big Data, Fintech, and Automation.(Built In Chicago)
Junior & Entry Cloud Engineer Roles
On ZipRecruiter, Junior Cloud Engineer roles in Chicago pay average around $74,000/year (~$35.6/hour), with common responsibilities like deployment, monitoring, and CI/CD support.(ZipRecruiter)
ZipRecruiter also notes entry cloud engineers make around $134,800/year on average (~$64.8/hour).(ZipRecruiter)
Chicago distribution: ~74% in-person, 16% hybrid, 10% remote.(ZipRecruiter)
Companies Hiring Remote or Hybrid
CrowdStrike, SailPoint, Guidewire Software, John Deere, among others, offer remote entry-level cloud positions tied to Chicago headquarters.(Built In Chicago)
Job Platforms to Explore
Indeed, ZipRecruiter, and Dice are solid sources for browsing entry-level Cloud/DevOps roles in Chicago.(Indeed, Dice)
Summary: You’ll find both large enterprises and startups in Chicago hiring for Cloud and DevOps foundational roles—with salaries ranging roughly between $65k–$135k depending on the job type and seniority.

Training & Certifications
Polish your skills and resume with these in-city and online learning paths:
University of Chicago – DevOps Program: Teaches CI, infrastructure design, virtualization, deployment, and includes completion credentials and digital badge.(programs.onlineprofessionaluchicago.com)
### In-person Certification Courses
Invensis offers a 2-day DevOps Foundation training.(Invensis Learning)
Edureka’s DevOps training covers Git, Jenkins, Docker, Ansible, Kubernetes, Terraform, Prometheus/Grafana (~$449 USD).(edureka.co)
### Other Options
NetCom Learning, Accelebrate, ExcelR, DevOpsSchool, Hartmann Software, CourseJet offer bootcamps or specialized DevOps training in Chicago.(Built In Chicago, Nevolearn, hartmannsoftware.com, coursejet.com, excelr.com, DevOps School, NobleProg, Advance Agility)

Communities & Networking in Chicago
Networking is a huge advantage for career transitions. Here’s where to build connections:
Chicago Cloud Computing Meetup – open group for discussions, best practices, and hearing about job opportunities.(Meetup, Sessionize)
Chicago AWS User Group – monthly free evening events in the Loop featuring peer-led presentations (not sales pitches), with pizza, Q&A, and strong community.(Meetup)
GDG Cloud Chicago – Google Developers Group chapter with cloud-focused meetups.(Google Developer Groups)
Cloud Community of Practice by Northwestern – bimonthly roundtables sharing years of cloud experience.(Northwestern University - Services)
DePaul Cloud Organization (DeHUB) – student group with expert access and networking on campus.(DePaul University Hub)
AFCOM Chicago – a professional association focused on IT/data center infrastructure career growth.(AFCOM)
GitHub repository of local engineering & tech communities – curated Slack/Discord channels and meetups in Chicago.(GitHub)

Roadmap: Making the Transition from Florida to Chicago
1. Strengthen Your Skills
Obtain a foundational certification (e.g., AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner, Azure Fundamentals) — highly valued for entry-level roles.
Deepen tooling experience: focus on Git, CI/CD tools (Jenkins, Travis), containers (Docker, Kubernetes), Infrastructure as Code (Terraform), and cloud (AWS/Azure/GCP).
2. Portfolio & Resume
Build mini-projects: deploy a sample app to the cloud, set up a CI/CD pipeline, automate infra with Terraform.
Host code on GitHub; share a blog post or portfolio that explains your process and learnings.
3. Engage in Chicago Communities (Virtually, then In-Person)
Join AWS Chicago, GDG Cloud Chicago, Cloud Computing Meetup to make connections and learn.
Start presenting or documenting a project through sessionize calls or offering to speak locally.(Sessionize)
Use the Slack/Discord list to connect with fellow developers and recruiters.(GitHub)
4. Upskill Through Training
Attend local bootcamps or courses (e.g., University of Chicago, Invensis, Edureka).
Choose short, credentials-based programs that boost your resume quickly.
5. Job Search Strategy
Target companies large and small offering entry cloud roles—filter by entry-level cloud engineer, DevOps engineer, junior cloud developer.
Consider remote/hybrid roles from Chicago companies (like CrowdStrike, SailPoint).(Built In Chicago)
Apply broadly but strategically; tailor applications to highlight your cloud and automation projects.
6. Plan Your Move
Research Chicago neighborhoods near your target employers; consider balance of commute, costs, and vibe.

Local bootcamps in Chicago sometimes offer merit-based or need-based scholarships, including programs like LaunchCode and scholarships for underrepresented groups such as women in tech, e.g., via Nucamp.(Nucamp)
3. Code Platoon — DevOps & Cloud Engineering
Code Platoon offers a DevOps and Cloud Engineering program (~$19,000) with scholarship eligibility and apprenticeship or preferred hiring pipelines for graduates.(Code Platoon)
** Other Broader and Tech-Focused Scholarships**
