## Key Insights
• Jean-Paul Sartre's concept of "bad faith" (mauvaise foi) is relevant to understanding how people deny or repress their own freedom.
• Existentialism emphasizes individual choice and responsibility.

---

Sartre's philosophy focuses on human existence and the concept of "being-for-itself." This means that humans have complete freedom to choose their actions, but this freedom also brings a sense of responsibility. People often try to escape or deny this responsibility through "bad faith," where they pretend to be something they're not or blame external circumstances for their choices.

Existentialism is often misunderstood as being pessimistic or nihilistic, but it's actually about embracing the reality of human existence and taking ownership of one's choices. By acknowledging our freedom and responsibility, we can live more authentically and make meaningful decisions.

Sartre's ideas have influenced many areas of philosophy, including ethics, politics, and psychology. His concept of "bad faith" is particularly relevant in understanding how people cope with the burden of their own freedom.
