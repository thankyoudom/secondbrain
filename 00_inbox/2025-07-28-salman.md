# salman

Here’s a closer look at Mohammed bin Salman (MBS)—his background, personal routines, reading habits, tech focus, and near-future plans—based on the latest verifiable information:

? Education & Early Career
Born in 1985, MBS is Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia and chairs both the Public Investment Fund (PIF) and the Saudi Data & AI Authority (SDAIA) (SDAIA).
He studied law and political science domestically and has no formal Western university credential. His rise was rapid following senior royal appointments in the Riyadh governorate and Ministry of Defense.

? Daily Routine & Habits
Concrete details on his daily personal habits are scarce. In interviews and profiles like TIME, he’s portrayed as highly driven: long working hours, commanding meetings, and personally overseeing megaprojects like NEOM and massive AI initiatives (THE MENA).
While the story about reading one book a week circulates, direct confirmation is absent in mainstream sources. Nevertheless, his leadership of multiple academic and innovation foundations (Misk Foundation, Nonprofit City, and Ilmi centre) indicates a strong interest in knowledge empowerment and education (Wikipedia).

? Reading & Recommended Books
There’s no verified list of his favorite books. The “book a week” anecdote remains anecdotal—but given his founding of the Misk Foundation and institutions focusing on innovation, the emphasis on continuous learning is clear (Wikipedia, Wikipedia).

? Tech & AI Focus
AI Leadership & Strategy
MBS launched HUMAIN in May 2025, the national AI company under PIF, aiming to build cloud infrastructure, data centers, and multimodal Arabic LLMs. He chairs HUMAIN directly, underscoring personal commitment to making Saudi Arabia a global AI hub (Wikipedia).
Saudi Arabia’s National Strategy for Data & AI (approved in July 2020) targets top-15 global ranking in AI by 2030, training 20,000+ specialists, and attracting ~75?billion?SAR investments (SDAIA).
Broader Tech Investments
Founded Alat (Feb 2024), a PIF-owned industrial tech conglomerate focused on semiconductors, smart devices, and infrastructure—projected to contribute billions to GDP by 2030 (Wikipedia).
Supports the Prince Mohammed Bin Salman College for Cybersecurity & AI, the first dedicated institution in Saudi Arabia with international partnerships (Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, etc.) (Wikipedia).

? Vision to 2050 & Near-Future Plans
His core framework is Vision 2030, which includes AI, renewable energy, tourism, and entertainment to reduce oil dependency. Projects like NEOM, the Red Sea Project, and Qiddiya are central pillars (THE MENA).
Through HUMAIN and Alat, the aim is to cement Saudi Arabia as a supercharger in AI and advanced manufacturing by 2030. Long-term, he projects the Kingdom as a global innovation leader well past 2050. Analysts note ambitions as high as lunar or Mars-scale aspirations in tech and science (TIME).
MBS is also crafting Saudi Arabia’s global digital diplomacy—hosting global AI summits (e.g., upcoming September 2025) and chairing strategic coordination via entities like the Digital Cooperation Organization (Saudi Press).

? Personal Side & Leadership Style
Observers call him a modernizer: informal, tech-savvy, aggressive in reforms, but also hard-edged—consolidating power via anti-corruption purges, and drawing criticism for human rights infractions such as the Khashoggi assassination (TIME).
His pragmatic diplomacy balances ties with the U.S., China, and Russia to pursue a “Saudi-first” agenda—seeking strategic autonomy rather than dependence on any bloc (TIME).
