# To build an audience on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube as a music…

To build an audience on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube as a music producer, you’ll need a strategic plan. Here’s a detailed daily and weekly breakdown to help you grow your presence and engage with your audience effectively.

### Daily Plan (Monday to Sunday)

**1. Content Creation (1-2 hours daily)**
- **Beat Making Videos:** Create short videos (15-60 seconds) showing your beat-making process. Focus on catchy snippets that highlight unique elements of your beats.
- **Behind-the-Scenes:** Share the creative process, your setup, or how you get inspired.
- **Tutorials:** Create quick tips or tricks for music production. These can be 30-second videos on specific techniques or gear.

**2. Posting Schedule**
- **TikTok:** Post 1-2 times daily. Optimal times are typically 6-10 PM when users are most active.
- **Instagram:** Post 1-2 times daily (feed + stories). Aim for high engagement times (11 AM - 1 PM, 7-9 PM).
- **YouTube:** Post 1 video weekly (5-10 minutes). Longer tutorials or beat showcases work well here.

**3. Engagement (30 minutes daily)**
- **Comment on Other Creators’ Posts:** Engage with similar music producers or genres.
- **Respond to Comments:** Always reply to comments on your posts to encourage community.
- **Direct Messages:** Reach out to followers or collaborate with other creators in your niche.

### Weekly Plan

**Monday:**
- Create and post a beat-making video (TikTok, Instagram).
- Engage with 10 relevant accounts by commenting and liking.

**Tuesday:**
- Post a behind-the-scenes video (TikTok, Instagram).
- Share a production tip on stories or a short video.

**Wednesday:**
- Release a tutorial video on YouTube. Promote it on your other platforms.
- Engage with followers and respond to comments on YouTube.

**Thursday:**
- Post a collaboration video (either with another producer or a vocalist).
- Host a Q&A on Instagram Stories about music production.

**Friday:**
- Post a fun or humorous video related to music production (TikTok).
- Share your weekly favorite music tools or samples on Instagram.

**Saturday:**
- Create a video showcasing a finished beat and how it evolved.
- Engage with followers and comment on similar content.

**Sunday:**
- Plan and record next week’s content (dedicate 1-2 hours).
- Reflect on the week’s engagement metrics and adjust strategy as needed.

### Additional Tips

- **Hashtags:** Use relevant hashtags (e.g., #musicproducer, #beatmaking) to reach a wider audience. Aim for 5-10 hashtags per post.
- **Trends:** Stay updated on trending sounds and challenges, especially on TikTok. Adapt them to fit your music.
- **Collaborations:** Regularly collaborate with other musicians or creators. This can expand your audience significantly.
- **Analytics:** Use insights from each platform to analyze what type of content performs best and adjust accordingly.

### Growth Metrics

- Aim for at least 50-100 followers on TikTok and Instagram per week initially.
- Track engagement rates, likes, comments, and shares to refine your content strategy.
- After a few months, consider running small ads on Instagram or TikTok to promote your best-performing posts.

By following this structured approach, you'll steadily build your audience across TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube while showcasing your skills as a music producer.
