# Getting into streaming in 2026 is a different beast than it was a…

Getting into streaming in 2026 is a different beast than it was a few years ago. Kick has moved past its "Wild West" phase and is now a high-velocity, personality-first platform. Since your goals are hosting, communication, and authenticity, you’re actually in a great spot—those are the "soft skills" that the Kick algorithm (which prizes Chat Velocity) rewards most.
Here is how you can leverage your Procreate skills and music taste to dominate a niche while building those host muscles.
1. Top Niches on Kick (and where you fit)
While "Slots & Casino" still pulls the biggest numbers, the Non-Gaming and Creative sectors are seeing the fastest relative growth this year.
| Niche | Why it works for you | Skill Development |
|---|---|---|
| The "Art Lounge" | Blending Procreate with your HiFi DJ mixes. High-quality visuals + high-quality audio. | Authenticity: It’s hard to fake a vibe for 4 hours. You learn to be yourself while working. |
| Just Chatting / IRL | Using a "Facecam" while you draw to engage directly with viewers. | Hosting: You have to drive the "show" without a game to hide behind. |
| Niche Gaming | Games like Rocket League or Euro Truck Sim 2 (currently trending on Kick) which allow for talk-heavy streams. | Communication: Balancing a task (gaming) with constant audience engagement. |
2. The "DJ Art Lounge" Strategy
Don't just draw; curate an experience. Kick viewers in 2026 crave "event-style" content.
- The Hook: Instead of "Drawing on Procreate," try "Designing [Niche] + HiFi Deep House Mix."
- The Tech: Mirror your iPad to OBS. Use a high-quality mic—if you want to develop hosting skills, your voice needs to sound professional.
- Music & Copyright: Kick’s DMCA system is more "progressive" than Twitch's, but still present. Use licensed tracks or "Stream-Safe" HiFi mixes to ensure your VODs (Past Broadcasts) don't get muted.
3. Developing the "Host" Mentality
Streaming is 10% the activity and 90% the hosting. To develop those skills:
- Narrate the Micro-Decisions: Don't just draw a line; explain why you chose that brush or that color. This kills the "dead air" and builds your ability to speak while performing.
- The "Invisible Crowd" Rule: Act as if 100 people are watching even when the count is at 0. If you only start "hosting" when someone types in chat, you’ve already missed the chance to hook them.
- Focus on Chat Velocity: Don't just answer questions; ask them. "What’s everyone’s favorite track in this mix?" "Should I go neon or pastel for the shading?"
4. The Path to Consistency: KCIP 2026
Kick's Creator Incentive Program (KCIP) is your best friend. In 2026, it offers an hourly wage ($16–$32/hr) for streamers who hit certain metrics (typically ~75 average viewers).
- No Zero Days: Even if it’s a 1-hour "sketch and chill" session, go live. Consistency isn't just about the schedule; it's about the "streak."
- The 95/5 Split: Remember that Kick lets you keep 95% of sub revenue. This makes even a small, dedicated community of 20 people highly profitable compared to other platforms.
> Pro Tip: Turn off your viewer count while you're live. It’s the fastest way to kill your authenticity. If the number goes down, you'll sound discouraged; if it goes up, you'll sound nervous. Host for the "ghosts" and the regulars will stay.
