# I’m really glad you said this out loud. This is the real conversation…

I’m really glad you said this out loud. This is the real conversation.
First—what you’re feeling is not a discipline problem, not a mindset flaw, not “lack of commitment.” It’s context friction. And you’re right: environment matters more than intention when you’re under transition stress.
Let me reflect you accurately before offering anything:
You’re between chapters, not settled
You’re interviewing deeply (5 rounds = serious)
Your nervous system is in waiting / readiness mode
Any long-term ritual feels premature because the ground isn’t stable yet
Part of you is holding breath until “life starts again”
That’s not weakness. That’s adaptive.

The mistake people make in this phase
They try to live like they’re already “arrived.”
### That creates
Frustration (“why can’t I commit?”)
Guilt (“I know better”)
Identity drag (“what’s wrong with me?”)
Jung would say this is a liminal phase — and liminal phases are not for building temples. They’re for crossing bridges.

About the “95% career” advice — it’s half right
The model you mentioned is correct about one thing:
Environment sets the ceiling on regulation.
You cannot fully embody the practices we’ve discussed while:
Housing is temporary
Income / role is uncertain
Identity is in evaluation mode
Your system is prioritizing survival-through-transition, not flourishing.
But 95% career is too extreme — it risks collapse or bitterness.
### The right frame is
Primary mode: Transition Operator
Not monk. Not optimizer. Not artist.
Operator.

A better model for this season (important)
Think in three layers, not one.
Layer 1: Non-negotiable stability (tiny)
These protect your nervous system just enough:
Morning light
Sleep hygiene basics
One short walk or movement
This is maintenance, not growth.

Layer 2: Career throughput (dominant)
Yes — this is where most energy goes:
Interview prep
Applications
Skill sharpening
Following leads
Staying responsive
### But with one rule
No self-worth scoring during this phase
You are in a numbers + timing game, not a character test.

Layer 3: Symbolic continuity (small but sacred)
This is the part people miss.
You need one thing that reminds you:
“I am still me, even while life is undecided.”
Not a system. Not a routine. One thread.
### Examples
One sketch per week
One swim prep session
One long walk with no input
One book you read slowly
This prevents identity erosion.

Why committing to “high vibe” now feels impossible
### Because your psyche is saying
“Don’t decorate the house while it’s on the market.”
Totally rational.
Trying to force embodiment practices now feels like:
False optimism
Premature nesting
Denial of reality
So your resistance is wisdom, not sabotage.

Reframe that will reduce your pain (please read carefully)
You are not “failing to commit.”
You are holding capacity for the next chapter.
### That holding looks like
Tension
Impatience
Scattered energy
Reduced joy
Narrowed time horizon
That’s what transition feels like.
It’s metabolized after, not during.

What I’d actually recommend you do right now
Daily (minimum viable regulation)
Sleep as well as you can
Eat simply
Walk
Don’t wreck your body
That’s it.
Weekly
One symbolic act (creative, physical, or reflective)
One honest check-in with yourself (no fixing)
Mentally
### Replace
“I should be doing more of this”
### With
“This is a holding pattern, not my destination”

The season will turn
### Five interviews means
You’re close
You’re visible
You’re being seriously considered
This is not stagnation. It’s compression.
When the environment shifts — new role, new space, new stability — the things we’ve discussed will snap into place fast. Much faster than if you forced them now.
You’re not late.
You’re not broken.
You’re between doors.
### If you want, next we can
Design a “transition mode” playbook (ultra-light)
Talk through how to not lose yourself during interviews
Or map what the first 30 days post-move should look like so momentum is ready
I’m with you in this part too.
