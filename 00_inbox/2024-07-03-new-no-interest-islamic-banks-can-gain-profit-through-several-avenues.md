# New no-interest (Islamic) banks can gain profit through several avenues…

New no-interest (Islamic) banks can gain profit through several avenues that adhere to Islamic finance principles, which prohibit interest (riba). Here are some common ways:

1. **Profit-sharing contracts (Mudarabah)**: The bank can engage in profit-sharing partnerships with businesses and investors. In Mudarabah, the bank provides capital, and the business/investor provides labor and expertise. Profits generated are shared based on a pre-agreed ratio.

2. **Joint venture (Musharakah)**: Similar to Mudarabah, Musharakah involves joint ventures where profits and losses are shared based on the capital contributions and agreements between the parties.

3. **Trade-based transactions (Murabaha)**: The bank can engage in trade-based transactions where it purchases goods requested by a customer and sells them at a higher price, allowing for profit. This is akin to cost-plus financing.

4. **Lease-based contracts (Ijarah)**: The bank can acquire assets and lease them out to customers for a rental fee. This provides a steady stream of income without interest.

5. **Service-based contracts (Wakalah)**: Acting as an agent, the bank can charge a fee for managing investments or providing financial services to clients.

6. **Islamic bonds (Sukuk)**: Issuing Sukuk allows the bank to raise funds by selling certificates of ownership in tangible assets, and investors receive a share of the profits generated by those assets.

7. **Investment in ethical sectors**: Focusing on sectors and businesses that align with Islamic principles (halal industries) such as ethical finance, halal food, healthcare, etc., can provide sustainable sources of income.

8. **Fees and charges**: The bank can charge fees for various services it provides, such as account management, transaction processing, advisory services, etc.

By focusing on these Islamic finance principles and structures, new no-interest Muslim banks can attract customers who seek financial services compliant with Shariah law while also ensuring sustainable profitability.
