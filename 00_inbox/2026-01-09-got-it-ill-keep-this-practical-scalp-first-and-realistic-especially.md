Persistent dandruff

Good question — and you’re right to think in “eradication vs maintenance” terms.
I’ll be very direct and realistic here, because true eradication is harder than people think, especially with scalp yeast.
First, an important truth (so expectations are correct):
You cannot permanently eradicate Malassezia/yeast from the scalp.
It is part of normal skin flora.
What is possible is deep suppression + long remission so symptoms disappear for months.
So when we talk “eradication-level,” we really mean:
periodic interventions that knock yeast way down and reset the scalp environment.
Below are the strongest non-daily, periodic tools beyond what you already listed.

1. Contact-time antifungal “reset” (monthly or flare-only)
This is stronger than normal shampooing.
How
Use ketoconazole or selenium sulfide
Apply to dry scalp before shower
Let sit 10 minutes (not 3–5)
Rinse ? gentle shampoo ? condition
Frequency
Once every 3–4 weeks
Or during bad flares only
Why it works
Yeast die-off is contact-time dependent
Dry scalp application penetrates biofilm better
?? Do NOT do this weekly — it will wreck your barrier.

2. Biofilm disruption (this is a big one)
Yeast protect themselves with biofilms. If you don’t break those, they rebound.
Periodic acid or enzyme disruption (every 2–4 weeks)
Options
Apple cider vinegar rinse
1 part ACV : 5–7 parts water
Apply to scalp 2–3 minutes, rinse
Salicylic acid scalp exfoliant (pre-shampoo)
Example: The INKEY List Salicylic Acid Scalp Treatment
Why
Lowers pH
Loosens flakes
Exposes yeast to antifungals
### ? Best sequence
Exfoliant ? antifungal shampoo ? deep condition

3. Sulfur-based scalp treatments (very underused)
### Sulfur is
Antifungal
Keratolytic
Anti-inflammatory
Options
De La Cruz Sulfur Ointment (thin scalp layer)
Sulfur + salicylic soaps (short contact)

Every 3–4 weeks or during flares

4. Short-course antifungal leave-on (targeted use)
This is closer to “medical” but still topical.
Examples
Ketoconazole foam/cream (off-label scalp use)
Ciclopirox solution (if prescribed)
How
Apply thinly to affected scalp areas
Leave on overnight
Use 3–5 nights, then stop
Why
Continuous exposure kills yeast more effectively than rinse-off shampoos
?? Not for constant use.

5. Sebum control periods (starving the yeast)
Yeast feed on fatty acids in sebum.
7–14 day “low-lipid scalp phase” (1–2x/year)
During this phase
No heavy conditioners touching scalp
No butters or rich oils
### Only
Jojoba (sparingly)
## Mct C8/C10
Wash 2x/week
Why
Dramatically reduces yeast food supply
Often resets chronic flaking cycles

6. Heat + sweat management (often overlooked)
Sweat + occlusion = yeast paradise.
Periodically
Avoid tight headwear for 1–2 weeks
Dry scalp fully after washing
Avoid sleeping with damp hair
This alone fixes some “mystery” recurrences.

7. Gut-side “eradication assist” (optional but real)
Not medical treatment — but impactful for some.
Short targeted phases
Caprylic acid (from MCT C8)
Oregano oil (very short course)
Saccharomyces boulardii (yeast that crowds out candida)
Why
Reduces gut yeast load ? less systemic signaling ? calmer skin
?? Go slow. Die-off reactions can happen.

What NOT to chase (saves you time)
? Daily ACV
? Daily tea tree
? Bleach, hydrogen peroxide, baking soda
? Over-washing “until clean”
These damage barrier and worsen rebound.
