# It sounds like you’re putting in the work, but the algorithm may not…

It sounds like you’re putting in the work, but the algorithm may not be fully favoring your content yet. To break through, you need a strategy that plays into TikTok’s strengths—engagement, trends, consistency, and community-building. Here’s a super solid game plan tailored for your music production content:
1. Nail Your First Few Seconds
Hook: The first 3-5 seconds need to grab attention. For a beat visualizer, this could mean a striking image or unexpected sound drop that makes people want to stick around.
Example: Start with a super catchy portion of the beat that sounds unique or different, or even tease a little “preview” of the full track.
Text Overlay: Use text overlays like “Can you guess what genre this is?” or “This beat took me X hours to make!” to engage curiosity.
2. Leverage Trending Sounds & Hashtags
Trending Sounds: Even though you’re posting original content, using trending audio in the background (as a secondary sound or mixed subtly) can help boost visibility. Just make sure it doesn’t overshadow your own music.
Hashtags: Use a mix of popular and niche hashtags. Examples:
General music: #ProducerLife #MusicProduction #BeatMaking #Beatmaker #ProducerCommunity
Niche/Targeted: #ProducerTikTok #StudioVibes #MakingBeats #MusicIsLife #ProducerJourney
Trending/Challenge Hashtags: Search the “Discover” page for viral hashtags related to music production.
3. Post Consistently & Time Your Posts Right
Consistency: The TikTok algorithm loves consistency. Aim to post at least 3-5 times a week. This doesn’t mean sacrificing quality for quantity, but rather staying visible and showing up regularly.
Timing: Post when your audience is most active. Generally, evenings (6-9 PM) and weekends work well, but check your TikTok analytics (once you have a Pro Account) to see when your followers are most active.
4. Engage With Comments & Respond Quickly
TikTok thrives on interaction. If someone comments on your video, reply as soon as possible. Engage with people who comment on your beats, whether they’re complimenting your work or asking questions. This interaction boosts the chances of your video being pushed out.
If a comment is really engaging, make a follow-up video addressing it—“Someone asked me how I made this bass sound, let’s break it down.”
5. Collaborations & Duets
Collaborating with other creators or using TikTok’s Duet feature is a great way to tap into another creator’s audience. You could duet with a vocalist or rapper using one of your beats, or even react to a popular beat challenge.
Try finding smaller producers with similar followings and cross-promote each other’s content, getting in front of their audience.
6. Behind-the-Scenes & Process Videos
People love seeing how things are made. Post content where you explain your beat-making process or show your workspace and gear.
Example: "I just made this beat using ONLY these 3 plugins..." or "How I went from a basic loop to a finished track."
7. Engage with the Music Production Community
Duet/Reactions: React to or remix other popular producer’s beats. This builds community and draws in viewers who are following that producer.
Challenges/Remixes: Participate in TikTok challenges or remix a beat from a trending TikTok meme.
8. Storytelling / Educational Content
Add value beyond just posting beats. People want to learn about the craft. Try educating your audience about music production, whether through tutorials or tips.
For example: “How to create a hard-hitting 808” or “3 things I wish I knew when starting as a producer.”
9. Create a Signature Series
Create a regular series around a specific type of beat or concept (e.g., “Weekly Chill Beats” or “Beat Breakdown Fridays”). Consistent series help viewers know when to expect new content and build anticipation.
10. Optimize Your Profile
Make sure your TikTok bio clearly communicates who you are and what you do. You should mention that you make beats or share music production content. This way, if people check your profile, they’ll instantly know what to expect from your posts.
Include a link to your music portfolio or beat store in your bio for easy access.
11. Use the Right Visuals
For a beat visualizer, ensure it’s eye-catching and matches the vibe of the music. Use cool animation or effects that flow well with the beat. The visual aspect is key for music producers because it makes your beat more engaging for non-musicians too.
Pro Tip: Try using TikTok’s built-in effects creatively—experiment with things like color grading or glitch effects that sync to the music.
12. Analyze & Adapt
Check which videos are performing better in terms of engagement, views, and shares. TikTok gives you data on what works. Double down on those types of content and iterate based on feedback.
Try testing different types of content (e.g., quick tutorials vs. full beats vs. remixing others' tracks) and see what resonates with your audience.
13. Engage With the Larger Music Producer Community
Follow other producers on TikTok, like and comment on their content, and become an active member of the music production community. If you engage with others' content, they may reciprocate and promote your work.
Also, try to participate in music-related TikTok groups or online communities where producers share tips, challenges, or collaborations.

### Example Weekly Posting Plan
Monday: Post a beat video with a catchy visualizer and trending sound. Include an engaging text prompt like "Guess the genre!"
Wednesday: Post a “How I made this beat” video, breaking down your production process with text overlays and captions.
Friday: Share a behind-the-scenes video of your setup or workflow. Maybe a quick tip on improving beat sound quality or creating unique melodies.
Sunday: Participate in a challenge or duet with another creator.
By leveraging these strategies consistently and engaging with your audience, you’ll increase your visibility and start building a dedicated following on TikTok. Let me know if you need any more tips on specific aspects like content creation or editing!
