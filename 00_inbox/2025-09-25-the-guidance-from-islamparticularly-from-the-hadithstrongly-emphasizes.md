# The guidance from Islam—particularly from the Hadith—strongly emphasizes…

The guidance from Islam—particularly from the Hadith—strongly emphasizes that a Muslim man, especially one "building a foundation," must be extremely selective about his immediate circle (his Khalil or close friends). This circle is considered the most desirable route toward acquiring an asset in the Hereafter.
The core principle, as stated by the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), is:
“A man is upon the religion of his best friend, so let one of you look at whom he befriends.” (Sunan al-Tirmidhi and Abu Dawud)

Specified Individuals for the Immediate Circle

For a man "building a foundation," the most desirable individuals to surround himself with are those who possess qualities that actively promote righteousness, piety (Taqwa), and success in the Akhirah(Hereafter).

The Next Route Towards Acquiring an Asset

The "asset" being referred to, in the most desirable Islamic context, is Paradise (Jannah) and Allah's Shadeon the Day of Judgment. The "route" is to cultivate a circle of friends whose sole purpose for their friendship is "for the sake of Allah."

Specific Guidance from Hadith

The route towards this asset is beautifully illustrated in the following narration:
Mutual Love for Allah's Sake: The Prophet (PBUH) mentioned among the seven types of people Allah will shade on the Day when there is no shade but His, are:
“...two men who loved one another for the sake of Allah, meeting for His sake and parting for His sake.” (Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim)

The Reward: Loving and keeping company with these righteous individuals for this divine purpose earns the highest reward.
Verily, Allah will say on the Day of Resurrection: 'Where are those who loved each other for the sake of My majesty? Today, I will shelter them in My shade on a day when there is no shade but Mine.'” (Sahih Muslim)

Therefore, the immediate circle for a man building his foundation should be comprised of these "Root People"—those whose commitment to Allah and the Sunnah is the unbreakable root that supports and nourishes his own life.

Individuals to Avoid (The Anti-Assets)

The Hadith and Quran also warn against having close ties with those who will negatively influence your faith:
The Follower of Desire: Avoid those "whose heart We have made heedless of Our remembrance, and who follows his desire and whose affair is ever [in] neglect." (Qur'an 18:28)
The Wrongdoer: The Qur'an mentions the regret of the one who says on the Day of Judgment, "Oh! Woe to me! Would that I had never taken so-and-so as a friend! He indeed led me astray from the Reminder (the Qur'an)." (Qur'an 25:27-29)
The Fool/Miser/Liar/Wrongdoer: Classical Islamic advice includes avoiding the fool (who intends to benefit but harms), the miser (who withholds support), and the liar (who is like a mirage).
