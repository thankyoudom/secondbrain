# Yeah—you’re talking about Shabbat (the Jewish day of rest). And you’re…

Yeah—you’re talking about Shabbat (the Jewish day of rest). And you’re not crazy for thinking “a whole day without tech sounds impossible.” Most modern Jews wrestle with this exact thing.
Let’s unpack it philosophically, not dogmatically.

The core idea (not rules yet)
Shabbat isn’t about “rest” like sleeping in or recovering from burnout.
It’s about stopping creation and control.
In Jewish philosophy, the world is split into:
Being (existing, experiencing, enjoying)
Doing (producing, optimizing, changing, extracting)
Shabbat is a weekly rebellion against productivity culture—before capitalism, before smartphones, before hustle culture even had a name.
“You are not what you produce.”
That’s the thesis.

Why no tech specifically?
The Torah doesn’t mention phones (obviously). It forbids melacha—often mistranslated as “work,” but more accurately:
Intentional acts that change the world or assert control over it
Modern rabbis map this to tech because tech is:
Creation (writing, posting, editing)
Control (searching, navigating, managing)
Measurement (analytics, likes, stats, time)
Optimization (scrolling for better content, better info)
Reddit, Twitter, email, even “casual browsing” all fall into this bucket.
Not because they’re evil—but because they keep your brain in extract mode.

So yes: traditionally
No Reddit. No surfing. No doomscrolling.
Not because fun is banned, but because:
You’re consuming infinite information
Comparing yourself
Optimizing identity
Being pulled outward instead of inward
Shabbat is finite by design.

The real benefit (not the Instagram version)
### People think Shabbat is about
? relaxation
? self-care
? detox
It’s actually about identity repair.
After ~24 hours of no tech, people report:
Reduced anxiety (even if boredom spikes first)
Creativity returning without forcing
A strange sense of dignity
Time feeling thicker
Desire replacing compulsion
### Your brain stops asking
“What’s next?”
### And starts asking

That’s rare. And powerful.

Why it feels unfathomable to you (important)
### You’re
A builder
A hybrid thinker
Creative + technical
Identity tied to output, mastery, and momentum
So a day with no leverage feels like death.
Shabbat directly confronts that fear and says:
“If everything stopped… you’d still be whole.”
That’s uncomfortable—but also stabilizing long-term.
