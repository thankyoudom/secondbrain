# Absolutely. Here's a **deep-dive technical checklist** for industry…

Absolutely. Here's a **deep-dive technical checklist** for industry-standard mixing practices across **all genres**, tailored especially for producers aiming to deliver **mixes ready for mastering**. Whether you're producing lofi, trap, pop, or house, this applies across the board.

---

# **Industry-Standard Mixing Checklist (Pre-Mastering Delivery)**

## **1. Session Preparation**
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz or 48kHz (higher OK, but maintain consistency across session).
- **Bit Depth**: 24-bit is standard. Avoid 16-bit at this stage.
- **Headroom**: Leave **-6dB to -3dB peak** on the master bus.
- **No Master Effects**: Remove limiters, stereo enhancers, or master EQ (unless mix-dependent).
- **Bounce Format**: WAV or AIFF (lossless, 24-bit, 44.1kHz+).
- **Stem Exports** (if requested): Render grouped tracks (Drums, Bass, Melody, Vocals, FX, etc.).

---

## **2. Frequency Balance (EQ Standards)**

### **General Frequency Targets**
| Instrument | Frequency Range | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|-------|
| Kick Drum | 50–100 Hz | Boost punch at 60-80Hz, cut muddiness at 250Hz |
| Bass | 60–120 Hz | Low pass around 200–300Hz if necessary |
| Snare | 150–250 Hz body, 2–5kHz snap | Cut boxiness at 400–600Hz |
| Hi-Hats | 5–10kHz | High-pass below 3kHz, avoid harshness above 12kHz |
| Vocals | 100Hz–15kHz | De-ess around 5–8kHz, remove mud at 250–400Hz |
| Pads/Keys | 200Hz–10kHz | Watch for clutter in 300–700Hz range |

### **EQ Practices**
- Use **high-pass filters** on most non-bass instruments to clear subspace.
- Cut rather than boost. Subtractive EQ preserves headroom and realism.
- Avoid excessive boosts over 6dB.
- Watch for **frequency masking** (e.g., kick vs. bass clashing in low end).

---

## **3. Dynamics & Compression**

### **Compression Targets**
| Element | Target Ratio | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------|
| Vocals | 2:1 to 4:1 | Use soft knee, fast attack/release to tame peaks |
| Drums | 4:1 to 8:1 | Parallel compression adds punch without losing transients |
| Bass | 3:1 to 5:1 | Smooth sustain, avoid distortion in lows |
| Mix Bus | 1.5:1 to 2:1 | Gentle glue with slow attack and fast release |

### **Best Practices**
- Use **parallel compression** for drums and vocals to preserve dynamics.
- Avoid **over-compressing**: it kills movement and depth.
- Check with meters: **aim for 3–6dB of gain reduction**, not more unless stylistic.

---

## **4. Stereo Imaging**

### **Stereo Field Control**
- Keep **low-end mono** (kick, bass below ~150Hz).
- Pan elements to create space (e.g., hi-hats L20, guitars R30).
- Use **mid/side EQ** for clarity (boost sides above 10kHz, cut mids at 400Hz).
- Use **stereo meters** (like Flux Stereo Tool or Voxengo Span) to check phase and width.

---

## **5. Reverb & Space**

### **Reverb Use Cases**
- Use **short reverbs** for drums (room or plate).
- **Long tails** (hall, lush) for pads, vocals, ambient FX.
- Use **pre-delay** (20–40ms) to maintain clarity.
- **High-pass and low-pass** your reverb returns to avoid muddy build-up.

---

## **6. Panning Guidelines**

| Element | Typical Panning |
|---------|-----------------|
| Kick, Snare, Bass | Center |
| Hi-Hats | 10–40% L/R |
| Guitars | 30–60% L/R |
| Background Vocals | 20–80% L/R |
| FX | Automate across field or wide |

- Check in **mono** to ensure no key elements vanish.
- Use **LCR mixing** (Left, Center, Right) to create clarity.

---

## **7. Automation**

- Automate **volume** for dynamics, build tension & release.
- Automate **reverb/delay sends** to highlight phrases or drops.
- Automate **EQ or filter sweeps** for movement.

Automation brings the track to life and is crucial in **professional-sounding mixes**.

---

## **8. Monitoring & Reference**

### **Monitoring Standards**
- Mix at 75–85dB SPL (low volume is best for long sessions).
- Use **multiple listening environments**: monitors, headphones, phone, car.
- Check **mono compatibility**.

### **Use Reference Tracks**
- Load 1–2 professionally mastered tracks into your DAW.
- Match tonal balance, perceived loudness, and stereo image.

---

## **9. Metering Tools (Free Options)**
- **Youlean Loudness Meter** – LUFS, True Peak
- **SPAN by Voxengo** – Frequency analyzer, stereo imaging
- **TDR Nova** – Dynamic EQ (visual reference)

---

## **10. Mastering Engineer Checklist (What They Expect From You)**
- **-6dB headroom** (no brickwall limiter on master).
- **24-bit WAV or AIFF**, 44.1kHz or 48kHz.
- **Clean gain staging** – no clipping anywhere in the mix.
- **Clearly named track versions** (e.g., TrackName_Mix_01.wav).
- **Optional**: provide reference tracks or mastering notes (vibe, platform, preferences).
