# LOUDNESS

## Loudness
Got it! It sounds like you're following a workflow where you export your video from CapCut, then extract the audio within TikTok, which could be introducing some of the loss in quality and volume. To get the cleanest and loudest track possible from FL Studio all the way to TikTok via CapCut, you'll want to address a few key points in each step of the process.
Here’s a detailed approach based on insights from various producers on Reddit and other forums:

1. Optimize Your FL Studio Output
The first step is making sure your track is as ready as possible before it even hits CapCut.
Master to Target Loudness: As mentioned, aim for an RMS of -10 to -6 dB, not just a peak limit of -1 dB. If your track’s RMS is too low, it won’t translate well through TikTok's compression.
### Mastering Chain
Use a limiter (like Fruity Limiter or third-party plugins like iZotope Ozone) to raise the overall loudness, but make sure it doesn’t squash the dynamics.
A multiband compressor can also help balance out the mix, especially if your track has a lot of bass or high-frequency content that might get compressed too much.
### Export Settings
Export your track as a WAV file (not MP3) with the following settings:
44.1 kHz sample rate (standard for most platforms)
24-bit depth for higher resolution (though 16-bit can also work)
Don’t use any dithering if you’re staying in 24-bit. Use no compression or lossless codecs like WAV to preserve audio quality.

2. CapCut Workflow for Audio
When editing in CapCut, the video editing app may still impact your audio quality.
Import WAVs into CapCut: Try to import the full, lossless WAV file directly into CapCut instead of a video file. This avoids any further compression when the audio is extracted within TikTok.
Avoid CapCut’s Export Compression: When exporting from CapCut, choose highest-quality export settings. Some users report that exporting videos at the highest resolution (1080p or 4K) also helps preserve the audio quality. Avoid exporting at lower resolutions (like 720p) because it can lead to more noticeable audio degradation.

3. TikTok Audio Extraction
Once you extract the audio in TikTok, the platform’s automatic compression might still degrade it a bit, but there are a few things you can do to minimize this:
Use a Clean Export from CapCut: Don’t extract audio from any low-quality video clips in TikTok. If you’re pulling the audio directly from a video that was compressed in CapCut, it may already sound flat and lacking in volume. Instead, export your clean WAV (or high-quality MP4) from CapCut, upload it as a separate sound to TikTok, and sync it with your video.
Upload as a Direct Audio File: Instead of extracting the audio in TikTok, you can upload the audio file to TikTok directly (using TikTok's sound upload feature). This bypasses the compression that happens when pulling the audio from a video.

4. Handling TikTok’s Compression
TikTok’s compression is inevitable, but here are a few tips to maximize your loudness without sacrificing too much quality:
Maximize Loudness Pre-TikTok: Since TikTok lowers the volume, make sure your track is already loud but controlled. Aim for peak levels near -1 dB and an RMS of -6 to -8 dB. Test your track in different environments (headphones, car speakers, etc.) to make sure it doesn’t sound too squashed.
Avoid Using TikTok’s Automatic Audio Enhancements: Turn off any automatic enhancements or effects TikTok might apply to your audio once it’s uploaded. These can further degrade the audio quality and volume.
Test Multiple Uploads: Upload different versions of your track to see how they behave in the app. For example, test one that’s slightly less limited and another that’s more “loudness-maximized.” This will help you see how much room you have before TikTok starts to apply aggressive compression.

5. Additional Tools and Plugins
Some users on forums also recommend the following plugins and tools for better volume control and clean output:
iZotope Ozone: This is a powerful mastering suite with a lot of preset options and dynamic control that can help you make the track loud without compromising on clarity. Many producers recommend it for ensuring tracks stay punchy on platforms like TikTok.
LoudMax Limiter: This is a free and transparent limiter that’s known for its ability to increase loudness without introducing harsh distortion.
Dynamic EQ: Sometimes using a dynamic EQ to tame certain frequency ranges (like bass or harsh highs) can help prevent the track from triggering aggressive compression in TikTok.

### Final Workflow Recap
FL Studio: Master the track to about -6 dB RMS with no clipping, use a limiter, and export as 24-bit WAV (44.1 kHz).
CapCut: Import your WAV file directly and export at the highest resolution and quality settings.
TikTok: Upload the track as a direct audio file (not extracted from a video) to preserve the best possible quality.

With this workflow, you should be able to get your track sounding as good as possible across the different stages. Keep tweaking and testing until you find the sweet spot where the volume and quality stay consistent across CapCut and TikTok!
