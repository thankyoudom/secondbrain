# Below is a structured, clear explanation that ties together Marcus…

Below is a structured, clear explanation that ties together Marcus Aurelius, “edgy vs. light” people, Jung’s Red Book, the Shadow, and how thought-shaped reality interacts with social media influence.

Marcus Aurelius: Did He Comment on “Edgy vs. Light” People?
Marcus Aurelius didn’t use modern language like “edgy,” but he absolutely did discuss contrasts in character: destructive vs. noble, agitated vs. calm, vicious vs. virtuous.
1. His core view:
People who behave negatively (angry, petty, dramatic, manipulative) do so because they lack understanding.
### In Meditations, he says repeatedly
People act out of ignorance, not true malice.
Your job is to maintain your own character, not imitate theirs.
The mind becomes dyed with the color of its thoughts.
To him, “edgy” people—those drawn to chaos, anger, or rebellion—would simply be untrained minds, driven by impulses.
“Light” people—calm, fair, measured—would be those living closer to logos, reason, virtue.
2. Aurelius believed solitude strengthens character
Not isolation, but inner stillness.
You encounter all kinds of people, but your peace comes from the inner citadel you build.
3. Did he see strength in himself?
Absolutely.
### He constantly talks about
Perseverance
Self-discipline
Enduring negativity without becoming negative
This was his definition of strength—not aggression, but self-command.

Carl Jung: The Red Book and the Shadow
Jung dealt with the inner world more directly than Marcus.
What is the Shadow?
The parts of yourself you reject
Instincts, desires, fears, anger
Aspects of yourself that you don’t admit you have
Jung believed that ignoring the shadow makes it stronger.
Integrating it makes you whole.
How is this connected to Marcus?
Marcus wants you to maintain rational virtue despite negativity.
Jung wants you to explore and integrate your inner darkness so it doesn’t control you.
### Together, they form a full picture
Stoicism: Strength through self-control
Jungian psychology: Strength through self-knowledge

Interacting with “Edgy Folks”: What Do These Thinkers Suggest?
### Marcus Aurelius’ approach
Keep your mind your own.
Don’t let others pull you out of your virtues.
Understand them, forgive them, but don’t absorb them.
### Jung’s approach
Ask what part of you is reacting to them.
Sometimes your attraction or discomfort with “edgy” people reflects something in your own shadow.
They can teach you about parts of yourself you haven’t integrated yet.

Do People Around You Shape Your Reality?
Yes — but not in a mystical way.
1. Cognitive psychology:
Your thoughts shape your perception.
Your environment shapes your thoughts.
Therefore, your environment indirectly shapes your reality.
2. Social media and the “edgy influence”:
### Even virtual people you follow can
shift your mood
influence your worldview
amplify fears or anger
reinforce traits within your shadow
normalize chaos or negativity
In a sense, social media is a constant shadow projection machine.
3. Marcus’s version:
“The soul becomes dyed with the color of its thoughts.”
If your thoughts are influenced by others’ negativity, you are letting them dye you.

So… Are “Edgy” People Around You Shaping Your Reality?
Yes, if you allow them into your mental space.
Both ancient philosophy and modern psychology agree:
What you repeatedly see shapes what you repeatedly think.
What you repeatedly think shapes who you become.
Who you become shapes the world you perceive.
### Marcus Aurelius would tell you
Guard your mind like a fortress.
### Carl Jung would tell you
Understand why those people affect you—something in you responds to it.
