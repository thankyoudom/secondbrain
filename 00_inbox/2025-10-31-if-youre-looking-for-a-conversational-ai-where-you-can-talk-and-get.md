# If you’re looking for a conversational-AI where you can talk and get…

If you’re looking for a conversational-AI where you can talk and get a transcript of that conversation, plus some AI features around it — yes: there are good options. Here are three that lean toward “talk-to-AI + transcription” (vs just passive audio-to-text). I’ll list pros/cons for each so you can pick what fits your use-case best.

1. Gemini (“Live Voice Mode”) from Google LLC
What it is: An AI chatbot that supports voice conversations (you speak, it replies) and is built into a mobile app. The “Live voice chat mode” was rolled out for Android (and later iPhone) allowing spoken interaction. (The Verge)
### Pros
Very conversational: you don’t just record yourself, you engage in a back-and-forth with the AI.
Free (at least the voice feature was made free on Android) for the base functionality. (The Verge)
Useful for brainstorming, asking questions, getting immediate spoken responses.
### Cons
It may not be optimized primarily for transcription of your full spoken conversation (i.e., you speak, AI replies — but if your need is “capture everything I say + save text” it might not be as strong).
Language / region limitations (initially English for voice). (The Verge)
Because it’s more of a chat assistant, privacy/data usage matters (your voice data is sent to cloud).
Best if: You want a conversational partner (ask-and-reply) and also would like transcripts or records of the conversation (if it allows export).
Free tier: Yes (voice mode free on Android).
Watch out for: If your goal is just capturing your own side of a multi-person conversation (e.g., meeting or interview), this may not be the perfect fit.

2. Voiser (Speech-to-Text / Voice to Text App)
What it is: A mobile app (iOS) that lets you talk or record and then converts voice into text (live or from recordings) with AI support. (Apple)
### Pros
Focuses on converting your voice/recorded speech into text (i.e., transcription) in real time. (Apple)
Supports multiple languages (over 140 languages claimed). (Apple)
Good if you want voice ? text to review or save.
### Cons
It’s more “record or talk and get text” rather than deep conversational back-and-forth AI. So less chat-AI and more transcription-AI.
Free version may have limitations (minutes, features) though some “free real-time” claim exists. (Apple)
If you want AI replies or conversational AI, this doesn’t cover that as strongly.
Best if: You mostly want your speech transcribed (e.g., you talk, app writes) rather than a full talk-with-AI experience.
Free tier: Yes there’s a “free” real-time speech-to-text free/app version claimed.
Watch out for: Accuracy will depend on audio clarity; speaker overlap or ambient noise may reduce quality.

3. StepWrite (Research-Stage Voice & Context System)
What it is: A research/system-paper describing a voice-based AI system that supports more structured conversational voice interactions (hands/eyes-free), generating context-aware text from spoken input. (arXiv)
### Pros
Very modern / advanced: designed for voice interactions where you talk and the system helps you craft longer structured text (emails, documents) by tracking context.
Shows direction of where “conversational + transcription + assist” is going.
### Cons
Not a polished consumer app right now (research/prototype).
Might not have a free-usable product or full features yet for everyone.
Best if: You’re interested in advanced voice-AI and maybe willing to explore something more experimental.
Free tier: N/A or limited (not a full commercial product).
Watch out for: Since it’s research, stability/availability will vary.

My Recommendation Based on Your Need
Since you said “more conversational” (you want to talk and get a transcription from that conversation), I’d suggest going for something like Gemini OR pairing a conversational AI with a good transcription tool.
If your main goal: Have a chat with AI and you want audio + maybe transcript: Gemini might be the best.
If your main goal: Speak your thoughts or ideas and get them transcribed into text (less about AI replying): Voiseris better.
If you want something that will do both (talk + AI replies + full high-quality transcript) and you’re okay with trying things out, keep an eye on emerging tools (like StepWrite etc).
