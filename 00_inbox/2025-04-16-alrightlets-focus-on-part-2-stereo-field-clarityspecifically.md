# Alright—let’s focus on **Part 2: Stereo Field & Clarity**—specifically…

Alright—let’s focus on **Part 2: Stereo Field & Clarity**—specifically how to tighten your mix to get closer to that Ivy Lab level of polish and depth. Here's a breakdown with concrete **suggestions for EQ, panning, spatial effects, and dynamic tools**:

---

### **A. Stereo Field Suggestions**
Ivy Lab’s mixes are wide, clean, and detailed. Here’s how to work toward that:

#### 1. **Pan with Intent**
- **Drums**:
- Kick + snare = center
- Hi-hats = 20–40% L/R (or automate them wider in fills for variation)
- Percs = experiment with 50–75% L/R — think call/response
- **Melodic elements**:
- Pad or sample textures = push wide (use stereo imaging plugins or record in stereo)
- Leave **space in the center** for vocals/bass/drums to breathe

#### 2. **Avoid Phase Issues**
- Use **SPAN** or **Ozone Imager** to check for mono compatibility
- Use **mid/side EQ** to clean out muddy mids and add sparkle on the sides

---

### **B. Clarity & Frequency Balance**

#### 1. **EQ Carving**
- **Kick**: Boost 40–60Hz, cut 300–400Hz (mud zone)
- **Bass**: Cut a small notch at the kick’s sweet spot to avoid fighting (around 50Hz usually)
- **Snares**: Add presence at 1–3kHz, remove harshness around 6–8kHz if needed
- **Pads**: Low-cut around 150–200Hz to make room for bass
- Use **TDR Nova** to dynamically control clashing frequencies in overlapping sounds

#### 2. **Reference the Pros**
- Import a clean Ivy Lab or Monte Booker instrumental into your DAW
- Level-match it to your beat and toggle between yours and theirs to see what’s missing
- Ask: *“Where’s their weight? Their sparkle? How open is the stereo field?”*

---

### **C. Transient Clarity**
Ivy Lab’s drums hit crisply. Here’s how to push yours closer:

- Use a **transient shaper** (like Bittersweet v3 or Kilohearts Transient Shaper)
- Boost attack on snare/kick
- Soften sustain on pads or reverb tails to reduce muddiness

- Layer drum samples for punch:
- Example: Use a clean dry kick for punch, layer a roomy or distorted one behind it for character
- Tune snares to the key of the beat for more cohesion

---

### **D. Space & Reverb**
Ivy Lab uses space like an instrument:

- **Valhalla Supermassive**:
- Send pads or melodic textures into a lush reverb or delay tail
- Automate the mix/delay time for movement
- **Reverb EQ**: Use an EQ on your reverb return channel to **low-cut below 300Hz** and **high-cut above 8kHz** — this keeps your reverb from muddying the mix
- **Short reverb for snare**, **longer modulated verbs for textures**

---

### **E. Loudness & Glue**
- Add **bus compression** (SN01-G or TDR Kotelnikov) on your drum bus to glue the elements
- Light **saturation** (IVGI or Saturation Knob) on your master or drums for warmth and loudness without killing headroom
- Keep LUFS between **-12 and -8** for digital streaming, use **Youlean Meter** to check
