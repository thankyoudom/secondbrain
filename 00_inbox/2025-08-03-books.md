# books

Trump is definitely an interesting figure, especially when it comes to communication. His style is very direct, often polarizing, and has made him a unique force in American politics. As for his recommendations on books and his influences, there’s actually a fair amount of information available about both.
### Books Recommended by Trump
Trump has mentioned a few books in interviews, speeches, and on his social media accounts that have either influenced his thinking or that he recommends:
"The Art of the Deal" by Donald Trump – This is probably the most famous book he's associated with. It’s part memoir, part business advice, and lays out his approach to deal-making and life. He often speaks about it as a defining piece of his success.
"The Bible" – Trump has frequently cited the Bible as a key influence in his life, though he doesn't always go into great detail about specific verses or how it shapes his worldview. He’s also mentioned how important it is to him as a source of strength.
"The Power of Positive Thinking" by Norman Vincent Peale – This book is one that Trump has referenced many times. Peale, a famous preacher, was also a mentor to Trump’s family. The book focuses on the power of optimism and positive mental attitude, something Trump seems to embrace in his approach to challenges.
"Think Big and Kick Ass in Business and Life" by Donald Trump & Bill Zanker – This is another self-help/business book where Trump shares his philosophy on business, success, and personal development.
"How to Win Friends and Influence People" by Dale Carnegie – Trump has often praised Carnegie's classic on people skills and relationships. It’s a book that teaches emotional intelligence and how to connect with others, which is key in both business and politics.
### Influences on Trump’s Success
While Trump doesn't always go into great detail about specific mentors or figures in his life, there are a few key influences that come up regularly:
Fred Trump (His Father) – One of the most significant influences in Trump’s life is his father, Fred Trump, who was a real estate mogul. Donald often credits Fred for instilling in him a strong work ethic and business acumen, especially in the real estate world.
Norman Vincent Peale – Peale was a well-known pastor and author who promoted the power of positive thinking. Trump’s family was closely tied to him, and Peale’s philosophy had a big impact on Trump’s outlook on life. Trump has said that Peale’s influence made him more optimistic, resilient, and focused on the positive side of things.
Roy Cohn – Cohn was a lawyer who became infamous for his aggressive tactics, most notably as the chief counsel for Joseph McCarthy during the Red Scare. Trump worked with Cohn early in his career and has described him as a mentor. Cohn taught him how to be tough in business, fight back against critics, and navigate the legal and media landscape.
Warren Buffett – Although they’re often seen as rivals, Trump has occasionally spoken about his respect for Warren Buffett. He’s acknowledged Buffett’s success in investing and business, even if they don’t share the same methods. There’s a sense that Trump admires Buffett’s long-term vision and approach to business, though they’ve had their differences.
Andrew Carnegie & John D. Rockefeller – Trump has spoken about the great industrialists of the Gilded Age, particularly Andrew Carnegie and John D. Rockefeller. These men were titans of industry and helped shape America’s economic landscape in ways that Trump has cited as influential in understanding how to build wealth.
### In Summary
Trump’s communication is heavily influenced by his upbringing, his father’s influence, and the mentors he’s had along the way. Books like "The Art of the Deal" and "The Power of Positive Thinking" are key to understanding his mindset. His influences range from his family (particularly Fred Trump) to figures like Norman Vincent Peale and Roy Cohn, who shaped his approach to business and conflict.
