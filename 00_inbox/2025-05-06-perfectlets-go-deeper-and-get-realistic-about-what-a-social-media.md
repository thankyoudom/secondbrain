# Perfect—let’s go deeper and get realistic about what a social media…

Perfect—let’s go deeper and get realistic about what a social media manager would step in and make you do as an independent music producer. This is where it shifts from theory to execution. Here’s what the experience might look like:

PHASE 1: Brand Cleanup & Positioning
### What the manager does
Audit all your accounts and content.
Interview you briefly to understand your vibe, goals, and audience.
### What they make YOU do
Clarify your niche: What kind of music? For who? Are you cinematic, trap, lo-fi, experimental, etc.?
Commit to a brand voice: Cool and mysterious? Energetic and funny? Industry pro?
### Provide key assets
A few high-res photos of yourself or your workspace.
Your logo (if you have one) or approve a new one.
A 1–2 sentence bio they can refine and unify across platforms.
Pick a color palette + font style (or approve one they mock up).

PHASE 2: Content System Setup
### What they do
Build a content calendar based on your goals (beat sales, streaming, collaborations, brand growth).
Plan out 3–5 content pillars you’ll stick to.
Set up templates (graphics, captions, hashtags).
### What they make YOU do
### Create raw content regularly
Record beat-making sessions (1–3 clips a week).
Do 1 talking-to-camera or voiceover per week (explain a trick, share a thought).
Share finished music, demos, or previews.
Share testimonials (DMs, reposts, syncs, placements).
### Be on camera sometimes
Even just showing your hands on the MPC or keys.
Maybe a “producer POV” or “day in the life” short.
If you're shy on camera, they’ll still need some content of you or your process, or they’ll push for faceless formats.

PHASE 3: Posting & Community Engagement
### What they do
Schedule and post content on your behalf (or help you post).
Write strong captions, design thumbnails, and time it right.
Respond to comments, DMs, and start networking on your behalf.
### What they make YOU do
Reply to key DMs and comments yourself, especially from artists, collaborators, and industry folks.
Film follow-up clips if a post pops off (they’ll push you to double down).
Show up for fans/artists—live sessions, Q&As, or giveaways.

PHASE 4: Growth Hacking & Monetization
### What they do
Collaborate with other producers/artists to do co-posts.
Set up beat pack drops, website funnel, or email list for you.
Run ads if you're ready (beat pack promo, sample kit, exclusive drops).
Analyze post data to improve strategy.
### What they make YOU do
Create exclusive content or bonus material (for freebies, kits, giveaways).
Give input or approval for pricing, bundles, or offers.
Possibly start a newsletter or email drip (they might ghostwrite it for you).

Example Weekly Content Flow a Manager Might Set Up
Mon – 15-sec “studio moment” with beat preview
Tue – Quick producer tip carousel
Wed – Drop a 30-sec beat video w/ caption for artists (“Tag someone who needs this”)
Thu – Behind-the-scenes clip (making a sound, plugin tip)
Fri – Release promo or drop tease
Sat/Sun – Repost artists using your beats OR go live for a beat breakdown
