# Achieving that Noah “40” Shebib sound—made iconic through his work…

Achieving that Noah “40” Shebib sound—made iconic through his work with Drake and OVO artists—is about blending minimalism, emotion, and texture into a deep, ambient sonic atmosphere. Below is a full breakdown of how to approach this sound in FL Studio, focusing on melodies and bass, with technical, theoretical, and stylistic considerations.

1. Overall Sonic Characteristics of 40’s Sound
Key Traits
Ambient, underwater/muffled sound
Dark, minimal, moody aesthetic
Space and silence used as musical elements
Deep sub-bass + filtered melodies
Reverb and delay are not effects—they're instruments
Often uses R&B-rooted chord progressions

2. Frequency Ranges & Mixing Approach
Melodies
Occupy 300 Hz – 4 kHz mostly
Use low-pass filters (cut above 3-5 kHz) to soften and round out
Slight boost around 400–700 Hz for warmth
Cut high-end, add noise layers for grit
Bass
Focus on 30–80 Hz for subs
Light saturation to bring out 2nd/3rd harmonics (90–300 Hz)
Don’t be afraid to low-pass at 100 Hz to remove clashing
Vocals (when added)
Sit centered but slightly submerged
40 tends to high-pass vocals and use subtractive EQ around 300–600 Hz for clarity
Panning
Pads and reverb tails: hard left/right
Melodic phrases: mild stereo spread (20–30%)
Bass and drums: center

3. Pre-Master / Resampling Techniques
Resample melodies to audio and pitch down for that washed-out texture
Reverse audio often, then low-pass it
Use RC-20 Retro Color (free alt: Izotope Vinyl, FerricTDS, ChowTape) for noise, wobble, and tone shift
Render the track and slow it down (especially intros/outros)
Bounce reverb tails as samples for textural pads

4. Chord Progressions & Harmonic Roots
Common Progressions
Minor key: Dm, Fm, Bbm are common roots
Extended chords: minor 7ths, 9ths, sus2, maj7
### Examples
i – VI – III – VII (e.g., Fm – Db – Ab – Eb)
ii7 – V7 – Imaj7 (jazz-inspired)
i – iv – v – i (classic R&B form)
Use
Use soft voicings (spread over octaves)
Add inversions and pedal tones to build tension
Avoid bright chord voicings—use darker intervals

5. Melody Techniques
Build melodies using minor pentatonic + blues scale
Use call-and-response phrasing
Layer reversed vocals/melodies subtly
Play a phrase, resample it, stretch it, and replay it reversed or pitched

6. Sub-Bass Design
Sound Design
Simple sine wave sub (use 3x Osc or Vital)
Layer a faint triangle or saw for harmonics
Add EQ bump at 50–60 Hz
Use Sidechain ducking to let the kick breathe
Tips
Don't overcompress—let it move naturally
Use subtle glide/portamento for movement

7. Retaining Harmonics & Color
Tape saturation (free: Chow Tape Model, FerricTDS)
Add subtle noise layers behind melodies (vinyl hiss, amp hum)
Use parallel distortion to enhance harmonics
Don’t over-EQ or over-clean—keep the “dirt”
Avoid stereo-widening the low-mids too much—it ruins warmth

9. Influences & Roots
Genres
90s–2000s R&B (Aaliyah, Sade, Brandy)
Early Trip-Hop (Massive Attack, Portishead)
Neo-Soul and Jazz Harmony
Toronto ambient hip hop scene
Sound design meets minimalism, inspired by Brian Eno & Boards of Canada

10. Workflow Tips to Get Closer to 40’s Sound
Create space—don’t fill every frequency
Play with silence—let melodies breathe
Use one-take melodies, mistakes and all
Automate filters, pitch, reverb sends
Use less drums, and when you do, keep them low and dry
Bounce out stems and re-import them for manipulation
Experiment with resampling entire loops into new melodies
