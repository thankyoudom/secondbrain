# credit journey

I'm really sorry you're facing this kind of financial stress, but there are ways to handle this. Here's a breakdown of what could happen and how to navigate this situation:
### Immediate Steps to Take
### Contact Discover Right Away
Stay proactive: Call Discover as soon as possible. Let them know your situation and explain that you have zero income currently but are actively seeking employment.
Ask for hardship options: Most credit card companies, including Discover, have hardship programs that can help people who are temporarily unable to pay due to job loss, illness, or financial crises.
These programs could include lower payments, temporary suspension of payments, or lower interest rates.
Request a forbearance or deferment: This may allow you to suspend payments temporarily without it impacting your credit score (this depends on their policies and your account status).
### Explore "Debt Management" Plans (DMP)
Non-profit credit counseling services: These organizations can help negotiate lower payments or interest rates with creditors. They will consolidate your debts into one manageable monthly payment.
Credit Counseling: You can work with a certified credit counselor to set up a debt management plan (DMP), but make sure to choose an accredited and reputable agency. The National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC) is a good place to look for certified counselors.
These programs typically involve a small fee, but can also lead to lower interest rates, fewer fees, and help you avoid collections.
### Check for Legal Protections
Financial Hardship Status: Some states have laws protecting consumers during financial hardship. In the U.S., there's the Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) which restricts how creditors can treat you during difficult financial periods.
Bankruptcy Considerations: This is a last-resort option, but depending on the amount of your total debt, bankruptcy (Chapter 7 or 13) may provide relief. However, it impacts your credit long-term, so only consider this if there’s no way out.
### Dealing with the Missed Payment
Late Fees & Reporting: Since they’ve reported a missed payment, it’s important to address it before it gets worse (e.g., multiple missed payments or collections). If you’re honest with Discover and show you're trying to work things out, they may offer options like removing the missed payment from your credit report as part of a repayment plan or hardship agreement.
Impact on Credit: Missing a payment will hurt your credit score, but it's not the end of the world. As long as you don't miss more payments and you take steps to resolve the debt, you can rebuild your credit over time. As you're looking to secure a job in a couple of months, the timing is key, so get in touch with Discover and explain that you're actively working on getting back on track.
What To Do Over the Next Few Months:
### Job Search
Keep pushing forward with your job search, especially since you've been progressing in interviews. Your situation will improve once you start earning again, and it’s critical to keep that momentum going.
### Alternative Income Streams
Even if you don’t have a job now, consider other ways to earn income in the interim. For example:
Gig economy jobs (Uber, DoorDash, freelance work, etc.)
Selling unused items online
Temporary or contract work (even short-term employment might help stabilize things).
### Set a Financial Plan
Once you start earning, set a clear budget that prioritizes paying down your debt and building an emergency fund.
Consider using apps or a simple spreadsheet to track your spending. It’s critical to avoid accumulating more debt while paying down existing balances.
### Monitor Credit
Keep an eye on your credit score. You can check it for free through services like Credit Karma. If you can, try to avoid additional missed payments and pay what you can, even if it’s less than the minimum.
Can You Bounce Back from This?
Absolutely, but it will require some strategic steps:
Short-Term Recovery: Focus on getting a job, negotiating with Discover for relief, and setting up a manageable plan to avoid falling further behind.
Medium-Term Recovery: Once you have income, focus on rebuilding your credit, tackling your debt, and creating a buffer between you and future financial troubles.
Long-Term Recovery: Stay disciplined with your finances, avoid high-interest debt (like credit cards), and consider building an emergency fund to prepare for any future hardships.
You can definitely come back from this. It’s a rough spot, but with the right plan in place and consistent action, you can work through it. Let me know if you'd like more details on any of these steps!
