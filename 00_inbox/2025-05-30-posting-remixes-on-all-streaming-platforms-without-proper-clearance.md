# Posting remixes on all streaming platforms without proper clearance…

Posting remixes on all streaming platforms without proper clearance can still violate copyright laws—even if you don't profit from them. Here’s a breakdown of what you can and can't do, along with alternatives to legally share your remixes and make them available as sounds on social media:

? THE LEGAL BASICS
? What You Can't Do Without Permission:
Upload a remix of a copyrighted song (even non-commercially) to:
Spotify
Apple Music
Amazon Music
Tidal
Most major streaming services
Use copyrighted material (vocals, instrumentals, stems) in your remix without a license
Even if it’s free, platforms can remove your remix due to copyright strikes. Repeated infractions can get your distributor account banned.

? What You Can Do (with conditions)
1. Use Licensed Stems or Acapellas
If you use acapellas or stems from platforms that offer clearance, you're safer. Sites like:
Tracklib
Splice (with royalty-free samples)
Loopmasters
These give you the legal right to distribute and monetize under certain conditions.

2. Use Platforms That Allow Unofficial Remixes
SoundCloud (with caution): You can post remixes, but they might get flagged if you use copyrighted material.
YouTube (with Content ID risk): Can be blocked or monetized by rights holders.
Audius: More lenient, crypto-based streaming site that accepts unofficial remixes.
Bandcamp: Gray area—only safe if you're remixing royalty-free or cleared content.

3. Get Official Remix Rights
This is tough unless you’re commissioned by the artist or label.
### Requires a remix license, which covers
Mechanical rights (reproduction of the composition)
Master rights (use of the original recording)
This is typically negotiated with the label or original artist.

? Using Remixes as Social Media Sounds
OPTION 1: Upload your remix to TikTok or Instagram Reels as a "sound"
You can use a distributor like SoundOn (TikTok) to get sounds on TikTok.
Or upload a video with the remix—others can then use your sound.
?? If you use copyrighted material, it might still get muted.

OPTION 2: Create Original Remixes Using Royalty-Free Vocals
Use public domain or royalty-free vocals (many exist).
Remix those creatively—post them with no copyright risk.
These can become TikTok sounds, Instagram Reels audio, or YouTube Shorts audio.

? ALTERNATIVES TO POSTING ON MAJOR PLATFORMS
