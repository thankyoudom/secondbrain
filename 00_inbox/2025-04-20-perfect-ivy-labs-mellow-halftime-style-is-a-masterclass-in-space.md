# Perfect — Ivy Lab’s mellow halftime style is a masterclass in **space…

Perfect — Ivy Lab’s mellow halftime style is a masterclass in **space, texture, minimalism, and deep sound design**. Here’s a **deep-dive playbook** on how to get closer to that sound — covering everything from sound design, beat structure, free plugins, to frequency guidance.

---

## **I. Key Traits of Ivy Lab’s Mellow Halftime**

### **1. Tempo**
- Typically around **70–74 BPM** (or 140–148 BPM perceived in halftime).
- Swingy, spacious, with micro-groove.

### **2. Texture & Sound Design**
- **Grainy, dusty, sometimes digital textures.**
- Sounds often feel **off-center**, subtly warped or out of tune.
- **Minimal but layered** — each sound is chosen with precision.

---

## **II. Beat Structure Breakdown**

### **Drums**
- **Kick:** Deep but soft — more felt than heard, often low-passed.
- **Snare:** Characterful, tight, mid-forward. Distorted or layered with foley.
- **Hats & Percs:** Placed with intention. Often shuffled or **off-grid** to create swing.

**Pro Tip:** Use FL Studio’s **Shift knob** (in the channel rack) to offset hi-hats slightly behind the beat. This creates that lazy swing Ivy Lab is known for.

### **Structure**
- **Lots of dropouts, switch-ups, and negative space.**
- Rarely 4-bar predictability. Think **1-bar phrasing** with unexpected fills.
- Often:
`Intro ? Texture Rise ? Beat A ? Switch Beat B ? Spacey Bridge ? Outro`

---

## **III. Frequency Playbook (Reference Range)**

| Element       | Frequency Range | Notes |
|----------------|------------------|-------|
| **Kick**        | 50–80 Hz (sub body), 800–1.5kHz (soft transient) | LPF at 120Hz to avoid click |
| **Sub Bass**    | 30–60 Hz (core), avoid crowding 80–120 Hz | Try sine waves or FM |
| **Snare/Clap**  | 150–250 Hz (body), 1.5–4kHz (bite), 5–8kHz (air) | Add noise layer or foley |
| **Hi-Hats**     | 5–10kHz (sizzle), 1–2kHz (body) | Slight LPF for softness |
| **Pads/FX**     | 300Hz–1kHz (body), 2–4kHz (detail), 8–12kHz (sparkle) | Use HPF ~200Hz |
| **Atmosphere**  | Wide spectrum | Automate HP/LP to create movement |

## **IV. Free Plugins for Ivy Lab Sound Design (FL Studio Friendly)**

### **Sound Design / Texture**
- **TAL-NoiseMaker** – Versatile subtractive synth w/ LFO & PWM.
- **Vital** – Wavetable synth like Serum. Perfect for rich, modulated basses & pads.
- **Surge XT** – Deep, modular, perfect for metallic, warped textures.
- **Dexed** – FM synth like the Yamaha DX7. Great for plastic, weird tones.

### **Distortion / Grit / Movement**
- **TDR Molot GE (Free version)** – Character compression.
- **FuzzPlus 3 (Audio Damage)** – Dirty analog fuzz.
- **Krush by Tritik** – Bitcrushing, downsampling — very Ivy Lab.

### **Modulation & FX**
- **Valhalla Supermassive** – Spacey delays, lush tails.
- **TAL Dub II / III** – True dub delay.
- **PanCake 2 (Cableguys)** – LFO-based auto-panning.
- **Fracture (Glitchmachines)** – Micro-glitch FX and chaos.

### **Foley / Organic**
- Use **Splice free pack downloads**, or:
- **record your own foley** (taps, paper, tools) and layer in.
- Stretch with **Fruity Granulizer** for lush atmospheres.

## **V. Sound Design Techniques to Practice**

### **1. Bass Design**
- **Start with a sine or triangle wave.** Add harmonics via wavefolder or saturation.
- Automate **filter cutoff and resonance**.
- Use **FM modulation** in Vital or Dexed for weird harmonics.
- **Modulate pitch or volume subtly** to avoid static loops.

### **2. Pad Textures**
- Layer **field recordings** with subtle reverb and chorus.
- Use **LFOs on filter cutoff and pitch** for warble.
- Try **detuned layers** and stereo widening (but keep mono compatibility).

### **3. Perc Sound Design**
- Layer **digital noise + foley + drum samples**.
- Clip or distort to taste. Ivy Lab snares are rarely clean.
- Use **weird source material**: mouth sounds, clicks, switches.

---

## **VI. Workflow Playbook to Emulate Ivy Lab**

### **Sound Palette**
- Build a palette first: synths, textures, foley, drums.
- Limit yourself to a few elements per beat.

### **Beat Building**
1. Create a **core loop (8 bars max)**.
2. Add **small variations** every 2 bars: dropouts, FX throws, different percs.
3. Automate volume, filter, stereo, and reverb for life.

### **Mix**
- Don’t be afraid of **space** — leave silence.
- Use **LPFs to remove highs** and emphasize mid-forward tones.
- Create depth with **sends for reverb/delay**, but automate them.
