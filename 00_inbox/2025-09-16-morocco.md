# Morocco

If you're looking for Moroccan cities that are not overrun with tourists, offer a high quality of life, and are attractive to younger folks (especially 30 and under) with an interest in tech, innovation, and urban energy—here are a few cities that could strike that "Chicago love" vibe:
1. Casablanca
Why it fits: Casablanca is Morocco's economic and financial hub, similar to how Chicago functions in the U.S. It's a dynamic, fast-paced city with a growing tech scene, diverse population, and plenty of opportunities for young professionals.
Vibe: Casablanca feels cosmopolitan, with modern amenities, business opportunities, and a connection to the global market. There's a mix of old and new, with wide boulevards, cafes, and bustling neighborhoods.
Tech Scene: It’s becoming a tech hub, with startups and incubators like Technopark Casablanca fostering innovation and digital entrepreneurship.
Quality of Life: High, with good healthcare, schools, and infrastructure, but traffic and pollution can be an issue, as it’s a major city.
2. Rabat
Why it fits: As the political capital of Morocco, Rabat has a more relaxed and organized vibe compared to Casablanca, but it's still vibrant and cosmopolitan. It's also safer and cleaner.
Vibe: More laid-back but still modern, with cultural landmarks and a waterfront. The city blends tradition and modernity well, with trendy cafes, cultural centers, and a growing expat community.
Tech Scene: Rabat is seeing growth in tech, especially with startups and educational institutions like Université Mohammed V contributing to an innovative environment.
Quality of Life: Good infrastructure, safety, and cleanliness make it appealing. It’s quieter compared to Casablanca but still urban enough for a young, dynamic crowd.
3. Marrakech (less touristy parts)
Why it fits: Marrakech is one of the most famous cities in Morocco, but if you live in neighborhoods that aren't too close to the touristy spots, you'll find a good balance of modern amenities and historic charm.
Vibe: Unique, with its mix of tradition (medina, souks) and modernity (high-end cafes, tech startups). The weather is warm year-round, which is a plus for many young people looking for a more relaxed environment.
Tech Scene: There’s an emerging tech scene here, with some start-ups and digital nomad communities setting up base in Marrakech.
Quality of Life: Great cost of living and vibrant cultural life. The city can get hot in the summer, though, and the pace is slower than cities like Casablanca.
4. Fes
Why it fits: Fes is less touristy than Marrakech, with a rich cultural heritage, and it’s more affordable. It’s also a city of education, with the historic University of Al Quaraouiyine (the oldest continuously operating degree-granting university in the world).
Vibe: Fes is more traditional, with a distinct Medina full of history. But it’s quieter, which appeals to those who want a slower pace of life with a rich cultural experience.
Tech Scene: It’s not as strong as cities like Casablanca, but the educational institutions here foster a community of young, educated individuals, some of whom are venturing into tech and startups.
Quality of Life: Affordable living with good infrastructure, although it’s quieter compared to the bigger cities.
5. Tangier
Why it fits: Tangier has recently been growing as a business and tech hub due to its proximity to Europe and its modern infrastructure.
Vibe: The city has a blend of Mediterranean charm and a more European feel. Its proximity to Spain adds an international flair.
Tech Scene: Tangier is becoming a focal point for digital nomads and entrepreneurs, especially in the tech and creative industries. The city has seen investment in infrastructure like coworking spaces and tech parks.
Quality of Life: Good, with a much lower cost of living than Casablanca or Rabat. There are excellent public services and a safe, growing expat community.
6. Agadir
Why it fits: Agadir is a coastal city that’s popular with locals for its beach and relaxed atmosphere but less so with tourists than cities like Marrakech or Casablanca.
Vibe: It’s more laid-back and less urban than Casablanca, with a mix of beach vibes and a growing expat scene. It’s great for those who love the sea and a slower pace of life but still want access to modern amenities.
Tech Scene: Not as developed, but it’s growing, especially for digital nomads working remotely. The city has a number of coworking spaces and an emerging startup culture.
Quality of Life: Excellent air quality, affordable living, and great weather make it a solid choice for those under 30 who want to live in a calmer city with access to nature.
7. Oujda
Why it fits: Oujda is an underrated gem in the east of Morocco. It’s not a major tourist city, so it’s relatively quiet, but it’s one of the top cities in terms of quality of life.
Vibe: Oujda offers a slower pace of life, with a focus on family and community. It's less international than the bigger cities, but the city has a lot to offer in terms of modern amenities.
Tech Scene: It’s not the tech hub of Morocco, but there is a growing community of young, ambitious professionals looking to make their mark.
Quality of Life: Affordable, safe, and clean. The cost of living is low, and the city is less crowded than Morocco’s larger urban centers.

### Equivalence to Chicago
Chicago has a certain urban "cool" vibe, driven by its combination of a booming tech scene, strong job market, diverse culture, and access to world-class amenities. If you're looking for something in Morocco that compares in terms of vibe, I'd say Casablanca feels the closest due to its role as the business capital, its diverse population, and its fast-paced urban life. Rabat comes in close, but it has more of a political and cultural feel rather than the industrial and entrepreneurial energy Chicago has.
