# surf

If you’re looking for the best waves in the U.S. while keeping shark anxiety to a minimum, your best bet is to head to Southern California or the Pacific Northwest. While no ocean is truly "shark-free," certain regions have significantly lower encounter rates due to water temperature, geography, and local ecosystems.
1. The Top Choice: Southern California (San Diego & Orange County)
Southern California offers the perfect balance of world-class surf and relatively low shark activity compared to other hotspots. While sharks live here (mostly juveniles), the risk of an unprovoked attack is statistically minuscule.
- La Jolla & Tourmaline (San Diego): These spots are famous for their gentle, long-period waves. While you might see harmless Leopard Sharks (which don't bite humans) in the shallows, actual incidents are incredibly rare.
- Malibu & Ventura: These classic point breaks are shielded by the geography of the coastline, making them less prone to the "deep water" visitors found in Northern California.
- Why it’s "Safe": The water is heavily monitored by lifeguards, and the high volume of people actually makes the statistical risk for an individual surfer lower.
2. The Statistical Safest: The Pacific Northwest (Washington)
If you are looking for the absolute lowest number of recorded shark incidents in a surfing state, Washington is the winner.
- Westport & Ocean Shores: These are the primary surf hubs in Washington.
- The Data: Washington has only recorded two shark attacks since 1837, neither of which were fatal.
- The Trade-off: The water is cold (you’ll need a 4/3mm or 5/4mm wetsuit), the weather is often grey, and the waves can be more rugged and challenging than the groomed points of California.
3. The "Zero Risk" Option: Wave Pools
If your fear of sharks is a dealbreaker, the U.S. now leads the world in high-end "surf ranches" and wave parks.
- Waco Surf (Texas): A world-class inland wave that offers air sections and barrels.
- Kelly Slater’s Surf Ranch (Lemoore, CA): The gold standard of artificial waves.
- The Lineup: You get 100% shark-free water, consistent conditions, and no "man in the grey suit" lurking beneath.
Places to Avoid (High Shark Activity)
If your goal is to minimize shark encounters, you should steer clear of these two regions:
| Region | Why Avoid? |
|---|---|
| Florida (East Coast) | New Smyrna Beach is the "Shark Bite Capital of the World." While the sharks are usually small (Blacktips/Spinners), the sheer volume of "nips" is the highest in the country. |
| The "Red Triangle" (NorCal) | The area between Monterey Bay, the Farallon Islands, and Bodega Bay in Northern California is a primary hunting ground for adult Great Whites. Spots like Bolinas and Salmon Creek have much higher sighting rates. |
Pro-Tips for Peace of Mind
- Avoid River Mouths: Sharks are attracted to the nutrient-rich, murky water where rivers meet the sea.
- Stay Away from Seals: If you see a large colony of seals or sea lions (like at some spots in Oregon or NorCal), you are essentially surfing in a pantry.
- Surf Mid-Day: Most shark activity occurs at "grey light"—dawn and dusk—when their hunting instincts are highest and visibility is lowest.
