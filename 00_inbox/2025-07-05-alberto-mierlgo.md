# Alberto mierlgo

Alberto Mielgo: A Brief Overview
Alberto Mielgo is a Spanish director, animator, and visual artist known for his distinctive and highly stylized approach to animation and visual storytelling. Mielgo's work blends traditional techniques with modern sensibilities, often playing with surrealism, color theory, and visual metaphors. He's gained significant recognition for his contributions to both television and film, with a style that is immediately recognizable due to its emotive quality and attention to detail.
Early Life and Career
Born: 1975 in Barcelona, Spain.
Education: Mielgo studied at the ESCAC (Escola Superior de Cinema i Audiovisuals de Catalunya), one of Spain's leading film schools, where he honed his craft in animation and visual storytelling.
### Notable Works
Love, Death & Robots: Jibaro (2022) – This episode became an instant fan favorite due to its visually stunning and experimental animation style. Jibaro is a perfect showcase of Mielgo's ability to combine breathtaking art with deep, almost mythical storytelling.
Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018) – Mielgo worked as a visual consultant on the film, contributing to the groundbreaking animation that mixed 2D and 3D styles. He played a key role in the design of the character Miles Morales, as well as influencing the overall visual direction of the film.
The Animated Short "The Wind" (2019) – A short film Mielgo directed, which earned widespread acclaim. It demonstrated his signature use of minimalistic design, cinematic atmosphere, and deep emotional resonance.
Puss in Boots: The Last Wish (2022) – He contributed to the design and animation of some scenes in this movie, which also showcased a unique and stylized visual approach.
### Key Features of Mielgo’s Style
### Visual Storytelling
Mielgo’s work is heavily focused on visual storytelling rather than dialogue or traditional exposition. This is especially evident in Jibaro, where minimal sound and no spoken language force the viewer to interpret emotions and narrative through the artwork itself.
Symbolism and Emotion: Mielgo uses color and visual metaphors to convey deep emotions. For instance, in Jibaro, colors are used to indicate the shift in mood or environment, often enhancing the emotional connection without words.
### Distinctive Art Style
Mielgo blends traditional animation techniques with 3D technology to create a hybrid style that feels fresh and organic. His designs often emphasize fluidity and expressiveness, creating a sense of emotional depth in even the most minimalist of characters.
Surrealism and Abstraction: His style is characterized by exaggerated proportions, bold use of light and shadow, and a penchant for abstract or surreal designs that amplify the psychological or emotional stakes of a scene.
Heavy Use of Color: His works are often characterized by strong, high-contrast color schemes, where every color serves a purpose. The use of warm, vibrant colors versus cold, muted tones can dictate the emotional atmosphere.
### Character Design
Mielgo’s characters tend to be very expressive, often simplifying shapes to their most basic form while maintaining emotional depth. He takes inspiration from graphic design and comic books, incorporating angular lines and dramatic silhouettes.
### Lighting and Shadow
Mielgo is a master of lighting, especially in how light and shadow play a role in enhancing the narrative. His use of silhouettes and backlighting emphasizes mood and tension, creating a dramatic effect that feels cinematic.
### Movement and Fluid Animation
One of the hallmarks of his animation style is the fluidity of movement. The characters in his works often exhibit highly stylized, yet fluid, motion. This is especially visible in the way his characters interact with their environments.

### Tutorials on Alberto Mielgo's Style
If you’re interested in learning his unique animation and art style, here are some ways you can break down his techniques and dive into creating your own work inspired by him.
1. Studying Color Theory and Lighting:
Mastering Color: Mielgo’s works often convey emotional subtext through bold, symbolic color choices. Watch films like Jibaro and Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse, and focus on how color influences mood. You can study traditional color theory and color palettes used in animation.
### Tutorial on Color in Animation
Look up resources that focus on color lighting and environmental design for animation (e.g., “Lighting and Color for Animation”).
Procreate and Photoshop tutorials often cover blending, gradients, and how to use color to create atmospheres.
2. Mastering Fluid Animation:
Mielgo’s animation style is very fluid, with smooth transitions between poses and subtle movements that carry emotional weight.
How to Animate Fluid Movements: Focus on the 12 Principles of Animation. These principles are crucial for achieving the kind of smooth, organic movement you see in Mielgo’s work.
Tutorial: Look for fluid animation tutorials on YouTube, especially those that focus on creating organic motion using animation software like Toon Boom Harmony or TVPaint.
3. Character Design & Stylization:
Mielgo is a master at taking simple shapes and making them emotionally resonant. A key tip is to focus on expression and emotion in your designs rather than getting bogged down in complex anatomy.
Tutorial: "Character Design for Animation" by Steve Cunningham is a great book. You can also find online courses from sites like Schoolism and Domestika on character design for animation.
4. Digital Painting & Art Techniques:
Mielgo's work is known for its highly detailed and dynamic digital painting, especially in creating environments and lighting.
Mastering Lighting: Learn how to create dramatic contrasts and cinematic lighting. This can be done using brushes and layer effects in software like Photoshop, Procreate, or Krita.
Tutorial on Digital Painting: Tutorials that focus on digital painting for concept art and cinematic environments will be particularly useful. There are great courses on CGMA and ArtStation Learning that teach lighting and digital painting.
5. Filmography and Animation Analysis:
If you're serious about understanding Mielgo's influences and style, dissecting his work is a great way to learn.
Watch his films, shorts, and episodes closely. Focus on how the camera moves, how light interacts with characters, and how the animation conveys story without dialogue. Tools like Storyboard Pro or TVPaint are great for replicating his type of fluid animation and expressive character movement.
6. Create Short Animations or Animatics:
One of the best ways to internalize Mielgo’s techniques is by creating short animations of your own. Focus on emotional beats, using color and lighting to enhance the narrative.
Create Animatics: Start with rough, quick animations to lay out the story, and then fine-tune them with detailed character designs, lighting, and effects. There are tons of online guides for animatics (such as Animatic Studios or YouTube tutorials).

### Final Thoughts
Alberto Mielgo’s style is a brilliant blend of emotive visual language, minimalism, and highly stylized design. Whether you’re interested in animation, storytelling, or visual design, studying his work can teach you a lot about the power of visual expression. His use of color, light, and shape to evoke mood is a great learning tool for any artist interested in storytelling through visuals.
