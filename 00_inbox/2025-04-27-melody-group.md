# melody group

To create a melody group that achieves the lush, harmonic, and deep sound like Ivylab using free FL Studio plugins, I'll walk you through a step-by-step guide to setting up a melody group FX chain. This will include some free plugins to enhance depth, warmth, and harmonic richness.

### Step 1: Set up your melody in FL Studio
1. **Choose your sound**: Start with a basic sound that can be shaped to fit the Ivylab style. Try using FL Studio’s stock **3xOSC** (a versatile synth) or **FL Keys** for a piano sound.

2. **Create a simple chord progression**: For a mellow vibe, you could use minor chords, which tend to sound more atmospheric and emotive.
- For example, try a progression like:
- C minor (Cm) - A# major (A#) - G minor (Gm) - F minor (Fm).

3. **Add a lead melody**: Play a lead melody over the chord progression using either **3xOSC** or **Sytrus** (a free plugin included with FL Studio).

### Step 2: Build your FX chain
Now, let's process your melody group with plugins that add depth, harmonic complexity, and lushness.

1. **Reverb - “Fruity Reverb 2”**:
- Reverb will add space and depth to the sound.
- Set the **Size** to around 50%, **Decay** around 4-6 seconds, and a moderate **Wet Level** (~30-40%). This will give the melody a lush, ambient feel without washing it out.

2. **EQ - “Fruity Parametric EQ 2”**:
- Use EQ to carve out space for the melody in the mix.
- Cut out low frequencies below 100Hz to prevent muddiness.
- Boost around **200-300Hz** slightly to add warmth.
- Add a slight boost around **4-5kHz** for presence and clarity.
- If needed, cut harshness around **1-2kHz**.

3. **Chorus - “Fruity Chorus”**:
- Apply subtle chorus to add width and movement.
- Set the **Depth** to around 30-40% and **Rate** to around 0.6-0.8 Hz for a smooth, wide sound.

4. **Stereo Imaging - “Fruity Stereo Shaper”**:
- Use this to widen the stereo field and add space.
- Apply subtle widening, especially to the high end, around **3-6kHz**. You can adjust the width to taste, but keep it subtle to maintain the track's cohesion.

5. **Distortion (optional) - “Fruity Fast Dist”**:
- Use a mild distortion to add warmth and harmonic saturation.
- Set the **Drive** very low (around 10-15%) for a subtle effect that doesn’t overwhelm the sound.

6. **Delay - “Fruity Delay 3”**:
- Delay will help to add space and dimension to your melody.
- Set the **Time** to 1/4 or 1/8 (depending on tempo), and adjust **Wet Level** to about 20-30% for a subtle, rhythmic echo.

7. **LFO Modulation (Optional) - “Fruity Love Philter”**:
- Apply some slow, sweeping modulation to create movement in the sound.
- Set it to **Lowpass Filter** and automate the cutoff to create dynamic movement over time.

### Step 3: Fine-tuning and layering
- **Layer sounds**: To make the melody even more complex and lush, consider layering sounds with similar chord progressions but different textures (e.g., one with a soft pad sound, one with a plucked sound).
- **Automation**: Automate volume, reverb wet/dry, or filter cutoff to create a more dynamic, evolving melody.

### ### Final Tips
- **Use subtle effects**: Ivylab’s sound often feels intricate but not overwhelming. Keep effects subtle for a cohesive, refined sound.
- **Experiment with panning**: Slightly pan different elements of your melody (like a second, complementary melody) to create a wider soundstage.

With these tools and techniques, you should be able to start shaping your melodies into the Ivylab-inspired sound you’re after. Let me know if you'd like to dive deeper into any specific plugin or technique!
