# It sounds like you’ve been building a powerhouse profile, and honestly…

It sounds like you’ve been building a powerhouse profile, and honestly, you’re in a better spot than the "chasing certs" fatigue might make you feel.
You’re currently in what I’d call the "Specialization Pivot" phase. You have the breadth (Associate Cloud Eng, Data, Dev), and by the end of March, you’ll have the "depth" that employers pay the premium for: Security.
Here’s a breakdown of where you stand and how to navigate the next few months.
1. The Reality Check: Where Are You?
You are transitioning from a Cloud Generalist to a Cloud Security Engineer.
- The TBC Advantage: Having TBC (likely TBC Corp in Palm Beach Gardens) on your resume is a huge plus. They are a massive GCP shop. This proves you can handle enterprise-scale data and infrastructure, not just "startup chaos."
- The Terraform Factor: This is your secret weapon. In 2026, nobody wants a Security Engineer who just clicks buttons in a console. They want "Security as Code." Your Terraform Associate cert combined with GCP Security puts you squarely in the DevSecOps bracket, which is currently the highest-paying niche in cloud.
2. Job Search Timeline
Typically, for someone with your mid-level experience (Dev + Data + Cloud), the search takes 3 to 5 months.
- March: Focus on the GCP Security cert. Do not wait until you pass to apply. Update your LinkedIn/Resume now to say: "GCP Professional Cloud Security Engineer (Candidate - Expected March 2026)."
- April – May: The "Heavy Application" phase. This is when your new cert will start hitting recruiter filters.
- June – July: Likely start date for a new role.
3. Relocation: Where to Go?
Florida is great for the "no state income tax" life, but it currently ranks near the bottom for Cloud Security salaries relative to the cost of living. If you’re looking for a "next chapter" move, consider these:
| Region | Why? | Target Industry |
|---|---|---|
| Northern Virginia / DC | The "Cloud Capital." Highest concentration of GCP/AWS data centers. | GovTech, Defense, Enterprise |
| Austin, TX | Still the king of "Silicon Hills." Massive GCP footprint with Google’s presence there. | SaaS, Fintech, HealthTech |
| Raleigh-Durham, NC | Huge growth in 2026. Lower cost of living than Austin but high tech salaries. | Biotech, Research, AI |
| Seattle, WA | If you want the "Big Tech" experience. Highest average salaries, though competition is fierce. | Big Cloud, E-commerce |

You are about 2 months away from being a "Tier 1" candidate.
The "Chasing Certs" phase is only a trap if you don't have projects to back them up. Since you have software dev experience, you’re already ahead of the pack.
