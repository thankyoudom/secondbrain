# That’s a great instinct—digging into the roots of 40’s sound helps…

That’s a great instinct—digging into the roots of 40’s sound helps you build from influence, not just imitation. Let’s break it down by musical ancestry, emotional language, production philosophy, and specific artists/genres that shaped the OVO/Noah “40” Shebib aesthetic.

1. Emotional and Sonic Philosophy
40’s music is emotionally heavy, introspective, sometimes cold—but never empty. It’s minimalism with soul. That feeling comes from a specific philosophy:
Less is more – Every sound earns its place
Emotion over complexity – Simplicity that hits hard
Vulnerability as power – It’s not afraid of sadness, space, or softness
Textures over melodies – You feel it before you hum it

2. Genre Influences (Musical DNA)
A) 90s R&B (Emotional Core)
Aaliyah (especially the Timbaland era)
Brandy – rich harmonies and vocal layers
Sade – melancholy, sultry, stripped-back arrangements
Jodeci / Ginuwine – slow jams with heavy bass and mood
How this manifests: slow BPMs, lush chord progressions, sultry textures, hypnotic groove

B) Trip-Hop (Atmospheric Texture)
Massive Attack – moody, urban, cinematic beats
Portishead – lo-fi, haunting vocal processing
Tricky – gritty and dark with minimal arrangements
How this manifests: vinyl noise, weird sampling, eerie pads, sparse but heavy drum work

C) Ambient & Experimental
Brian Eno – layering of texture over form
Boards of Canada – warped analog warmth, tape feel
Aphex Twin (select works) – ambient piano, reverb abuse
How this manifests: pads that sound like memories, reverbs that become instruments

D) Jazz & Neo-Soul (Harmonic Language)
Robert Glasper – modern jazz voicings with hip hop drums
D’Angelo – swing, late timing, rich chords
Jill Scott / Erykah Badu – raw vocal phrasing, vibe-first production
How this manifests: 7th/9th chords, loose timing, smooth modulations, soul-rich voicings

E) Southern Hip Hop & Screw Culture (Drake’s Southern Lean)
DJ Screw – slowed down tracks, bass-heavy
UGK / Z-Ro – deep sub-bass, dark keys, emotional street tales
How this manifests: pitched-down samples, slow BPMs (60–75), syrupy feel, deep bass

3. Film Score / Soundtrack Influence
40 has spoken about scoring ideas—think about:
Hans Zimmer (use of space and tension)
Atticus Ross & Trent Reznor (raw, industrial textures)
Blade Runner OST / Vangelis – ambient synths, emotional isolation
How this manifests: cinematic pads, slow builds, emotionally evocative silence

4. Spiritual & Cultural Vibe
40 grew up in Toronto, a hyper-diverse city where Caribbean, Middle Eastern, African, and European cultures collide. This multicultural DNA leaks into the beats:
Dancehall / Reggae swing in rhythm
Middle Eastern scales and melodic phrasing
African percussive restraint—space between drums is key
Gospel-like vocal processing—stacked harmonies, wet reverbs

5. Technical Heroes & Mentors
Timbaland – groove-driven production, vocal FX
J Dilla – swing, pocket, sample manipulation
Kanye West (early) – soul samples, pitched vocals
Nujabes – emotion-focused hip hop instrumentals
