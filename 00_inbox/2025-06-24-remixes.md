# remixes

Distributing remixes as TikTok sounds is a great way to gain exposure! However, when it comes to remixes, there are some legal considerations to keep in mind, especially regarding copyright. Here are a few platforms that allow you to distribute remixes or sounds for TikTok while respecting copyright:
1. SoundCloud
Usage: You can upload your remixes to SoundCloud and then share them on TikTok. SoundCloud has a large remix culture, and many artists share their remixes for public use.
Legal Consideration: You should make sure you’re not violating any copyright laws, and consider using SoundCloud’s “Creative Commons” licensing options for your tracks to clarify how others can use them.
2. YouTube (via YouTube Music or YouTube Content ID)
Usage: You can upload your remix to YouTube, and then potentially use TikTok’s music library to access the track if YouTube and TikTok have licensing agreements.
Legal Consideration: Some remixes might be blocked or removed if they infringe on copyright. YouTube’s Content ID system also flags copyrighted music, so you’ll need to be careful with what you remix.
3. DistroKid
Usage: DistroKid allows you to distribute music to platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, TikTok, and others. You can upload your remixes as long as you have the appropriate permissions or licenses for the original tracks.
Legal Consideration: You need to have a valid license or permission from the original copyright holder for the remix to be distributed.
4. Amuse
Usage: Amuse is a distribution platform similar to DistroKid but offers a free option. You can distribute your remixes to TikTok, Spotify, and other platforms, as long as you have the rights.
Legal Consideration: Make sure you have the necessary rights to distribute the remix. Without permission, your remix could be taken down.
5. UnitedMasters
Usage: UnitedMasters is another distribution platform that allows you to send music to TikTok, Spotify, and other platforms. It’s popular among independent artists.
Legal Consideration: Again, you need permission to distribute remixes legally.
6. TikTok Sound Upload (via TikTok Music Distribution Partners)
Usage: Some music distribution services have direct partnerships with TikTok (e.g., TuneCore, DistroKid, CD Baby), allowing you to upload music directly to TikTok as a sound.
Legal Consideration: Make sure you have the legal rights to the remix. Some platforms may ask for proof of your rights to the content you’re distributing.
7. Tracklib
Usage: Tracklib is a platform that provides access to original stems and tracks for legal remixing. If you remix tracks that are available on Tracklib, you’re more likely to avoid legal issues.
Legal Consideration: Tracks on Tracklib are cleared for remixing, so you’re safe as long as you follow the platform’s rules.

### Tips for Distributing Remixes Legally
Get permission from the original artist or use royalty-free or Creative Commons music to avoid copyright issues.
Check the Terms of Service for platforms like TikTok, as they may have specific rules around user-generated content.
Use services with licensing options to ensure you're covered legally when distributing remixes.
If you're not sure about the copyright of a specific track, it’s always safer to reach out to the original creator or get a license before distributing your remix.
