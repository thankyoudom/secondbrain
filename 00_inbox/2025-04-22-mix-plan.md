? MIXING SAUCE FOR THAT FULL, TSURUDA-ISH SOUND
These tricks add texture, depth, and cohesion:
1. Parallel Compression (Drums Bus)
Duplicate drums to a bus
Smash with compressor (Ratio 10:1, Fast Attack, Medium Release)
Mix in at 10–30%
Result = Punch without over-limiting
2. Saturation (Tastefully)
Drums: Tape or analog-style (Decapitator, Saturn, RC-20)
Bass: Subtle drive for harmonics
Master Bus (light): Glue saturation (Kramer Tape, Black Box, etc.)
3. Reverb Sends Instead of Inserts
Create one or two main reverb buses
Send melody, FX, and perc to them with different send levels
Keeps space unified, instead of each sound floating on its own
4. Sidechain Ducking
Don’t just duck bass—duck pads, reverb returns, background percs
Keeps drums center stage without overpowering

? Final Tips
Highpass ruthlessly – even FX and melodies need clean low ends
Automate volume and width – little shifts in loop sections = dynamic growth
Compare to references (Tsuruda, Ivy Lab, Shades) at equal loudness
Bus your groups: drums, melodic, FX, vocals—process them in chunks
