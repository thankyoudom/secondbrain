# This is a really thoughtful question—and it shows you care about both…

This is a really thoughtful question—and it shows you care about both your friend and your own well-being. Being supportive while protecting your mental health is not selfish; it’s necessary. Here’s how healthy boundaries can look in a situation like this:

1. Be Clear About What You Can Offer
Support doesn’t mean being available 24/7.
### Example
“I really care about you and want to support you. I may not always have the capacity to talk late at night, but I’m here when I can be.”
This sets expectations without rejection.

2. Offer Presence, Not Solutions
You don’t need to fix their grief or emotions.
### Healthy support looks like
Listening
Acknowledging pain
Saying “That sounds really hard” instead of trying to make it better
This keeps you from carrying emotional weight that isn’t yours.

3. Set Time & Energy Limits
Especially if your own mental health is fragile, it’s okay to limit duration and intensity.
### Examples
“I can check in for a bit today, but I may need to step away afterward.”
“I want to be there for you, and I also need to take care of myself so I don’t burn out.”
Boundaries protect both of you.

4. Don’t Become the Only Support System
Gently encourage a wider circle of support.
### Example
“I’m really glad you’re talking to me. It might also help to lean on family, friends, or a counselor during this time.”
This reduces emotional dependency.

5. Respond With Compassion When You Need Space
You can step back without disappearing.
### Examples
“I’m thinking of you today, even if I’m a bit quiet.”
“I don’t have the emotional bandwidth to talk right now, but I’ll check in tomorrow.”
Kind + honest = healthy boundary.

6. Watch for Signs You’re Taking on Too Much
### Red flags include
Feeling emotionally drained after every interaction
Anxiety before responding to messages
Guilt for needing space
If you notice these, it’s time to tighten boundaries, not push yourself harder.

7. Remember: Boundaries Are Loving
### Healthy boundaries say
“I care about you enough to be honest and sustainable.”
They prevent resentment, burnout, and emotional collapse.

A Simple Script You Can Use
"I care about you deeply and want to support you. At the same time, I’m navigating my own mental health, so I may need to pace myself. Please know that my care for you doesn’t change—even when I need a little space."
