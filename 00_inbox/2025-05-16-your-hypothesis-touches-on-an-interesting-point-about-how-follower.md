# Your hypothesis touches on an interesting point about how follower…

Your hypothesis touches on an interesting point about how follower/following ratios and engagement affect algorithmic performance on platforms like TikTok and Instagram. Let’s break this down:

TikTok: Does Your Follow Ratio Matter for Performance?
TikTok’s algorithm primarily prioritizes engagement signals over follow/following ratios. What matters most:
Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares, watch time, replays)
Content watch time and completion
User interest matching (based on prior behavior)
Your follower/following ratio can affect how your profile looks to others (e.g., if you're following many but few follow you back), but it doesn’t directly impact how TikTok shows your content.
However, here's where your idea has some merit:
If you only follow 5 people who consistently engage, and don’t follow a lot of inactive accounts, your "friend" circle becomes highly active, which might:
Slightly improve the initial "seed" engagement TikTok uses to test new content.
Make the algorithm interpret your account as more meaningful or active within a niche community.
But overall, reducing your following count to only include engaged users won’t dramatically boost performance. TikTok's For You Page (FYP) is engagement- and interest-based, not follower-dependent.

Instagram: Does the Follow Ratio Matter More Here?
Instagram is more sensitive to follower/following dynamics:
A high following-to-follower ratio can make your account seem less authoritative, especially for newer creators or business pages.
Engagement rate matters most: If you have 185 followers but only 5 interact, your engagement rate is low, which hurts reach.
Your hypothesis has stronger weight on Instagram:
If you clean up your followers/following and only keep people who engage, it could slightly boost visibility, especially if:
You're using Stories, Reels, or Posts and those 5 people consistently interact.
Instagram sees a tight, engaged network, which sometimes helps push your content further in the explore/reel algorithms.
However, engagement from a broader, real audience is still crucial. The algorithm can’t be “tricked” simply by pruning your list.
