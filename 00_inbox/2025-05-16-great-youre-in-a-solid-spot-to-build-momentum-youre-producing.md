# Great, you're in a solid spot to build momentum — you’re producing…

Great, you're in a solid spot to build momentum — you’re producing content, have a clear niche (beats/music production), and you already know your demographic. Here's a full breakdown to help improve your TikTok strategy:

1. Best Time to Post on Friday (for 18–30 audience)
### Tomorrow (Friday)
### Best time to post
12 PM – 2 PM and 4 PM – 6 PM (local time)
These windows catch people on lunch breaks and winding down after school/work. For musicians and creators, Fridays are also when people start scouting for weekend vibes or music content.

2. Your Current Stats: What They Mean
### 110 views and 3–4 likes suggests
People aren’t watching the full video (hurts your reach).
Your hook (first 2 seconds) might not be strong enough.
Your audience targeting or hashtags may need refining.
Or you’re not yet posting consistently or frequently enough for the algorithm to pick you up.

3. Key Tips to Improve Your TikTok Growth
A. Hook People in the First 2 Seconds
Try a strong visual cue: waveform animations, beat drop tease, or text like “You’ve never heard drums hit like this.”
Ask a question or make a bold claim:
“What if Kanye heard this?” or “Sampled from a 70s soul record…”
B. Use Captions, Text Hooks, and Trends
Add on-screen text that explains or teases your beat.
Use trending audio and remix it with your beat (makes it searchable).
Stitch or duet producers/artists or react to trending music content.
C. Post 3–5x a Week
Momentum is everything. The algorithm favors creators who post more often and engage.
One post can be a beat, another a behind-the-scenes clip, another showing your setup, another asking for duets or collabs.
D. Hashtags That Work
Use a mix of big and niche hashtags:
#ProducerLife
#TypeBeat (plus the artist name — #DrakeTypeBeat etc.)
#Beatmaker
#MusicTok
#UndergroundBeats
#SampleFlip
Also include a few rotating ones based on trends.

4. Video Ideas (Beat Producer Focused)
Before/After Sample Flip: Show the original sample then your flip.
Making a Beat in 30 Seconds: Fast-paced edit.
“If X Artist Walked into My Studio…” (Play a type beat)
Duet or Collab With Rappers/Singers: Let others freestyle over your beat.
Your Setup Tour: People love gear and software breakdowns.
Story Behind a Beat: What inspired you? What did you sample?

5. Engage With the Community
Comment on other music creators’ videos.
Reply to comments on your videos with video responses.
Ask viewers questions: “Who should rap on this?” or “Rate this 1–10.”
