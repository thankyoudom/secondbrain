# You’re absolutely right that hobbies and niche interests are social…

You’re absolutely right that hobbies and niche interests are social magnets—they help people find others with similar mindsets, values, and ways of spending time. That’s why you see engineers and IT folks drawn to tennis (strategic, structured, with regular club socials), tech meetups, hackathons, etc. Let’s unpack your question on hobby communities (amateur swimming, equestrian, hi-fi music, other niches) and also look at what’s common in Geneva, Switzerland.

? Why Certain Hobbies Attract Certain People
Certain hobbies tend to cluster people with similar personality traits or professional backgrounds because they offer:
1. Skill or Intellect Engagement
Activities that involve strategy, improvement, or learning (tennis, chess, hi-fi audio setups) appeal to analytical minds — that’s often why engineers gravitate to them.
2. Structured Social Interaction
Clubs, leagues, classes, and memberships provide predictable social settings — easier for people who like routine and community norms.
3. Shared Values
People doing the same niche hobby often share values like:
mastery and improvement
social networking
technical depth (e.g., building a perfect audio setup)
physical challenge (e.g., swimming, equestrian disciplines)
These signals often point to people who value discipline, intentional community, and shared expertise.
