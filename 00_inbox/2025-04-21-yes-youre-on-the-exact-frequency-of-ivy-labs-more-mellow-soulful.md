## **What to Try: Techniques to Capture the Vibe**

Here’s a **weekly challenge cycle** to get your layering + vibe tighter:

### **Week 1: Drum Grooves**
- Use ultra-short samples with swing.
- Push and pull drums manually off the grid.
- Layer dusty hats with foley pops or pen clicks.
- Use **Velocities & EQ** to make every hit feel hand-made.

### **Week 2: Sample + Flip**
- Grab a jazz/soul sample, EQ the mids, pitch it, then *resample and chop*.
- Add subtle chorus or warble (Fruity Phaser + automation).

### **Week 3: FX & Texture Layers**
- Make a 16-bar loop of ambience using:
- Reversed reverb tails
- Vinyl noise loops
- Distant vocals processed into pad texture
- Use **Gross Beat**, **Fruity Granulizer**, or **Newtone** for glitchy variation.

### **Week 4: Arrangement + Space**
- Build a mellow drop with:
- 1 melodic hook (vocal or soft lead)
- 1 sub pattern
- Sparse percussion
- Background atmosphere pad or noise wash
- Focus on **contrast between dry and wet** sounds.

---

### **FL Studio Tips for Ivy Lab Vibe:**

- **Fruity Convolver** – Use it with weird IRs (like glass bowls or empty halls).
- **Fruity Love Philter** – Great for lo-fi filter automation on pads or vocals.
- **Fruity Slicer** – Chop audio into stutters and edits.
- **Pitch Automation** – Automate pitch bend over time for weird tape feel.
- **Granulizer / Gross Beat + Resample** – Reverse, stretch, glitch, then resample.
