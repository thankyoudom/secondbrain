# Yes, Ableton Live offers a free version called Ableton Live Lite. It's…

Yes, Ableton Live offers a free version called Ableton Live Lite. It's a more limited version of the full Ableton Live software, but still very functional for beginners or for those who want to test out the software before committing to a purchase.
Ableton Live Lite is often bundled with various hardware (like audio interfaces, MIDI controllers, or other music production gear), so if you already have some gear, you might already have access to it.
Here are some of the key limitations in Ableton Live Lite compared to the full versions:
Track Count: It limits the number of audio and MIDI tracks you can use.
Effects & Instruments: You get a more limited selection of built-in instruments and effects.
Scenes & Tracks: Fewer scenes and clips for creating compositions.
Sampling: You get fewer sample-editing features, though it’s still functional for basic work.
If you're just starting out or need something for simple music projects, it might be enough. If you find you need more features down the line, you can always upgrade to one of the paid versions.
