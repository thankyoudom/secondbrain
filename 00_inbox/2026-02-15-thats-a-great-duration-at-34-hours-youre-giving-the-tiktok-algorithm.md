# That’s a great duration. At 3–4 hours, you’re giving the TikTok algorithm…

That’s a great duration. At 3–4 hours, you’re giving the TikTok algorithm enough time to "find" your audience, but you’re finishing before you burn out or lose the quality of your linework.
Since you’re in the "grind" phase of studying anatomy, the goal is to make your process the product. Here is your Year 1 blueprint for turning those study sessions into a business.
1. The "Show Flow" (How to spend those 4 hours)
Don't just draw in silence. A "Study with Me" stream works best when it has a rhythm.
- Hour 1: The Warm-up. Low stakes. Basic shapes, gesture drawings, and getting the hand moving. This is when you greet people and talk about your goals for the day.
- Hour 2-3: The Deep Dive. This is your "Deep Work" block. Focus on a specific muscle group or bone structure. Mention what resources you’re using (e.g., "I'm using the Proko method today").
- Hour 4: The Review & Q&A. Scroll back through what you drew. Show the "before and after." This is the best time to drop your affiliate links because the "value" has already been delivered.
2. The "Visual HiFi" Aesthetic
Since you love HiFi DJ sets, your stream should look like it sounds.
- Audio Visualizers: Use an app or OBS overlay that shows a waveform of your music. It adds a "lo-fi radio" vibe that keeps the screen dynamic even when you aren't moving much.
- The "Desk Glow": Invest in a single warm light (orange/amber) behind your iPad or desk. It creates that "late-night study" aesthetic that does very well on TikTok.
- Alternative Music: Since Mixcloud is risky, check out StreamBeats by Harris Heller or Epidemic Sound. They have "Chill/Lo-Fi" DJ-style mixes that are 100% safe. You can even find "Lo-fi DJ Sets" on YouTube that are marked "No Copyright."
3. Turning Anatomy into Affiliate Sales
You don't need to sell your own art yet. You sell the tools you use to learn.
- The "Paperlike" Effect: Screen protectors that make the iPad feel like paper are the #1 affiliate for iPad artists.
- Ergonomic Grips: If you use a chunky stylus grip to save your hand during long anatomy sessions, people will ask about it.
- Digital Brushes: If you use a specific brush set (like Jame's Coffee or Sinix brushes), mention them. Many creators have affiliate programs for their Procreate packs.
- The "Affiliate Hook": Instead of saying "Buy this," say: "My hand used to cramp after two hours of anatomy, then I got this $10 grip. Link is in the showcase if you draw long hours too."
4. The Content Flywheel (Timelapses)
Your stream is the "raw material." Your TikTok posts are the "marketing."
- Export Procreate Timelapses: Every single stream should result in one 30-second timelapse.
- The "Mistake" Edit: Don't just post the perfect drawing. Post the part where you struggled with a leg muscle for 20 minutes and then fixed it. Captions like "Anatomy is hard, day 45 of trying to get the pelvis right" get way more engagement than "look at my cool drawing."
- Educational Carousels: Take screenshots of your best studies and post them as a "Photo Mode" slideshow. People Save educational art tips, and "Saves" are the #1 metric for TikTok growth right now.
5. Year 1 Outlook & Returns
| Period | Focus | Revenue Source |
|---|---|---|
| Months 1-4 | Consistency & Audio/Visual Vibe | TikTok "Gifts" (Coffee money) |
| Months 5-8 | Building "The Kit" (Link in bio/Showcase) | Small Affiliate commissions ($50-$200/mo) |
| Months 9-12 | Authority (People ask you for advice) | Brand deals & Shop sales ($500+ /mo) |
The "Better Way" to look at this: You aren't "streaming for views." You are documenting your education. If you view it as "I have to do this anatomy study anyway, I might as well leave the camera on," you will never feel like you're wasting your time.
