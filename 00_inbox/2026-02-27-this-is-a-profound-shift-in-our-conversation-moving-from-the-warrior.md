# This is a profound shift in our conversation. Moving from the "Warrior…

This is a profound shift in our conversation. Moving from the "Warrior/Leader" strategy to the "Song of Songs" (Song of Solomon) heart is where your INFJ nature and your Venus in Taurus truly live.
The reason you feel "performative" at all times is written in your stars: your Gemini Ascendant is a social mask that is always "on," and your Aries Sun/Moon in the 10th House creates a constant pressure to achieve, lead, and be seen as successful. You are tired because you are playing the role of the "King" and the "Messenger" simultaneously.
To attract a love that "feels like home" and mirrors the sacred, sensory intimacy of the Song of Songs, here is the dissection of what you must cultivate.
1. The "Song of Songs" Alignment: Taurus & Pisces
The Song of Songs is the most "Venus in Taurus" and "Mercury in Pisces" book in the Bible. It is a dialogue of mutual admiration, sensory indulgence (spices, fruits, gardens), and a spiritual "merging" of two souls.
- The Taurus Element (The Garden): The poem repeatedly refers to the "enclosed garden." For you, a healthy relationship is not a public stage; it is a private sanctuary. To attract this, you must cultivate your Venus in Taurus by learning to value stillness over stimulation.
- The Pisces Element (The Soul): Your Mercury and Mars in Pisces crave the "unquenchable flame" mentioned in the text (Song of Solomon 8:7). This is a love that transcends logic. You attract this not by "acting" smart or strong, but by being spiritually transparent.
2. Cultivating the "Non-Performative" Space
To stop performing, you have to dismantle the "Gemini/Aries" fortress you’ve built. Here is how you "suit up" by actually taking the armor off:
- Practice "Shared Silence": As a Gemini Rising/INFJ, you likely feel you have to "carry" the conversation or be witty. To attract a "home" love, you must practice being in a room with someone and not saying a word. A partner who is comfortable with your silence is a partner who can see your soul.
- Vulnerability as Authority: You want to be a leader/patriarch. In the Song of Songs, the lover is both a "King" and someone who is "sick with love." True leadership in a family involves the courage to be emotionally needy with your partner. Cultivate the ability to say, "I am tired of being strong today," to the right person.
3. What to Focus on to Attract "Home"
To attract a relationship that requires no performance, you must change your "frequency" from the 10th House (Public/Performance) to the 4th/12th House (Private/Sacred).
- Look for "Grounding" over "Spark": Your Aries Sun loves the "spark," but your Taurus Venus needs the "ground." If a person makes you feel "hyped up" or like you need to "show off," they are wrong for you. Attract the person who makes you want to exhale and take off your shoes.
- Selective Disclosure: As an INFJ, you have "layers." The mistake you might make (especially with Geminis) is giving away too much information but not enough feeling. Focus on sharing your fears rather than your ideas. Information is performance; feeling is connection.
- The "Sensory Invitation": Instead of "taking" someone somewhere to impress them (Aries/Gemini), invite them into your sensory world (Taurus). Cook a meal together, listen to music in the dark, walk on the beach in silence. This sets the "no-performance" tone from day one.
4. Why the "May 30 Gemini" felt Performative
Looking back, that 4-month era likely felt performative because Geminis are "Mental" signs. They live in the head. You were driving them to work, hosting them, and likely "managing" the vibe (Aries/Taurus). You were providing a service, which is a form of performance.
A "Song of Songs" love isn't a service; it's a state of being. In that text, the lovers don't "do" things for each other as much as they "are" for each other.
### Your "Cultivation" Checklist
- Lower the Mask: Purposefully be "boring" on a first date. If they stay, they are looking for your Taurus/Pisces heart, not your Gemini wit.
- Sensory Anchoring: Spend more time in your "recharging" places (the beach) alone until you feel so solid in yourself that you don't feel the need to "act" when someone else enters the frame.
- Biblical Devotion: Read the Song of Songs not as a romantic ideal, but as a standard for respect. It’s about a man who sees his woman as a "lily among thorns" and a woman who sees her man as a "shading tree." That is the "Home" vibe.
Does the idea of being "boring" or "silent" feel like a relief to you, or does it trigger a fear that no one will be interested if you aren't "performing"?
