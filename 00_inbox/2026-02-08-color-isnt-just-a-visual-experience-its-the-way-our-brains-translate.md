1. The Scientific Spectrum: Physics & Biology
Visible light is a tiny sliver of the electromagnetic spectrum, ranging from roughly 380 to 750 nanometers (nm).
| Color | Wavelength | Scientific Pertinence |
|---|---|---|
| Red | ~700 nm | Longest wavelength, lowest energy. It penetrates tissue most deeply (used in infrared therapy) and triggers a physical "alert" response. |
| Orange | ~600 nm | Associated with thermal energy and fire. It stimulates appetite and social interaction. |
| Yellow | ~580 nm | The most visible color to the human eye. It triggers the release of serotonin. |
| Green | ~530 nm | The "rest" point for the eye. The lens focuses green light almost exactly on the retina, requiring the least amount of muscular effort. |
| Blue | ~470 nm | High energy, short wavelength. It suppresses melatonin production (keeping us awake) and is the color of the "far horizon." |
| Violet | ~400 nm | Shortest wavelength, highest energy. It has the highest frequency before transitioning into Ultraviolet (UV) light. |

If you are looking to soothe a "fried" or overstimulated nervous system, specific colors act as biological anchors:
- Soft Sage Green: Resonates with nature and signals to the amygdala that "resources are plentiful; we are safe."

- Amber & Warm Orange: These colors mimic the setting sun. They signal the body to transition from the Sympathetic (fight/flight) to the Parasympathetic (rest/digest) state by stimulating natural melatonin.
