# I love that you're looking for a clear journey to follow. Let’s break…

I love that you're looking for a clear journey to follow. Let’s break down a realistic journey for an indie music producer on TikTok, using a real-world example to track progress from the ground up to the point where money starts coming in. I'll share an example based on a hypothetical but plausible case that mirrors what’s worked for indie producers over the last couple of years.
Scenario: Indie Producer Journey to Monetization on TikTok
Phase 1: Starting From Scratch
### Starting Point
Followers: 0
Content: Initially, the producer posts some beats, some behind-the-scenes (BTS), and a few tutorials on music production. At this stage, the content is raw and unpolished—no clear strategy, just figuring it out.
Posting Frequency: 2-3 posts per week
Engagement: Very low (maybe 50-100 views per post, 2-3 likes, 1-2 comments).
Benchmark to hit: At this point, the main goal is just to get noticed. The TikTok algorithm hasn’t yet favored the account, and engagement is slow. This phase could last 1-2 months.
Phase 2: Gaining Momentum & Building Engagement (3-4 months)
Followers: 300-500
Content: The producer refines their content strategy. They start using trending sounds, experimenting with visualizers, and engaging in TikTok trends/challenges. They may also start doing “beat breakdown” videos or giving production tips (which works well for educational content).
New Strategy: Focus on high-quality beats, clear text overlays, and engaging with the comments (asking questions, thanking people for feedback).
Posting Frequency: 3-5 posts per week (at this point, TikTok rewards consistency).
### Engagement: Engagement starts picking up
Average views per video: 300-1,000 views.
Likes per video: 20-40 likes.
Comments per video: 5-10 comments.
Milestone: A video starts to gain traction, maybe hitting 2,000-5,000 views. The first real taste of “viral” content starts to appear, and engagement spikes. This could be from a specific tutorial, a reaction video to a challenge, or a remix of a popular meme song.
### Income Potential at this Stage
Monetizing via TikTok's Creator Fund: At this stage, TikTok might start recommending your videos to a wider audience. The TikTok Creator Fund is available once you hit 10,000 followers, but the pay per view is relatively low.
Revenue estimate: Typically, for every 100,000 views, a creator makes around $20-$40 from the Creator Fund. So at 1,000 views, you’re making around $0.20-$0.40 per post.
Total Income at this stage: Minimal, but starting to see pennies trickling in from TikTok. You might make $10-$30 a month from Creator Fund if you’re posting consistently.
Phase 3: Steady Growth and Engagement (6-12 months)
Followers: 1,000-3,000
Content: At this stage, content is getting more refined. The producer now has a clear brand identity. They continue posting tutorials, remixes, and collaborations with other creators.
They may start doing paid collaborations (shoutouts from fellow producers, working with influencers in the music scene), or they might promote their beat store in a more direct way (showing the link in the bio).
Posting Frequency: 4-6 posts per week.
Engagement: Their videos are getting more attention:
Average views per video: 3,000-10,000 views.
Likes per video: 100-300 likes.
Comments per video: 20-50 comments.
Milestone: One video hits 20,000-50,000 views, which starts to lead to more followers and more attention to the producer’s profile. This could happen through a viral beat, remix, or collaboration.
### Income Potential at this Stage
Creator Fund: As the producer now hits 10k followers and posts consistently, their earnings from the Creator Fund can rise.
Revenue estimate: 100,000 views per month could lead to $40-$100 in Creator Fund income.
Selling Beats: The biggest leap here is monetizing directly through selling beats or offering music production services. Many indie producers use TikTok as a platform to sell their beats. If they start promoting their beat store, they might sell beats for $30-$200 per beat.
Example: The producer sells 2-3 beats a month for $50 each (reasonable for an indie producer), generating $100-$150 in beat sales.
Total Income at this stage: Between $150-$250 per month, combining Creator Fund earnings, beat sales, and potentially other small sources like collaborations or shoutouts.
Phase 4: Full Monetization & Brand Deals (12-18 months)
Followers: 5,000-10,000+
Content: Now, the producer has developed a recognizable brand. Their content consistently goes viral or gains a decent amount of traction. They’re engaging with comments, responding to messages, and collaborating with other producers and musicians.
At this point, they may start running ads for their beat store or offering exclusive content (like private tutorials or behind-the-scenes access).
They might also start promoting their own digital products like sample packs, courses, or subscriptions.
Posting Frequency: 5-7 posts per week, plus interaction in comments and Duets.
### Engagement: Their content gets significant attention
Average views per video: 10,000-50,000 views, with a couple of posts potentially going viral.
Likes per video: 500-1,000+ likes.
Comments per video: 50-150+ comments.
Milestone: The producer now consistently gets 100,000+ views per month across multiple videos. This leads to a significant increase in followers, potentially reaching 10,000-20,000.
### Income Potential at this Stage
Creator Fund: Now earning more from TikTok’s Creator Fund. With 100,000 views per month, you’re looking at $100-$200.
Beat Sales: At this stage, the producer might sell 5-10 beats per month at $50-$150 each, leading to $300-$1,500per month in beat sales.
Brand Deals: With a strong following, the producer might start to get brand deals or collaborations. For example, they might get paid $200-$500 for sponsored content or for reviewing music production gear/software.
Total Income at this stage: Combining Creator Fund earnings, beat sales, and brand deals, the producer could be making $500-$2,000+ per month.
Phase 5: Established Producer with Ongoing Revenue (18+ months)
Followers: 20,000+
Content: At this stage, the producer is an established creator with multiple revenue streams. They may even be considered a micro-influencer within the music production community. They’re doing regular brand partnerships, selling beats, and possibly running paid ads to promote their products.
They may also be offering premium services (e.g., custom beats, exclusive tutorials) or even teaching courses about music production.
Posting Frequency: 5-7 posts per week, plus live streams or Q&A sessions with followers.
Engagement: With 20k+ followers, posts are now getting 50,000-200,000 views regularly.
### Income Potential at this Stage
Creator Fund: Earnings may rise to $300-$500 a month, depending on overall views.
Beat Sales: Selling 10-20 beats per month at $100 each could bring in $1,000-$2,000 per month.
Brand Deals and Sponsorships: Brands might offer $500-$2,000 per post depending on the agreement, leading to $1,000-$3,000 from brand collaborations.
Other Monetization: You could sell products like sample packs, courses, and membership subscriptions, generating another $500-$2,000 monthly.
Total Income: By this point, the producer could be consistently making $2,000-$5,000 per month or more from TikTok and associated revenue streams.

Key Takeaways
Growth Timeline: It takes time—around 6-12 months of consistent posting, engagement, and refinement to hit significant milestones.
Income Streams: Initially, TikTok will provide some money through the Creator Fund, but the bulk of revenue will come from beat sales, brand deals, and other product offerings.
Consistency & Engagement: The real secret is consistency, engagement, and building a community. TikTok rewards creators who show up regularly, engage with their followers, and produce high-quality content.
In this real-world scenario, after around 1 year, an indie producer could be making anywhere from $500-$2,000/monththrough a mix of
content, beat sales, and brand collaborations. This is just the beginning, and once the momentum builds, income potential could grow significantly as the brand becomes more established.
