# Games come in a wide variety of genres, each offering different experiences…

?

Games come in a wide variety of genres, each offering different experiences and appealing to different player preferences. Here are some of the most common types of games:

### 1. **Action Games**
- **Subgenres**: Platformers, shooters, fighting games, beat 'em ups.
- **Examples**: *Call of Duty*, *Super Mario*, *Street Fighter*.
- **Focus**: Fast-paced gameplay, reaction time, and hand-eye coordination.

### 2. **Adventure Games**
- **Subgenres**: Narrative-driven, exploration-based.
- **Examples**: *The Legend of Zelda*, *The Walking Dead*, *Life is Strange*.
- **Focus**: Storytelling, puzzle-solving, exploration.

### 3. **Role-Playing Games (RPGs)**
- **Subgenres**: Action RPGs, turn-based RPGs, MMORPGs.
- **Examples**: *Final Fantasy*, *The Witcher*, *World of Warcraft*.
- **Focus**: Character development, story, quests, and strategy.

### 4. **Simulation Games**
- **Subgenres**: Life simulation, construction & management, vehicle simulation.
- **Examples**: *The Sims*, *SimCity*, *Microsoft Flight Simulator*.
- **Focus**: Simulating real-world activities or systems.

### 5. **Strategy Games**
- **Subgenres**: Real-time strategy (RTS), turn-based strategy (TBS), tower defense.
- **Examples**: *StarCraft*, *Civilization*, *Age of Empires*.
- **Focus**: Planning, resource management, tactics.

### 6. **Sports Games**
- **Subgenres**: Traditional sports (football, basketball), extreme sports, racing.
- **Examples**: *FIFA*, *NBA 2K*, *Gran Turismo*.
- **Focus**: Simulating sports, competition.

### 7. **Puzzle Games**
- **Subgenres**: Match-three, logic puzzles, physics-based puzzles.
- **Examples**: *Tetris*, *Candy Crush*, *Portal*.
- **Focus**: Problem-solving, logic, pattern recognition.

### 8. **Horror Games**
- **Subgenres**: Survival horror, psychological horror, jump-scare games.
- **Examples**: *Resident Evil*, *Silent Hill*, *Amnesia*.
- **Focus**: Creating tension, fear, and survival challenges.

### 9. **Sandbox/Open-World Games**
- **Subgenres**: Creative sandbox, open-world RPG.
- **Examples**: *Minecraft*, *Grand Theft Auto V*, *The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim*.
- **Focus**: Freedom to explore and interact with the world, often with a focus on player creativity.

### 10. **Party Games**
- **Subgenres**: Mini-games, multiplayer challenges.
- **Examples**: *Mario Party*, *Jackbox Games*, *Among Us*.
- **Focus**: Multiplayer fun, casual play, social interaction.

### 11. **Battle Royale Games**
- **Examples**: *Fortnite*, *PUBG*, *Apex Legends*.
- **Focus**: Last-man-standing gameplay, large player counts, and shrinking play areas.

### 12. **Rhythm Games**
- **Examples**: *Guitar Hero*, *Dance Dance Revolution*, *Beat Saber*.
- **Focus**: Timing actions to music, rhythm-based gameplay.

### 13. **Virtual Reality (VR) Games**
- **Examples**: *Half-Life: Alyx*, *Beat Saber*, *Superhot VR*.
- **Focus**: Immersive experiences using VR headsets, often with interactive environments.

### Sandbox/Open-World Games with Paid Add-ons or Mods

1. **Minecraft**
- **Platform**: PC, Console, Mobile
- **Marketplace**: Minecraft Marketplace allows creators to sell skins, texture packs, and worlds for real-world money.
- **Monetization**: Players can buy and sell custom content using Minecraft Coins.

2. **Roblox**
- **Platform**: PC, Console, Mobile
- **Marketplace**: Roblox offers a platform where creators can develop games and sell virtual items, skins, and game passes.
- **Monetization**: Developers earn Robux, which can be exchanged for real money.

3. **Garry's Mod**
- **Platform**: PC
- **Marketplace**: The Steam Workshop allows creators to upload mods, maps, and tools. Some of these can be monetized if they meet Steam’s guidelines.
- **Monetization**: Creators can earn revenue from paid mods or items sold through the Steam Workshop.

4. **Grand Theft Auto V (GTA Online)**
- **Platform**: PC, Console
- **Marketplace**: Rockstar does not officially support direct monetization of mods, but third-party sites allow for the sale of custom scripts, maps, and models (though this is a gray area legally).
- **Monetization**: Some creators earn money through donations or by selling custom content outside official channels.

5. **The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim (Special Edition)**
- **Platform**: PC, Console
- **Marketplace**: Bethesda’s Creation Club allows for the sale of approved mods, which can include new weapons, armor, quests, and other content.
- **Monetization**: Mod creators can sell their content officially through the Creation Club, earning money directly.

### Battle Royale Games with Paid Add-ons or Mods

1. **Fortnite**
- **Platform**: PC, Console, Mobile
- **Marketplace**: Creators can earn money by designing and selling custom skins, emotes, and other cosmetic items through Epic’s Support-A-Creator program.
- **Monetization**: Players can purchase these items using V-Bucks, which translate to real money for creators.

2. **PUBG (PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds)**
- **Platform**: PC, Console, Mobile
- **Marketplace**: While PUBG doesn’t officially support modding, there’s a secondary market for cosmetic items, and some community creators earn through platforms like Steam.
- **Monetization**: Selling rare skins and crates can lead to significant real-world earnings.

3. **Apex Legends**
- **Platform**: PC, Console
- **Marketplace**: Similar to PUBG and Fortnite, Apex Legends allows for the creation and sale of custom skins and cosmetic items.
- **Monetization**: Creators can participate in revenue-sharing programs for in-game cosmetics.

4. **Rust**
- **Platform**: PC
- **Marketplace**: Rust offers support for community-created skins and items, which can be sold on the Steam marketplace.
- **Monetization**: Creators can earn money by selling their designs through Steam’s marketplace.

5. **ARK: Survival Evolved**
- **Platform**: PC, Console
- **Marketplace**: The game supports modding through the Steam Workshop, where creators can sell or offer mods for free.
- **Monetization**: Some mods and maps created by the community are paid, allowing creators to earn money through Steam.

Here are five sandbox/open-world games that allow players to submit or sell add-ons and mods for real-world money:

### 1. **Minecraft**
- **Marketplace**: Minecraft offers a Marketplace where players can sell skins, texture packs, and custom maps. This allows creators to earn real money from their designs.
- **Example Add-ons**: Skins, custom maps, mini-games, and more.

### 2. **Grand Theft Auto V (GTA V)**
- **FiveM**: Through FiveM, a popular modification framework for GTA V, players can create custom servers, mods, and even monetize them through Patreon or other platforms.
- **Example Add-ons**: Custom game modes, maps, vehicles, and server scripts.

### 3. **The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim**
- **Creation Club**: Bethesda’s Creation Club allows modders to create and sell mods for Skyrim (and Fallout 4) directly through an official platform.
- **Example Add-ons**: New quests, weapons, player homes, and gameplay mechanics.

### 4. **ARK: Survival Evolved**
- **Steam Workshop**: ARK supports modding through the Steam Workshop, and some modders have been able to monetize their work through sponsored mod programs or Patreon.
- **Example Add-ons**: New creatures, maps, and gameplay mechanics.

### 5. **Rust**
- **Community Marketplace**: Rust allows players to create and sell custom skins for weapons and items. The game also supports mods through Oxide, with some creators monetizing their mods through third-party platforms.
- **Example Add-ons**: Skins, custom servers, and gameplay modifications.
