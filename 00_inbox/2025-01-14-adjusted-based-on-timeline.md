# Adjusted Based on timeline

Here’s the **adjusted timeline** for your training plan, with more specific dates for your **half marathon** and **marathon** preparation, based on the goal of running your **marathon on October 26, 2026** and having roughly **1 year and 9 months** to prepare:

### ### **Key Dates**
- **October 26, 2026**: Marathon day
- **Half Marathon Goal**: You'll likely be ready for a half marathon around **April 2026** (about 6 months before your marathon).

### ### **Detailed Training Phases**

#### **Phase 1: Base Building (January – June 2025)**
**Goal**: Build endurance, comfortably run 5–6 miles during the week, and increase long runs to 10 miles.

### **Key Milestones**
- **End of March 2025**: You should be able to comfortably run 5–6 miles.
- **End of June 2025**: Your long run should be 10 miles, and you should be running around 5–6 miles on weekdays.

### **Weekly Structure**
- 3–4 runs per week.
- Start with 3-mile runs, gradually increasing long runs every 2 weeks.
- Cross-training 1–2 days a week.

#### **Phase 2: Endurance & Strength Building (July – December 2025)**
**Goal**: Increase long runs to 13–14 miles, start adding speedwork and tempo runs.

### **Key Milestones**
- **End of September 2025**: Your long run should reach 13 miles.
- **End of December 2025**: Peak long runs at 14 miles, with consistent 5–7 mile weekday runs.

### **Weekly Structure**
- 4–5 runs per week, with one long run (12–14 miles) on weekends.
- Tempo runs and interval training once a week.
- Mid-week runs of 5–7 miles for endurance.
- Recovery and easy runs on Wednesdays or Sundays.

#### **Phase 3: Marathon-Specific Training (January – June 2026)**
**Goal**: Transition to marathon-specific training. Begin adding marathon-pace runs, build long runs to 18–22 miles.

### **Key Milestones**
- **End of March 2026**: You’ll have reached your first half marathon distance (13.1 miles). This could be a **good goal race date** for you to gauge progress before your full marathon training ramps up.
- **End of June 2026**: Peak long runs should be at 20–22 miles.

### **Weekly Structure**
- 5–6 runs per week.
- One long run of 15–20 miles.
- Speed and hill work once or twice per week.
- Start incorporating race pace training during your long runs.

#### **Phase 4: Tapering & Race Prep (July – October 2026)**
**Goal**: Reduce mileage, focus on recovery, and ensure you're fully prepared for the marathon.

### **Key Milestones**
- **End of September 2026**: Begin tapering your weekly mileage.
- **Week of October 26, 2026**: Race week. Keep runs minimal and focus on rest.

### **Weekly Structure**
- Decrease weekly mileage by 20–30% each week.
- Maintain intensity but reduce volume (for example, short tempo runs and interval sessions).
- Focus on hydration, fueling, and mental preparation for the marathon.

### ### **Half Marathon Target**
Since you're aiming for a marathon on **October 26, 2026**, your best time for a **half marathon** will likely be **April 2026**. At this point, you will have built up enough mileage, endurance, and speed to perform well in a half marathon, and it will also give you a solid indication of where you are in terms of race-day pacing and fueling strategies for your full marathon in October 2026.

### ### **Additional Considerations**
- **Strength Training**: Continue to integrate 2 days per week of strength training throughout your plan to help with injury prevention and to support your running.
- **Cross-Training**: Incorporate 1–2 days per week of low-impact activities (cycling, swimming, etc.), especially in the base-building phase, to maintain aerobic fitness without overloading your running.
- **Recovery**: Plan for regular stretching, foam rolling, and rest days. Listen to your body, and don’t be afraid to adjust mileage if you feel fatigued or injured.
