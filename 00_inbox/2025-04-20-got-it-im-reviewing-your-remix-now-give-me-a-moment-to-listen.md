### **2. Mix & Arrangement Critique**

**Kick & Bass:**
- The kick has character but gets buried sometimes, especially when the sub is active.
- The bass is a little muddy. Feels like it's overlapping too much with the low mids and clouding the groove.
- **Fix**: Sidechain the kick slightly or use dynamic EQ to carve out 60–90 Hz when the kick hits.

**Drums (Percs, Snares, Hats):**
- The snare is nicely textured — raw but not too dry. Has that J Dilla crunch.
- The hi-hats could use **stereo spread** or **micro-automation** to add movement.
- Consider **low-passing your hats around 10–12kHz** to give a smoother, dub/lofi texture.

**Melodics:**
- There’s some really beautiful harmonic stuff going on, but they’re a bit **mid-heavy**.
- Try carving out 200–400Hz just a bit to clear room for the kick/bass.
- **Use stereo imaging**: push some melodic elements slightly wider or use Haas delay tricks.

**Arrangement:**
- You have that laid-back push/pull pocket that feels intentional and J Dilla-inspired. Love that.
- Consider **introducing more dub effects** like echo throws or dropouts at transitions.

#### **Reverb / Delay**
- **TAL Dub II or III** – Absolute gold for Jamaican-style delay throws.

#### **Modulation / Texture**
- **Izotope Vinyl** – For lofi wow/flutter, noise, warble.
- **Kilohearts Chorus/Phaser** – Add subtle movement to static melodies.

#### **Stereo / Imaging**
- **Ozone Imager** – Simple stereo field widening (be careful below 150Hz).
- **PanCake 2 by Cableguys** – Automate panning for hats/percs à la Monte Booker.
