# Absolutely! Trading with "fake money"—formally known as paper trading…

Absolutely! Trading with "fake money"—formally known as paper trading—is the smartest way to learn the ropes without the stress of losing your actual savings.
Most major platforms now offer high-quality simulators that use real-time market data, so the "feel" is exactly like the real thing. Here are the best free platforms to get started in 2026:
1. Best Overall for Beginners: Webull
Webull is widely considered the most user-friendly entry point. It’s a mobile-first platform, but their desktop app is also excellent.
- Virtual Cash: Unlimited.
- Assets: Stocks, ETFs, and Options.
- Why it’s great: It has a very "clean" interface. You can jump into their Paper Trading tab immediately after signing up, and they often host paper trading competitions where you can win real prizes.
2. Best for Advanced Charting: TradingView
If you want to focus on "technical analysis" (reading charts and patterns), TradingView is the industry standard.
- Virtual Cash: Customizable (you can set it to $100k, $1M, etc.).
- Assets: Stocks, Crypto, Forex, and Futures.
- Why it’s great: You don’t even need a brokerage account. Just open a chart, click the "Trading Panel" at the bottom, and select Paper Trading. It’s arguably the best visual experience for practicing strategies.
3. Most Realistic (Professional Grade): thinkorswim (Schwab)
This is the "pro" choice. It’s a bit more complex, but if you want to see what professional day traders use, this is it.
- Virtual Cash: $100,000.
- Assets: Almost everything (Stocks, Options, Futures, Forex).
- Why it’s great: Their paperMoney feature is a 1:1 replica of their live trading software. It’s perfect if you eventually want to trade options or complex strategies.
4. Best "Quick Start" (No Signup): Trade Nation
If you don't want to deal with emails or accounts just yet, Trade Nation offers a simulator that doesn't require a login.
- Why it’s great: It’s a "sandbox" style simulator designed specifically for learning the basic mechanics—like what "Buy" vs "Sell" actually does and how leverage works—without any friction.

A Quick Tip for Beginners
> Treat the "Fake Money" as Real: The biggest mistake people make is taking wild risks because it's not real cash. To actually learn, try to trade with a "fake" amount that matches what you actually plan to invest (e.g., if you have $1,000 saved, don't trade with $1,000,000 in the simulator).
