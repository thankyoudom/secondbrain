# Native American men, like many Indigenous peoples, have long embraced…

Native American men, like many Indigenous peoples, have long embraced natural remedies for health and grooming, particularly when it comes to hair and scalp care. While their practices may vary based on tribe and region, some general principles and natural treatments are common. If you're dealing with a yeast infection (Candida) on your scalp, there are some traditional and modern natural remedies that may help.
Natural Practices for Scalp Health
### Herbal Rinses
Native Americans often use herbal rinses to cleanse and nourish the scalp. Some herbs that are commonly used include:
Rosemary: Known for its antimicrobial properties, rosemary can promote circulation and help balance oil production on the scalp.
Sage: Another antimicrobial herb, sage is thought to reduce scalp inflammation and combat fungal infections.
Peppermint: Often used for its cooling, soothing properties, peppermint can help with scalp irritation and promote healthy hair growth.
How to use: You can brew a tea with dried herbs like rosemary, sage, or peppermint. Once cooled, use it as a final rinse after washing your hair.
### Tea Tree Oil
Tea tree oil is a well-known natural remedy for fungal infections. It's been used for centuries for its antiseptic and antifungal properties.
How to use: Add a few drops of tea tree oil to a carrier oil (like coconut or jojoba oil) and massage it gently into your scalp. Leave it on for about 20 minutes before washing out. You can also add a few drops of tea tree oil to your shampoo.
### Aloe Vera
Aloe vera has soothing properties and is often used to calm irritated skin, including the scalp. It can also help balance the scalp's pH, which may help prevent fungal overgrowth.
How to use: You can apply fresh aloe vera gel directly to your scalp. Let it sit for 20 minutes before washing it out with a gentle shampoo.
### Apple Cider Vinegar (ACV)
ACV is a popular remedy for various scalp issues, including dandruff and fungal infections, due to its antifungal and antibacterial properties. It also helps balance the scalp’s pH, which can prevent the overgrowth of yeast like Candida.
How to use: Dilute ACV with water (about 1:4 ratio) and use it as a rinse after shampooing. Let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out.
### Coconut Oil
Native Americans have long used coconut oil for its moisturizing and antimicrobial properties. It's great for soothing the scalp and combating fungal infections, as it contains lauric acid, which has antifungal qualities.
How to use: Apply coconut oil directly to your scalp and hair, leaving it on for at least 20-30 minutes before washing out. If you're dealing with a Candida infection, using coconut oil can be particularly helpful.
### Jojoba Oil
Jojoba oil is similar to the natural oils produced by our skin, making it an excellent moisturizer for both scalp and hair. It can help reduce dryness and flakes, and it also has antifungal properties that can help with yeast infections.
How to use: Massage jojoba oil into your scalp and leave it on for 20-30 minutes before washing it out. It's light enough that it won't leave a greasy residue but provides good moisture.
Dietary Adjustments for Candida Overgrowth
Candida thrives on sugar and refined carbohydrates, so adjusting your diet is often a key part of resolving fungal infections like scalp yeast infections. Some dietary changes that may help include:
Lower sugar intake: Avoid refined sugars, processed foods, and excess alcohol, which can feed Candida.
Increase probiotic foods: Foods like sauerkraut, kimchi, kefir, and yogurt (with live cultures) can help restore healthy gut flora and combat Candida overgrowth.
Coconut oil: Incorporating coconut oil into your diet is also beneficial, as it has antifungal properties that can help fight Candida both topically and internally.
Avoid Over-washing or Harsh Chemicals
Sometimes, scalp issues like fungal infections can be exacerbated by over-washing or using products with harsh chemicals (like sulfates). Gentle, natural shampoos are ideal, and it’s important not to strip the scalp of its natural oils too frequently. If you’re prone to infections, you might want to avoid heavy use of products with artificial fragrances, as they can irritate the scalp.

Things to Keep in Mind
Consult with a Healthcare Professional: If you suspect a yeast infection or Candida, especially if it persists or worsens, it’s always good to consult with a healthcare professional or dermatologist. Natural remedies can help, but they may not always resolve the underlying issue if it’s more severe.
Consistency is Key: Natural remedies often require consistent use over time, so patience is important in the healing process.
