# Great question—if you're just starting out and want solid analytics…

Great question—if you're just starting out and want solid analytics without diving into a paid plan right away, here’s how I’d rank them based on ease of use, usefulness of the free features, and overall value for beginners:

### **Best to Start With:**
**1. Buffer (Free Plan)**
- **Why:** Clean UI, very beginner-friendly, and gives you solid post-level analytics for Instagram, Facebook, and X (Twitter).
- **Best for:** Solo creators or small teams just starting with content planning and performance tracking.
- **Bonus:** You can also schedule posts, so it doubles as a content manager.

**2. Hootsuite (Free Tier)**
- **Why:** One of the most trusted platforms, even on the free plan. Lets you track engagement and manage up to 2 social accounts.
- **Best for:** Multi-platform users who want to see cross-platform performance at a glance.
- **Watch out for:** Limited features unless you upgrade.

**3. SumAll**
- **Why:** It connects social media + ecommerce, and the automated reports are simple and useful.
- **Best for:** Creators who also sell products and want to see how social activity ties into sales or traffic.

Once you're familiar with those and want more granular control or goal-tracking, then tools like **Quintly** or **Cyfe** are great next steps.

Do you mainly use Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, or all of the above? I can tailor the recommendation more precisely depending on your platforms.
