# That’s a beautiful aesthetic you're chasing — J Dilla’s swing and…

That’s a beautiful aesthetic you're chasing — J Dilla’s swing and grit, Ivy Lab’s futuristic textures, Erykah Badu’s soul-drenched warmth. You’re in a great lane.

### **Resampling: Sample Rate / Bit Depth Tips**
You’re already resampling your drum group at **16kHz / 16-bit**, which gives a nice **vintage, lo-fi edge** — very reminiscent of SP-1200 or older MPCs. Some tweaks you can experiment with:

- **12-bit / 22kHz**: Emulate the **SP-1200**. Brings a gritty, but not too muddy vibe.
- **8-bit / 11-16kHz**: Extreme lo-fi, nice for *ghost snares*, percussive fills, or weird ear candy. Avoid for main kicks unless you want a very degraded sound.
- **44.1kHz to 22kHz conversion**: Can introduce a subtle loss of top-end that sounds great on Rhodes, vocal chops, or percussion loops.
- **Non-standard rates (e.g., 13.5kHz)**: Gives weird, digital aliasing textures. Try on short percussive hits or chords.

Also try **bit crushing** *after* resampling to decouple bit depth from sample rate for more control.

### **Where Else to Resample**
Resampling isn’t just for drums — here’s where it can get juicy:

#### 1. **Chords / Sample Chops**
- Resample chord progressions through effects (chorus, phaser), then pitch down and resample again. Adds grit and texture.
- Time-stretch then resample back to tempo to emulate vinyl or cassette warping.

#### 2. **Vocal Layers**
- Resample adlibs or harmonies, layer underneath clean vocals for that **neo-soul haze**.
- Chop and re-pitch harmonies like pads.

#### 3. **FX / Atmospheres**
- Resample analog hiss, vinyl noise, reverb tails. Pitch + layer under the beat.
- Resample stretched-out field recordings and run through guitar amps or tape sims.

### **Less Obvious Ideas to Dive Deeper into the Sound**
Here’s where you can get more surgical and soulful:

#### 1. **Drum Swing & Microtiming (Dilla-ism)**
- Nudge snares and hats **slightly off-grid**, but keep kicks tight. Use delay/midi shift on individual hits to mimic human groove.
- Try **triplet swing (67%-75%)** on hi-hats and ghost notes.

#### 2. **Use Parallel Saturation**
- Set up parallel chains with tape saturation (e.g., Softube Tape, Decapitator), crush slightly, then blend.
- Ivy Lab often does this to thicken without muddiness.

#### 3. **Pitch Imperfections**
- Slight *random pitch LFOs* (0.1-0.3 semitones) on keys or vocals to mimic analog drift.
- Automate or modulate warble subtly with tape plugins (e.g., RC-20, SketchCassette).

#### 4. **Re-amp Digitally**
- Bounce your beat, then run it through amp sims or plugins like *Soundtoys Radiator*, *UAD Oxide*, or even Logic’s Pedalboard for non-traditional tones.
- Re-record the output with a mic + speaker setup (re-amping IRL) for depth.

#### 5. **Filtered Noise Layers**
- Add filtered pink noise synced with the kick or snare to mimic analog hiss or dusty vinyl feel.
- Duck it with sidechain for glue.

#### 6. **Polyrhythms / Ghost Percs**
- Create percussive polyrhythms under straight grooves — bounces like Ivy Lab or late Dilla.
- Low-volume ghost percussion, heavily swung, pitched oddly.
