# Getting your app listed on the Epic EHR (Electronic Health Record…

Getting your app listed on the Epic EHR (Electronic Health Record) App Store, also known as App Orchard, involves a few steps and requirements. Epic Systems is a major provider of EHR solutions, and they allow third-party applications to integrate with their system through their App Store. Here's a general breakdown of what’s required to get an app on the Epic EHR App Store:
1. Join the App Orchard Program
Sign up: The first step is to become a member of Epic’s App Orchard program. You’ll need to sign up and pay the membership fee, which grants you access to various resources and tools to develop, test, and integrate your app.
Membership tiers: There are different membership tiers based on your needs. The basic tier allows you to get started, while higher tiers may offer more advanced capabilities and support.
2. Understand Epic’s Standards & Requirements
FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): Epic uses FHIR standards for data exchange. Your app must support FHIR-based APIs for integration with Epic systems.
Security & Compliance: Your app must meet strict security and privacy standards to comply with regulations like HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act). This includes ensuring your app encrypts sensitive health data and follows guidelines for patient data privacy.
Certifications: Depending on the nature of your app, it may need to be certified for clinical use and tested for interoperability with Epic's platform.
3. Develop the Application
API Integration: Your app must integrate with Epic’s APIs. These are available once you join the App Orchard program. You'll need to ensure that your app communicates effectively with Epic’s system for patient data, schedules, orders, and more.
Epic’s App Framework: Epic provides documentation on how to build apps that can run within their ecosystem, including UI/UX guidelines and any specific requirements for how your app should behave within the Epic system.
4. Testing Your App
Sandbox Environment: Epic provides a sandbox environment where you can test your app's functionality before submission. This allows you to validate how your app will interact with Epic’s systems and data.
Security Testing: Epic requires rigorous security testing, especially around the handling of patient data. Ensure that your app meets these standards, including vulnerability scanning and other security assessments.
5. Submit the App for Review
App Submission: Once your app is developed, tested, and ready, you’ll need to submit it to Epic for review. The review process involves verifying that your app meets all technical, security, and compliance requirements.
Review Timeline: The review process can take several weeks, so be prepared for this step to take time. Epic’s team will test your app in various scenarios to ensure it meets their quality standards.
6. Maintain Compliance and Updates
Continuous Updates: Once your app is approved and listed on the App Store, you must keep it updated with regular patches, security fixes, and feature updates. Epic may require you to resubmit the app if significant changes are made.
Ongoing Compliance: You must continuously meet Epic’s compliance standards, especially with new versions of Epic’s software. Failure to comply could result in your app being delisted.
7. Commercialization and Marketing
Promoting Your App: Once your app is listed on the Epic EHR App Store, you’ll have the opportunity to market it to healthcare organizations that use Epic. However, Epic's App Store is relatively niche, and most users (healthcare providers) will come through Epic’s direct recommendations or via App Orchard.
Pricing and Licensing: Epic doesn’t handle the sales or pricing of third-party apps, so you’ll need to determine how to handle these aspects (e.g., subscription models, licensing, etc.).
### Key Things to Remember
HIPAA Compliance: Your app must adhere to strict security and privacy standards, including encryption and user access controls.
App Development Guidelines: Epic has strict guidelines for the user experience, data handling, and UI/UX within their system.
Integration and Interoperability: Your app needs to integrate with Epic’s existing workflows and data systems.
Ongoing Support: You must provide support for your app post-launch, including bug fixes, security patches, and addressing any issues raised by users or Epic.
