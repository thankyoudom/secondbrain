# fancy Linux

Ah, I see exactly what you’re asking! That “fancy ASCII Arch Linux logo” you often see is not part of the minimal CLI environment. It’s part of neofetch or the login message on full Arch setups. Here’s the breakdown:
Minimal Arch install (CLI) – after booting from the USB and chrooting or just logging in after installation:
You’ll usually see a simple prompt: root@archiso ~#
No ASCII logo by default.
Fancy ASCII Arch logo – what you’re referring to is typically generated by tools like:
neofetch ? Shows Arch logo + system info. Not installed by default.
screenfetch ? Another alternative to display system info + ASCII logo.
How the fancy logo appears in some setups:
On some Arch-based ISOs (like Manjaro or Arch-based live ISOs), the login prompt is customized in /etc/issue or through a motd (message of the day) script.
Minimal Arch leaves it blank; you can add it manually if you want.
? To get it on your minimal install:
sudo pacman -S neofetch
neofetch
That will print the Arch ASCII logo plus system info. You can even auto-run it at login by adding neofetch to your ~/.bash_profile or /etc/profile.
