# quranic

Got it! You’re looking for completely free programs, especially those that are structured and can help you advance in your Arabic skills—ideally with the goal of reaching C1 or C2 levels. Below are the best free, structured options that can help you start from scratch and progress steadily:

1. Madani Arabic (Completely Free)
Website: Madani Arabic
### Why It’s Great
Madani Arabic offers a structured path for learning Quranic Arabic from scratch. Their curriculum is designed for both beginners and more advanced learners. This site focuses specifically on Quranic Arabic, so it’s perfect if your main goal is to read and understand the Quran in its original language.
### What It Covers
Basic Arabic Grammar: Nouns, verbs, particles.
Pronunciation and Phonetics: Learn how to pronounce Arabic letters correctly.
Vocabulary: Quranic and general Arabic vocabulary.
Sentence Structure: Focus on syntax and understanding Quranic verses.
How to Start: Begin with their free lessons, which guide you through pronunciation, grammar, and essential vocabulary. You can also download the Madani Qaida (a foundational text used to teach Quranic Arabic).

2. Quranic (Free Version)
Website: Quranic
### Why It’s Great
The Quranic app offers a unique approach to learning Quranic Arabic by focusing on the 1,000 most-used words in the Quran. This method helps you learn the language progressively and efficiently. The free version provides access to some of the most important vocabulary, making it perfect for beginners.
### What It Covers
Quranic Vocabulary: Focuses on the most frequent words in the Quran.
Grammar: Offers basic grammar lessons focused on Quranic Arabic.
Reading Practice: Helps you practice reading Quranic verses.
How to Start: Download the app for free, and start with their foundational lessons to build your understanding of Quranic Arabic.

3. Arabic Online (Free Trial and Basic Resources)
Website: Arabic Online
### Why It’s Great
Arabic Online offers structured, university-style courses in Modern Standard Arabic (MSA). Although it’s primarily MSA, the site offers a clear learning progression, with levels ranging from beginner to advanced (up to C1).
### What It Covers
Grammar and Sentence Structure: Extensive lessons in grammar and structure.
Reading and Writing: Helps you build your skills through written exercises.
Vocabulary: Includes vocabulary that will help in formal and modern contexts.
How to Start: Use the free trial to begin with the basic lessons in MSA. As you progress, you can continue to explore further lessons.

4. Duolingo (Free and Structured Path)
Website: Duolingo Arabic
### Why It’s Great
Duolingo is well-known for its fun, gamified approach to language learning. The Arabic course here starts at an absolute beginner level and takes you up to an intermediate level (around B1). While it focuses on Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) and doesn’t cover Quranic Arabic directly, it’s a good foundation for beginners and can be useful for vocabulary and grammar.
### What It Covers
Vocabulary: You’ll learn a wide range of vocabulary, from basic phrases to more complex concepts.
Grammar: Basic Arabic grammar like noun-adjective agreement, plurals, verb conjugation, etc.
Pronunciation: Practice listening and speaking with native speaker pronunciation.
How to Start: Sign up for a free account and begin progressing through the levels. The structured lessons will take you from A1 to B1 (and potentially beyond).

5. Arabic For Nerds (Free Grammar Lessons)
Website: Arabic For Nerds
### Why It’s Great
This is a comprehensive resource for anyone serious about mastering Arabic grammar. It offers free, detailed explanations of Arabic grammar rules, which is essential for achieving fluency.
### What It Covers
Advanced Grammar: It provides free grammar lessons that will take you from intermediate to advanced levels.
Sentence Structure: Learn how to form complex sentences, understand syntax, and use different verb forms.
Verb Conjugation: Covers the more intricate aspects of verb conjugation.
How to Start: Start with the free articles on Arabic grammar. These are deeply detailed and will give you a solid foundation for tackling more complex Arabic.

6. Alhuda Online Quran Academy (Free Resources)
Website: Alhuda
### Why It’s Great
Alhuda is a Quranic educational platform that offers a wealth of free resources aimed at teaching Quranic Arabic. They also offer free PDFs, videos, and beginner lessons that focus on helping you understand the Quran in Arabic.
### What It Covers
Quranic Grammar: Deep dive into understanding Quranic grammar.
Tafseer (Exegesis): Learn the meanings of Quranic verses in context.
Vocabulary: Focuses on understanding the language of the Quran.
How to Start: Explore their free content, including PDFs and video lessons. They also offer structured, progressive learning paths.

7. Learn Arabic with Maha (YouTube Channel)
YouTube Channel: Learn Arabic with Maha
### Why It’s Great
Maha's YouTube channel offers a series of well-organized and free lessons that cover everything from the Arabic alphabet to advanced grammar. It’s structured enough for beginners but also provides material for intermediate and advanced learners.
### What It Covers
Arabic Alphabet: Start from scratch with the alphabet and pronunciation.
Grammar: Lessons in grammar and sentence structure.
Culture and Vocabulary: Learn Arabic in context with cultural lessons.
How to Start: Start with her beginner videos and work your way up. The lessons are short and easy to follow, making it a great resource for structured learning.

8. Tandem (Language Exchange App)
App Link: Tandem
### Why It’s Great
While not a traditional "course," Tandem connects you with native Arabic speakers for language exchange. It’s a great way to practice speaking, writing, and listening in a structured conversational way.
### What It Covers
Real-life Practice: Communicate with native speakers via text, voice, and video calls.
Pronunciation and Listening: Gain real-time feedback on pronunciation and grammar.
Cultural Immersion: Learn in the context of native speakers.
How to Start: Download the app, set your learning goals, and connect with native speakers for practice. It’s completely free, though you can also opt for premium features if needed.

### How to Combine These Resources
Start with Madani Arabic for foundational Quranic Arabic.
Use Duolingo for general vocabulary and grammar building (MSA).
Dive into Arabic for Nerds for in-depth grammar lessons.
Supplement with Tandem for conversational practice.
Quranic can be a great tool to start recognizing and learning high-frequency Quranic words.
By combining these resources, you'll have a structured and comprehensive path to reach an advanced level (C1/C2) in Arabic. It will take time, but these free resources are perfect to start building a strong foundation!
