# When Nikola Tesla said, "If you want to find the secrets of the universe…

When Nikola Tesla said, "If you want to find the secrets of the universe, think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration," he was speaking as a physicist and electrical engineer. He was fascinated by resonance—the idea that every physical object has a natural frequency at which it vibrates most efficiently.
While social media influencers often use this quote to describe "vibes" or manifesting, Tesla was referring to the literal, measurable physics of electromagnetic waves and mechanical oscillations. However, there is a bridge between his engineering principles and human biology.
1. What Tesla Meant vs. Modern "Vibes"
Tesla’s focus was on physical resonance. He believed that if you could match the frequency of a system (like the Earth’s ionosphere or a bridge), you could transfer energy with zero resistance.
In a biological sense, "raising your vibration" is more accurately described in science as Biological Coherence. This is when your heart, brain, and nervous system are in a state of rhythmic, efficient communication.

2. Science-Based Ways to "Raise" Your Biological Frequency
If we define "frequency" through the lens of neuroscience and physiology, there are four measurable ways to optimize your state:
A. Brainwave Regulation (Neuro-frequency)
Your brain operates at different frequencies measured in Hz. You can consciously shift these through specific actions:
- Gamma (30–100 Hz): Peak focus and "aha" moments. Induced by deep meditation and high-level problem-solving.
- Alpha (8–12 Hz): The "flow state." Induced by light relaxation, walking in nature, or creative play.
- Theta (4–8 Hz): Deep intuition and REM sleep. Often accessed during the "hypnagogic" state just before waking up.
B. Heart Rate Variability (HRV)
HRV is the measure of time variation between each heartbeat. A higher HRV indicates a "high-vibration" state of resilience.
- The Daily Habit: Coherent Breathing. Breathe in for 5 seconds and out for 5 seconds. This synchronizes your heart’s rhythm with your breath, signaling the nervous system to move from "survival" (low frequency/stress) to "thriving" (high frequency/rest).
C. Circadian Rhythm Alignment
Your body has a "master clock" (the SCN in the brain) that dictates the frequency of hormone release (cortisol and melatonin).
- The Daily Habit: Morning Sunlight. View natural light within 30 minutes of waking. This sets the "oscillation" of your hormones for the next 24 hours.
D. Mitochondrial Efficiency
Mitochondria are the "energy plants" of your cells. When they are efficient, your biological energy is high.
- The Daily Habit: Cold Exposure or Heat (Sauna). These "hormetic" stressors force mitochondria to become more robust, literally increasing the energy output (vibration) of your cells.
3. Major Things That "Lower" Your Frequency
In biological terms, "low frequency" corresponds to entropy—disorder and inflammation.
| Factor | Biological Impact |
|---|---|
| Chronic Stress | Keeps the body in Beta brainwaves (high-anxiety state), draining energy. |

4. Daily Habits for Compounding Gains
If you want to apply Tesla’s philosophy to your health, focus on rhythm and resonance:
- The "3-2-1" Rule: Stop eating 3 hours before bed, stop work 2 hours before bed, and turn off screens 1 hour before bed to protect your sleep frequency.
- Earthing/Grounding: While still debated in some circles, some studies suggest that physical contact with the Earth can help neutralize "bioelectric" stress by exchanging electrons.
- Vagal Toning: Hum, sing, or take a cold shower. These stimulate the Vagus nerve, which is the "master switch" for a high-coherence state.
Tesla was right: everything is frequency. By managing your light, breath, and thoughts, you aren't just "feeling better"—you are literally changing the electrical and chemical oscillations of your biology.

1. How Music "Tunes" Your Biology
Music isn't just entertainment; it’s a series of pressure waves that physically move the air and your eardrums.
A. The 432 Hz vs. 440 Hz Debate
- The Claim: 440 Hz (standard tuning) is "unnatural" or "aggressive," while 432 Hz is the "heartbeat of nature."
- The Science: While the mystical claims are often exaggerated, research does show that music tuned to 432 Hz correlates with a slightly lower heart rate and blood pressure compared to 440 Hz. It is perceived as "warmer" and more relaxing, likely because the lower pitch is less stimulating to the sympathetic nervous system.
- Action: If you are trying to relax, look for "432 Hz" versions of ambient or classical music.
B. Solfeggio Frequencies (e.g., 528 Hz)
- The Claim: 528 Hz can "repair DNA."
- The Science: There is no peer-reviewed evidence that sound waves repair DNA in a living human. However, a 2018 study found that 528 Hz music significantly reduced cortisol (stress hormone) and increased oxytocin. It’s not "magic," it’s just a frequency that the human ear/brain finds particularly soothing, triggering a deep relaxation response.
C. Binaural Beats (Brainwave Entrainment)
If you play two slightly different frequencies in each ear (e.g., 100 Hz in the left and 104 Hz in the right), your brain "perceives" the difference—4 Hz.
- For Sleep: Listen to Delta (0.5–4 Hz) or Theta (4–8 Hz) binaural beats. Your brainwaves will literally slow down to match the beat, making it easier to slip into deep sleep.
2. The "Frequency" Night Routine
Your night routine is about transitioning from the high-frequency "Beta" state of the day (busy, thinking) to the low-frequency "Delta" state of deep sleep.
The 10-3-2-1-0 Rule
This is the "gold standard" for protecting your biological rhythm:
- 10 Hours before bed: No more caffeine (it blocks adenosine, the "sleep frequency" signal).
- 3 Hours before bed: No more food or alcohol (digestion raises your core temperature and "vibrational" metabolic rate, keeping you awake).
- 2 Hours before bed: Stop working. Move from "output" to "input."
- 1 Hour before bed: Zero Blue Light. Blue light mimics the frequency of the sun, tricking your brain into thinking it’s noon and suppressing melatonin.
- 0: The number of times you hit the snooze button in the morning (which fragments your first "frequency" of the day).
Biological Coherence Habits
