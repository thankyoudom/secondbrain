# Love it. *The Artist's Way* is a brilliant path to creative recovery…

Love it. *The Artist's Way* is a brilliant path to creative recovery and expression. If you’re diving into it and want to go deeper—with less consumption and more *creation*—we can definitely shape a plan. Here's how we'll approach it:

---

### **Overall Weekly Structure (Artist’s Way Enhanced)**

- **Morning Pages** *(daily, non-negotiable)*: 3 pages of longhand, stream-of-consciousness writing.
- **Artist Date** *(1x per week)*: 1–2 solo hours doing something playful or curiosity-sparking.
- **Daily Creative Exercise**: Tailored prompts to build skill, taste, and courage without bingeing content.
- **Weekly Reflection**: Light journaling, mood check-in, and taste tracker.

---

## **Week 1 Plan: "Excavation + Permission to Play"**

### **Daily Exercises**
Each takes 15–45 minutes, max. You can scale up/down.

**Day 1 – Sensory Walk & Sketch**
- **Task**: Go for a walk. Choose one object/scene that grabs your senses. Sketch or describe it in vivid detail (words or image).
- **Stuff needed**: Sketchpad or notebook, pen/pencil.
- **Why**: Re-engages your raw attention and aesthetic intuition.

**Day 2 – “Bad Art” Session**
- **Task**: Make something deliberately “bad” (ugly poem, wonky doodle, off-key song).
- **Stuff**: Anything you like—crayons, camera, keyboard.
- **Why**: Loosens perfectionism, boosts creative play.

**Day 3 – Taste Builder (Creation-Not-Consumption)**
- **Task**: Think of one creator you admire. Instead of watching/reading them today, make something *inspired by* their *vibe* (not their content).
- **Stuff**: Journal/sketchpad.
- **Why**: Develops your creative *voice* via influence without mimicry.

**Day 4 – Creative Constraints Game**
- **Task**: Make a thing (poem, story, drawing) using strict limits (e.g., only circles, only 50 words, only three colors).
- **Why**: Constraints = creativity fuel.

**Day 5 – Creative Collage (Analog or Digital)**
- **Task**: Cut/paste words & images into a mini-vision board or vibe collage.
- **Stuff**: Magazines, glue, scissors *or* Canva/Pinterest.
- **Why**: Sparks subconscious taste + mood awareness.

**Day 6 – Altered Object**
- **Task**: Take a common object (e.g., spoon, book, receipt) and alter it creatively.
- **Why**: Shifts your perception of the everyday.

**Day 7 – Reflection + Taste Audit**
- **Task**: Journal:
- What did I *love* doing this week?
- What bored/drained me?
- What kind of art do I *want* to make more of?
- **Bonus**: Write a letter *from* your inner artist to yourself.

---

### **Taste-Building Without Over-Consuming**
### Instead of watching tons of content
- **Taste Notebook**: Keep a section in your journal where you jot *specifics* you admire (e.g., “I love how this painter uses white space”).
- **Micro Reviews**: After consuming something, write 2–3 lines of critique—what worked, what didn’t, what you’d try.
- **“Create Before Consume” Rule**: Don’t open any media until you’ve created *something*, even a 3-line poem.
