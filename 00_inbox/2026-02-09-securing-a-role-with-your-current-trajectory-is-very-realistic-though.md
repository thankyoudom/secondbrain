# Securing a role with your current trajectory is very realistic, though…

Securing a role with your current trajectory is very realistic, though "confidence" in this market usually comes from the intersection of your certs and your specific bootcamp experience.
The GCP Professional Security Engineer is a heavy-hitter. In 2026, with the rise of AI-driven threats, security has become the top hiring priority. While a CS degree helps with HR filters, your combo of experience + bootcamp + professional certs is a proven alternative path.

Is it "Enough" Without a CS Degree?
The short answer is: Yes, but your portfolio must "speak" code.
Without a CS degree, hiring managers look for "Evidence of Competence." Since you already have the Terraform Associate, you have a head start. In 2026, security is no longer just "checking boxes"; it’s Security-as-Code.
- The Power Move: Don't just get the DevOps cert next—integrate them. Build a project where you deploy a secure GKE cluster using Terraform, with workload identity and binary authorization enabled.
- The Experience Factor: Since you have a bootcamp background, emphasize your troubleshooting and build experience. Professional certs prove you know the what; your experience proves you know the how.
Strategic Roadmap
If you want to be "un-ignorable" in these markets, follow this sequence:
- Finish the Security Professional: This validates you for high-paying roles in regulated industries (Banking in FL/GA, Healthcare in NC).
- The "Bridge" Project: Before the DevOps cert, create a GitHub repo showing a CI/CD pipeline that includes security scanning (Snyk, Checkov, or GCP’s Cloud Build security tools).
- The DevOps Professional: This is your "closer." It proves you can automate the security you just learned.
