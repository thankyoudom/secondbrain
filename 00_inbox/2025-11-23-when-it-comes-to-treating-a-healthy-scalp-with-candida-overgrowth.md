# When it comes to treating a healthy scalp with candida overgrowth…

When it comes to treating a healthy scalp with candida overgrowth—especially for African American hair—it’s essential to balance maintaining scalp health, reducing fungal growth, and nourishing the hair. The goal is to create a routine that addresses the scalp's needs while also ensuring the hair is hydrated, nourished, and well-maintained.
Here's a step-by-step scalp treatment routine for candida that also considers African American hair texture and specific needs:

? 1. Scalp Cleansing (2–3 times a week)
### What to use
Anti-fungal shampoo (like tea tree oil, ketoconazole, or coal tar-based) to reduce candida and fungal buildup.
Gentle sulfate-free shampoo with added oils for hydration if you're doing it more frequently.
### How it works
Tea Tree Oil is antifungal and antimicrobial, and it helps balance scalp pH.
Apple Cider Vinegar (ACV): Diluted in water (1:1 ratio) can help with candida and maintain a balanced scalp. ACV has antifungal properties and can lower the pH of the scalp, making it less friendly to yeast.
Exfoliating Scalp Scrub: If you have buildup or flakes, use a scrub with gentle exfoliating agents like sugar or salt (do this 1–2 times a month).
Tip: Avoid harsh shampoos that dry out the scalp, as that can lead to even more irritation. Look for shampoos that moisturize while still being antifungal.

? 2. Scalp Moisturization & Hydration (Daily or as needed)
### What to use
Natural oils like argan oil, jojoba oil, or castor oil.
Aloe vera gel or coconut oil (if you don’t have an allergy to it).
Tea tree oil diluted in a carrier oil (to keep things antifungal).
### How it works
These oils help to seal in moisture, fight dryness, and create a barrier against fungal overgrowth.
Jojoba oil helps balance scalp oils and moisturizes.
Coconut oil has antifungal properties (contains lauric acid) and can help soothe the scalp. However, be cautious if you have a sensitivity to it.
Tip: Lightly massage oils into your scalp (don't overdo it). Over-oiling can attract dust and grime, leading to more irritation.

? 3. Scalp Treatments (Anti-fungal and Rebalancing) (1–2 times a week)
### What to use
Apple cider vinegar rinse: After shampooing, rinse your scalp with diluted ACV.
Essential oils: Tea tree oil (for antifungal), lavender oil (for calming), or rosemary oil (for hair growth) in a carrier oil.
Neem oil (has antifungal, antibacterial properties).
Aloe vera: Soothing and anti-inflammatory.
### How it works
Tea tree oil: In a carrier oil, massage into the scalp for its antifungal effects.
Aloe Vera: It’s gentle, soothes irritation, and balances pH, which is especially important if you’re prone to scalp imbalances from candida.
Tip: Do a patch test before using any new oil or treatment on your scalp to ensure you’re not allergic.

? 4. Scalp Massages (2–3 times a week)
### How it works
Massaging helps stimulate circulation and can help with the absorption of any topical treatments you’re using (like oils or antifungal creams).
Massage gently in circular motions to avoid irritation or hair breakage.
Tip: Use your fingertips (not nails) to avoid damaging the scalp or hair follicles.

? 5. Anti-fungal Creams or Topical Treatments (When necessary)
### What to use
Ketoconazole cream or shampoo: An effective antifungal treatment for persistent candida or fungal infections.
Clotrimazole (a topical antifungal) may also be an option.
Coconut oil with tea tree oil: Make your own antifungal cream by mixing a small amount of tea tree oil in coconut oil.
### How it works
Ketoconazole is a potent anti-fungal that can help balance out any fungal overgrowth on the scalp.
Tip: Be cautious about applying antifungal creams too frequently, as it could dry out the scalp.

?? 6. Hair Care Products for African American Hair
Since you’re dealing with both scalp health and hair texture, you also want to maintain hair hydration and strength.
### Shampoos & Conditioners
Use moisturizing shampoos and conditioners that support dry, curly hair.
Look for products free from sulfates, parabens, and silicones to avoid further irritation to the scalp.
Consider adding a leave-in conditioner with natural oils (like shea butter or argan oil) to lock in moisture after you cleanse.
### Deep Conditioning
Deep conditioning is important for keeping the hair moisturized and preventing breakage.
Use hydrating deep conditioners (once a week) that contain ingredients like shea butter, honey, or avocado oilto maintain softness and elasticity.

?? 7. Maintain Gut Health & Nutrition
Since candida is an overgrowth of yeast that thrives in certain environments, maintaining a balanced gut microbiome can also help improve scalp health.
Anti-candida diet: Reduce refined sugars and high-carb foods that can feed candida. Focus on whole foods, non-starchy vegetables, and lean proteins.
Consider adding probiotic-rich foods (like kefir, sauerkraut, or kimchi) to help promote a healthy balance of gut bacteria.

### ? What to Avoid
Harsh chemical treatments (like perms or relaxers) which can further irritate the scalp.
Heavy oils or products that leave a residue on the scalp, which can contribute to fungal growth.
Scratching: Avoid itching, as it can break the skin and lead to infection.

### ???? Routine Summary
Shampoo (anti-fungal) 2–3 times a week.
Scalp massage (with oils or treatments) 2–3 times a week.
Deep conditioning (for hair) once a week.
Moisturization (with oils) daily.
Fungal treatments as needed (like ketoconazole).
By following this routine, you'll be tackling both the candida overgrowth on your scalp and nourishing your hair in a way that supports the texture and health of African American hair.
