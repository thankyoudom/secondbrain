# Artistic Direction

Good choice. This is exactly where Stoicism shines: short anchors + disciplined reflection, not overthinking.
I’ll give you one core mantra, a few situational mantras, and then Stoic-style prompts you can actually use before or after posting.

1. Core Mantra (memorize this one)
“I am responsible for my intention, not for interpretation.”
### Say this when
You feel guilt after posting
You start imagining how content might affect others
You’re tempted to over-justify yourself
This is straight Epictetus.

2. Situational Mantras (rotate as needed)
When content feels “too negative” or “too materialistic”
“Representation is not endorsement.”
You are showing something that exists, not instructing anyone how to live.

When you worry about “worthless content”
“Craft is never worthless when done with care.”
Stoics respected craftsmanship as an expression of virtue (discipline, attention, restraint).

When you feel morally superior or morally anxious
“Purity is not my task; integrity is.”
This cuts through both self-righteousness and self-shame.

When you’re stuck deciding whether to post
“Act, then release.”
Post (or don’t), then drop attachment to outcome.

3. Stoic Prompts for Creative Decisions
Use these like a checklist or journal—don’t over-answer. One or two sentences is enough.

Before creating or posting
“What is my intention with this piece?”
(Not impact. Not reception. Just intention.)
“Is this aligned with who I am today, not who I fear becoming?”
This prevents both hypocrisy and paralysis.
“Am I avoiding this because of fear, or moving toward it because of ego?”
Either answer is useful. Neither requires punishment.

When guilt or doubt arises
“What part of this is actually under my control?”
Name it. Ignore the rest.
“Am I judging the content, or judging myself for wanting to share it?”
This usually reveals misplaced shame.

After posting (important for closure)
“Did I act with honesty and care?”
If yes ? stop reviewing the decision.
“Would I make the same choice again, knowing what I know now?”
If no ? adjust next time, without self-attack.

4. A Weekly Stoic Creative Reset (optional but powerful)
### Once a week, ask
“What kind of creator am I practicing being?”
### Not
successful
relevant
moral
popular
### But
attentive
disciplined
sincere
unafraid
That’s a Stoic metric.

5. One final grounding thought
Marcus Aurelius wrote Meditations never intending it for publication.
Yet it helped millions.
You cannot predict usefulness.
You can only practice virtue in expression.
“I create in good faith. The rest is not mine.”
