For best video quality on TikTok, here are a few tips to minimize compression and degradation:
### Resolution and Frame Rate
Shoot in 4K (if possible) to retain the highest quality, even if TikTok compresses it. 1080p can still look good but 4K will give you extra clarity and flexibility.
Use 30fps or 60fps, depending on the feel of the video. Higher frame rates can give a smoother look, especially for action shots.
### Bitrate and File Format
Use a high bitrate when exporting the video to keep details intact.
MP4 is ideal for TikTok with H.264 codec for optimal balance of quality and file size.
### Lighting and Exposure
Good lighting reduces noise and compression artifacts. Consider shooting in well-lit environments or use softboxes/LED lights.
Adjust your exposure settings to avoid underexposure or overexposure that might lead to more noticeable compression.
### Editing and Compression
When exporting, try to avoid excessive compression. Aim for a balance between high quality and manageable file size. Export at 1080p (or 4K) with a high bitrate.
If you’re using a third-party editor like CapCut or Adobe Premiere, ensure the export settings are optimized for web/video platforms.
### TikTok Upload Settings
Upload in portrait mode (9:16 aspect ratio) to maximize screen real estate.
Avoid uploading through TikTok’s mobile app editor if possible, as it might compress your video further. Use the native file upload instead.

Good question — audio is just as tricky as video on TikTok, because they always re-encode your audio. The goal is to give TikTok something that’s already clean, loud, and balanced without clipping (since their codec can exaggerate distortion).
### Here’s how I’d refine your workflow

?? Export from Your DAW
Peak level: -6 dB is a great target (keeps headroom for encoding).
Loudness (LUFS): Aim for -14 LUFS integrated (streaming standard). That way TikTok’s compression doesn’t squash it too hard.
File format: Export WAV (44.1 kHz, 24-bit). You can always convert later, but TikTok chews up MP3/ACC more.

?? Mastering
BandLab’s “Universal” mastering is okay, but I’d recommend doing this manually if you want consistency:
EQ ? cut mud (low-mids ~200-400 Hz), brighten highs if needed.
Compression ? gentle glue (2–3 dB gain reduction max).
Limiter ? ceiling at -1 dB True Peak (to avoid inter-sample clipping after TikTok recompression).
Target loudness ? -14 LUFS integrated / around -9 LUFS short-term for music/beat drops.

? Importing to CapCut
Never reduce volume in CapCut unless clipping ? putting it to 88% means your master is too hot to begin with.
Instead, master it to the right loudness upfront (-14 LUFS, -1 dB ceiling). Then leave CapCut at 100% volume.
CapCut exports at AAC (compressed) ? TikTok recompresses again. By starting clean and not attenuating in CapCut, you keep more clarity.

? Final Export (CapCut ? TikTok)
Export video with AAC audio, 320 kbps, 44.1 kHz if CapCut allows.
Don’t normalize or adjust gain on export — just keep it consistent.
